Assumptions & sources

Everything is calculated in today’s purchasing power with real returns of 3.0% / 5.0% / 6.5% p.a. (long-run nominal returns minus an assumed 2.5% inflation — applied once, never twice). Contributions stop at your retirement age. We never estimate Social Security for you: only the amount and start age you enter count, and they count as zero before that age — check yours at the SSA benefits estimator. Withdrawal is an illustrative rate you control. Educational tool, not financial advice.

Methodology

Three deterministic scenarios project your combined savings year by year at real (inflation-adjusted) returns, net of your fee input. The financial-independence year is the first year in which the illustrative withdrawal plus any benefit you entered covers your target income. Ranges are shown because the future is a corridor, not a line.

Worked example

Age 38, retiring at 67, $105,000 saved, $900/month, 0.6% fees, $4,500 target with a $1,500 benefit from 70: the base scenario reaches independence around 2058, with $654k–$1.34M at 67 across scenarios (base ~$976k).

Frequently asked questions

Why don’t you estimate my Social Security?

Because eligibility and amounts are personal. We link the official estimator and use only the figure you enter, from the start age you enter.

Is 4% a safe withdrawal rate?

It’s an illustration, not a guarantee — that’s why you can set it between 2.5% and 5% and see the sensitivity yourself.
