## Key Features for Australian Users

### Brand Voice for Australian English

The brand voice feature is the single most important capability for Australian finance teams and represents the clearest differentiation from general-purpose AI tools. Training Jasper on Australian English is a one-time setup process that takes approximately 30-60 minutes and involves uploading 10-20 examples of your existing content. From that point forward, every output will default to Australian spelling conventions, local terminology, and the specific tone you have established.

For financial services firms, this matters on multiple levels. Australian English has distinct spelling conventions from American English — organisation versus organization, colour versus color, behaviour versus behavior — and consistency in client-facing materials is a basic professionalism requirement. Beyond spelling, Australian finance content requires specific terminology that American AI models often mishandle: superannuation rather than retirement account, SMSF rather than self-directed IRA, concessional contributions rather than pre-tax contributions, and the ATO rather than the IRS. Training Jasper's brand voice on these distinctions eliminates a significant editing burden that otherwise falls on human reviewers.

<CollapsibleSection title="Brand Voice Training Checklist" count={8}>

- Upload 10-15 examples of your best existing Australian finance content (client newsletters, blog posts, educational guides)
- Include at least two EOFY communications with seasonal Australian tax messaging
- Add examples featuring superannuation terminology (concessional caps, bring-forward rules, pension phase)
- Specify Australian English as the default spelling convention in the brand voice settings
- Define your firm's specific tone: conservative and technical versus accessible and educational
- Include examples of how you reference regulatory bodies (ATO, ASIC, APRA) in context
- Add your standard compliance disclaimers and disclosure language as brand voice reference
- Review and iterate the brand voice training after the first 20 outputs for accuracy

</CollapsibleSection>

### Superannuation and SMSF Content

Jasper performs exceptionally well for superannuation education content once it has been trained on the Australian context. The platform's ability to hold and apply structured knowledge makes it well-suited to the rules-based nature of super legislation, where contribution caps, tax rates, and eligibility criteria need to be communicated accurately and clearly to clients across different life stages.

Australian financial advisers, accountants with SMSF clients, and super fund marketing teams will find that Jasper can draft high-quality educational content on topics including concessional and non-concessional contribution strategies, the bring-forward rule for non-concessional contributions, the transfer balance cap and its implications for retirement planning, pension phase minimum drawdown rates across different age bands, Division 293 tax for high-income earners, and the first home super saver scheme mechanics. The output quality is high as a starting point, but every piece of super content must be reviewed against current ATO and APRA guidance before publication because the legislative environment changes frequently and AI models do not update in real time.

<Warning>
**Superannuation Rule Verification:** Superannuation contribution caps, transfer balance caps, and tax rates are updated by the ATO annually and can also change through mid-year legislative amendments. Jasper's training data has a knowledge cutoff and may not reflect the most recent changes. Always cross-reference AI-generated super content against the current ATO website or your compliance team's guidance before client distribution.
</Warning>

## Privacy Act and ASIC Compliance Considerations

### Data Privacy Under the Privacy Act 1988

Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) apply to all entities handling personal information as part of their business operations, and the choice of AI tools used in content workflows can fall within this framework depending on what data is being processed. Jasper's enterprise security posture is strong by the standards of Australian privacy compliance: the platform holds SOC 2 Type II certification (audited annually by an independent third-party auditor), uses AES-256 encryption for data at rest and TLS for data in transit, and does not use customer inputs to train its underlying AI models.

| Privacy Requirement | Jasper's Position |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| SOC 2 Type II certification | Certified — annual third-party audit |
| Data encryption at rest | AES-256 |
| Data encryption in transit | TLS 1.2+ |
| Data residency | US servers (permitted under APPs for most content workflows) |
| Customer data used for model training | No — contractually excluded |
| Data retention controls | Configurable per enterprise plan |
| Deletion requests | Supported with documented SLA |

The critical best practice for Australian finance firms is to maintain a clean separation between Jasper's content drafting use case and any client-specific data. Jasper should be used to draft general educational content, client newsletter templates, blog posts, and marketing materials — not to process documents containing client tax file numbers, account details, portfolio valuations, or other personally identifiable information. Using any cloud-based AI tool to process actual client PII without appropriate data processing agreements and privacy impact assessments creates regulatory exposure under the Privacy Act.

<Warning>
**Never Input Client PII:** Australian Privacy Principles create specific obligations around how personal information is collected, used, and disclosed. Do not input client tax file numbers (TFNs), date of birth, account numbers, portfolio details, or any other personally identifiable information into Jasper or any other cloud-based AI tool without formal data processing agreements and a privacy impact assessment reviewed by your legal team.
</Warning>

### ASIC Content Marketing Compliance for AFSL Holders

Australian financial services licensees (AFSL holders) operate under strict content marketing obligations that require all client-facing communications to be clear, concise, and not misleading. The key ASIC regulatory framework relevant to AI-generated financial content includes the general advice and personal advice distinction under the Corporations Act 2001, the product disclosure statement (PDS) and target market determination (TMD) requirements, the financial services guide (FSG) obligations, and ASIC's Regulatory Guide 234 on advertising financial products and services.

Jasper can legitimately produce content in the following categories without triggering personal advice obligations, provided the content is positioned as general information only and includes appropriate disclaimers:

<CollapsibleSection title="Content Safe for AI Drafting (With Human Review)" count={8}>

- General financial literacy and education articles explaining concepts like compound interest, diversification, or tax deductions
- Market commentary describing what has happened in markets, without specific investment recommendations
- Explainer content on superannuation rules, government schemes, and regulatory changes
- Process guides explaining how to do things such as setting up a self-managed super fund or filing a tax return
- Product category overviews comparing features of different product types without recommending a specific product to a specific person
- FAQ content answering common questions about financial topics at a general level
- Newsletter content summarising industry news and regulatory developments
- Educational blog posts and guides targeted at general audiences without referencing individual circumstances

</CollapsibleSection>

Content categories that require mandatory compliance review before any AI tool is used to draft them include personal advice tailored to an individual's financial situation, goals, and needs; specific product recommendations; performance projections or return forecasts; financial services guide content; and any communication that could be construed as a recommendation to buy, hold, or sell a specific financial product.

<Warning>
**AFSL Compliance Reminder:** AI-generated content does not meet the compliance obligations of a qualified financial adviser. If your firm holds an AFSL, every piece of client-facing content — regardless of whether it was drafted by AI or humans — must be reviewed by your compliance team before distribution. ASIC does not accept "the AI wrote it" as a defence for misleading or deceptive conduct under the Australian Consumer Law or the Corporations Act.
</Warning>

<Tip>
**Build a Compliance Review Workflow:** Configure Jasper's approval workflow so that all content drafted for client distribution routes to your compliance officer before publishing. Add a standard review checklist that includes verifying: (1) no personal advice triggers, (2) all regulatory facts verified against current ATO/ASIC guidance, (3) required disclaimers present, and (4) AFG/AFSL number and FSG reference included where required.
</Tip>
