Physical bullion provides the purest form of ownership with zero counterparty risk, but carries storage costs and wider bid/ask spreads. ## 1. ASX Gold ETFs — Best for Cost-Efficient Long-Term Exposure

ASX-listed gold ETFs represent the most accessible and cost-efficient way for Australian investors to gain gold exposure. These exchange-traded funds hold physical gold in secure vaults, and you buy and sell units on the ASX through any standard brokerage account — the same way you would trade BHP or Commonwealth Bank shares. For long-term investors, the combination of low management fees, high liquidity, and minimal operational complexity makes ETFs the default starting point. The three primary ASX gold ETFs differ in management fees, vault location, and government backing — distinctions that matter more than most investors realise over a 10-year holding period.

### Perth Mint Physical Gold ETF (ASX: PMGOLD)

PMGOLD is backed by the [Government of Western Australia](https://www.wa.gov.au/) and is the most cost-efficient way to gain allocated physical gold exposure on the ASX. Each PMGOLD unit represents 1/100 of a troy ounce of gold physically stored at the [Perth Mint](https://www.perthmint.com/) — a facility with over 125 years of operating history and government backing. The management expense ratio of 0.15% p.a. is the lowest of any Australian gold ETF, meaning you pay just A$15 per year on a A$10,000 investment. Gold is physically held in Perth Mint vaults with government guarantee, and units can be redeemed for physical gold delivery when minimum thresholds are met. ASX liquidity is significant with daily volume and spreads typically ranging from 0.05-0.1%. Capital gains tax applies on disposal, with the asset held in a taxable Australian entity.

PMGOLD is the default recommendation for most long-term Australian investors seeking gold exposure in superannuation or a standard brokerage account, based on its government-backed security and lowest-in-class management fee.

### VanEck Gold Bullion ETF (ASX: NUGG)

NUGG is a newer ASX-listed gold ETF backed by physical gold stored at The Royal Mint in the United Kingdom. Launched in 2023, it offers a management expense ratio of 0.25% p.a. with gold storage in London rather than Australia. An AUD-hedged option is available under the ticker NUGGAU for investors who want to eliminate currency exposure between the Australian dollar and the US dollar. For Australian investors who prefer domestically-held gold, PMGOLD remains more appropriate due to its lower fee and government backing. NUGG suits investors comfortable with UK vault storage at a marginally lower fee than most peers, and the AUD-hedged variant adds a useful tool for investors concerned about AUD/USD volatility diluting their gold returns.

### Global X Physical Gold (ASX: GOLD)

GOLD is Australia's largest gold ETF by assets under management, backed by physical gold held in the HSBC vaults in London. Its management expense ratio is 0.40% p.a. — significantly higher than PMGOLD — but its larger size provides tighter bid/ask spreads on the ASX, making it marginally more cost-efficient for large single trades despite the higher annual fee. There is no AUD hedging, so investors carry full AUD/USD foreign exchange exposure. Long-term holders are generally better served by PMGOLD's lower annual fee, but GOLD's superior liquidity and tighter spreads make it the better choice for investors executing large block trades or requiring immediate fills. Over a 10-year horizon on a A$100,000 position, the 0.25% annual fee difference between GOLD and PMGOLD compounds to approximately A$2,800 in additional costs — a material consideration for buy-and-hold investors.

## 2. Digital Allocated Gold — Best for Fractional Physical Ownership

Digital allocated gold platforms occupy the middle ground between ETFs and physical bullion. You own real, allocated physical gold stored in secure Australian vaults, but you buy and sell it digitally — often from as little as A$50 — without needing to arrange physical delivery, storage, or insurance yourself. The two leading platforms in Australia are Perth Mint's online digital gold service and Ainslie Wealth, the digital arm of Australia's oldest private bullion dealer. Both offer allocated gold, meaning your specific gold is held separately from the company's assets and is legally yours, not a claim against the company's balance sheet.

### Perth Mint Gold (PMG) — Digital Platform

The [Perth Mint's online digital gold platform](https://www.perthmint.com/invest/online-trading/) allows fractional purchase of gold from A$50, backed by physical gold in Perth Mint's government vaults. PMG is available via the Perth Mint website and is separate from the ASX-listed PMGOLD ETF. The storage fee is 0.15% p.a. — identical to the PMGOLD MER — and gold is allocated to the individual account holder. Like PMGOLD, it is backed by the Government of Western Australia and is convertible to physical delivery when minimum weights are met. For investors who want physical allocated gold with the security of government backing — and do not want to invest via the ASX — PMG is the most cost-efficient digital gold product in Australia.

### Ainslie Wealth

[Ainslie Wealth](/au/gold-investing/ainslie-bullion-review) is the digital gold platform from Ainslie Bullion, Australia's oldest private bullion dealer established in 1974. Gold is stored in Ainslie's Brisbane vault and fully allocated to each account. The storage fee is 0.5% p.a. with a minimum purchase of A$50. The platform also offers digital silver, has a mobile app available on iOS and Android, and provides in-person collection options at Brisbane, Sydney, and Melbourne showrooms. Ainslie Wealth suits investors who value Australian private vault storage, physical collection options, and the backing of a 50-year operating history. While the 0.5% annual storage fee is higher than Perth Mint's 0.15%, the private vault infrastructure and physical showroom access provide a level of personal service that government-backed alternatives do not offer.

## 3. Physical Bullion — Best for Direct Ownership

Physical gold ownership remains the purest form of gold investment, offering zero counterparty risk and tangible possession. Australian investors can purchase gold bars and coins through [Ainslie Bullion](/au/gold-investing/ainslie-bullion-review) (Brisbane, Sydney, and Melbourne showrooms), [Perth Mint](https://www.perthmint.com/) (Perth showroom and online), or online dealers with delivery to any Australian address. The appeal of physical gold is straightforward — you own it outright, it sits in your possession or a vault you control, and there is no platform, company, or counterparty between you and your gold. For [SMSF](/au/superannuation/) trustees, physical gold is a permitted investment provided storage meets [ATO](https://www.ato.gov.au/) requirements including commercial storage with proper custody documentation, separate from personal assets.

The cost structure for physical gold is more complex than ETFs or digital platforms. Premiums over the spot price typically range from 1.5-6% depending on the product, with cast bars at the lowest premium and collector coins at the highest. Investment-grade gold of 99.5% purity or higher is exempt from GST in Australia, which keeps the upfront cost manageable for serious allocations. Storage options include home storage (with associated theft and loss risk), bank safe deposit boxes, or professional vault storage at approximately 0.4-0.6% p.a. Insurance is required for meaningful holdings, with specialist precious metals insurance available through several Australian providers.

The key limitation of physical gold is that it generates no yield and carries ongoing storage and insurance costs. Bid/ask spreads when buying and selling physical gold are typically wider than ETF or digital gold alternatives, making it less suited to investors who may need to liquidate quickly. Physical gold makes the most sense for direct ownership requirements, absence of counterparty risk, and physical possession for portfolio diversification.

## 4. Gold Mining Stocks (ASX) — Leveraged Gold Exposure

Australian-listed gold mining companies offer leveraged exposure to the gold price — their profitability and share price generally move more than the gold price itself, both up and down. When gold rises 10%, a well-run miner with fixed costs might see profits increase by 20-30%, driving a disproportionate share price gain. The reverse is equally true in falling gold environments, making mining stocks substantially more volatile than gold itself. For investors who want equity-style returns with gold price sensitivity, ASX-listed miners provide this exposure within a standard brokerage or [trading account](/au/trading/).

Major ASX gold miners in 2026 include Newmont Corporation (ASX: NEM), the world's largest gold miner by production which is dual-listed on the NYSE and ASX. Northern Star Resources (ASX: NST) is Australia's largest pure-play gold miner with operations in Western Australia and Alaska. Evolution Mining (ASX: EVN) operates as a mid-tier Australian gold miner with assets in NSW and QLD. Regis Resources (ASX: RRL) is a WA-focused gold producer, and De Grey Mining (ASX: DEG) is a major WA development-stage miner centred on the Hemi deposit. Each of these companies carries operational, management, and geopolitical risks that physical gold does not. A miner can underperform even in a rising gold price environment due to cost overruns, production problems, or hedging decisions. Gold mining stocks are equity investments, not gold price proxies, and should be evaluated as such.

## 5. Gold CFDs — For Traders, Not Investors

Gold CFDs are offered by [ASIC](https://asic.gov.au/)-regulated brokers including [IC Markets](/au/forex/ic-markets-review), [Pepperstone](/au/forex/pepperstone-review), and [CMC Markets](/au/trading/cmc-markets-review). XAU/USD (gold in US dollars) is one of the most actively traded CFD instruments in Australia, providing synthetic price exposure without any physical gold ownership. Leverage is available up to 20:1 under [ASIC limits](https://asic.gov.au/regulatory-resources/find-a-document/regulatory-guides/rg-227-over-the-counter-contracts-for-difference/) for retail clients, meaning a A$1,000 margin deposit controls a A$20,000 gold position — amplifying both gains and losses proportionally. Overnight financing costs apply for held positions, and spreads on gold CFDs from major brokers typically range from 0.0 to 0.09 pips.

Gold CFDs are suitable for short-term trading, hedging existing gold exposure, and tactical portfolio allocation. They are explicitly not suitable for long-term gold investment because overnight financing costs erode returns over time, making a position progressively more expensive to maintain. An investor holding a leveraged gold CFD position for 12 months would pay substantially more in financing costs than the annual MER on any gold ETF. For long-term investors, ETFs, digital gold, or physical bullion are categorically better options.

## Gold and Tax in Australia

Understanding the tax treatment of gold investments is critical for maximising after-tax returns, and the rules differ depending on the type of gold exposure you hold. The [Australian Taxation Office](https://www.ato.gov.au/) treats gold investments as capital assets, meaning disposal triggers capital gains tax obligations. A 50% CGT discount applies for assets held for more than 12 months by individuals, which significantly favours long-term gold allocation strategies over short-term trading. For investors managing broader [personal finance](/au/personal-finance/) portfolios, the CGT discount makes gold one of the more tax-efficient alternative asset classes for patient holders.

Investment-grade gold of 99.5% purity or higher is GST-free in Australia, keeping the upfront acquisition cost manageable for physical bullion purchases. Lower purity products, collector coins, and jewellery may attract 10% GST. Gold generates no income — unlike dividend-paying equities or interest-bearing deposits — which means there is no ongoing income tax liability during the holding period. Gold mining stocks do pay dividends, which are taxed as ordinary income (with franking credits where applicable). SMSFs can hold physical gold as a permitted investment provided it is stored commercially and meets [ATO standards](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/), and PMGOLD and other gold ETFs can be held through standard SMSF brokerage accounts.

Consult a registered tax adviser for your specific circumstances before making gold investment decisions. The interaction between CGT, the 50% discount, SMSF rules, and your marginal tax rate makes personalised advice essential for optimising after-tax gold returns.
