## True ECN Pricing Explained

### How IC Markets' ECN Model Works

IC Markets operates a genuine ECN (Electronic Communication Network) execution model. This means:

1. **No dealing desk** — IC Markets does not take the opposite side of your trades
2. **Direct liquidity** — Orders are matched with prices from 25+ liquidity providers
3. **Raw spreads** — You see actual interbank bid/ask prices from 0.0 pips
4. **Transparent commission** — A$7 per lot round-turn, clearly separated from the spread
5. **No requotes** — Market orders are filled at the best available price

### IC Markets Liquidity Providers

| Provider Type | Examples |
|---------------|---------|
| **Tier 1 Banks** | JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, Citibank, HSBC |
| **Prime Brokers** | Morgan Stanley, Deutsche Bank |
| **Non-Bank LPs** | Citadel Securities, XTX Markets |
| **ECN Aggregators** | Integral, Currenex |

### ECN vs Market Maker — Why It Matters

| Factor | IC Markets (ECN) | Market Maker |
|--------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Conflict of interest** | None — broker profits from commission | Yes — broker profits from your losses |
| **Spread source** | Interbank liquidity providers | Set by broker |
| **Requotes** | None | Common during volatility |
| **Order execution** | Straight-through processing | Dealer intervention possible |
| **Spread during news** | Widens naturally with liquidity | May widen significantly |
| **Scalping allowed** | Yes, unrestricted | Often restricted |

<Info>
**Why ECN matters for Australian traders:** ASIC's 30:1 leverage cap means every pip of spread cost has a greater impact on your returns. ECN pricing from 0.0 pips with a known commission lets you calculate exact trading costs before entering a position, eliminating spread markup uncertainty.
</Info>
