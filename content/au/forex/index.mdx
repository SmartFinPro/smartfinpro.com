### ASIC's Product Intervention Powers

Since October 2021, ASIC has exercised product intervention powers that impose strict conditions on all retail CFD and forex trading in Australia. These rules are mandatory for every ASIC-regulated broker and cannot be waived for retail clients.

### ASIC Leverage Restrictions (Retail Clients)

| Asset Class | Maximum Leverage | Margin Required |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Major currency pairs** (AUD/USD, EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY) | 30:1 | 3.33% |
| **Minor currency pairs** (AUD/NZD, GBP/AUD, EUR/GBP) | 20:1 | 5.00% |
| **Exotic currency pairs** (USD/TRY, USD/ZAR, AUD/SGD) | 15:1 | 6.67% |
| **Commodities** (gold, oil, silver) | 10:1 | 10.00% |
| **Equity indices** (ASX 200, S&P 500, FTSE 100) | 10:1 | 10.00% |
| **Individual equity CFDs** | 5:1 | 20.00% |
| **Cryptocurrency CFDs** | 2:1 | 50.00% |

### Mandatory Retail Protections

All ASIC-regulated forex brokers must provide the following protections to retail clients:

| Protection | Details |
|------------|---------|
| **Negative balance protection** | Mandatory since 2021. Your losses cannot exceed your account balance --- you will never owe more than you deposited |
| **Client fund segregation** | All client money must be held in segregated trust accounts at authorised Australian deposit-taking institutions (ADIs) |
| **Standardised risk warnings** | ASIC requires brokers to display the percentage of retail accounts that lose money (typically 67-80%) |
| **No incentives to trade** | Brokers cannot offer bonuses, rebates, gifts, or promotional benefits that incentivise retail clients to trade CFDs |
| **Real-time margin closeout** | Positions must be automatically closed when account equity falls to 50% of required margin |

<Info>
**Professional client classification:** If you meet ASIC's criteria for a wholesale or professional client (generally A$2.5M+ in net assets or A$250K+ gross income for the past two financial years), you may apply for higher leverage. However, this waives mandatory negative balance protection, standardised risk warnings, and real-time margin closeout protections. We recommend most traders maintain retail classification.
</Info>

### Retail vs. Professional Client Classification

| Factor | Retail Client | Professional/Wholesale Client |
|--------|--------------|------------------------------|
| **Leverage** | Capped at 30:1 (majors) | Negotiable (up to 500:1) |
| **Negative balance protection** | Mandatory | Waived |
| **Risk warnings** | Required on all materials | Not required |
| **Margin closeout** | Automatic at 50% margin | Negotiable |
| **AFCA access** | Available | May be limited |
| **Eligibility** | All retail traders | A$2.5M+ net assets or A$250K+ income for 2 years |

  icon="regulation"

The **Australian Financial Complaints Authority (AFCA)** is the external dispute resolution scheme for financial services complaints in Australia. All ASIC-regulated brokers must be AFCA members.

### How the AFCA Process Works

1. **Contact your broker first** --- ASIC requires all regulated firms to have an internal dispute resolution (IDR) process. Lodge your complaint directly with the broker.
2. **Wait for IDR response** --- the broker has 30 days (or 45 days for complex complaints) to respond.
3. **Lodge with AFCA** --- if unresolved or you are unsatisfied, lodge a free complaint at [afca.org.au](https://www.afca.org.au).
4. **AFCA investigation** --- AFCA reviews evidence from both parties and may attempt conciliation.
5. **Binding determination** --- AFCA issues a decision that is binding on the broker but not on you. You retain the right to pursue the matter through the courts.

**AFCA coverage limits:**
- Financial loss claims: up to **A$1,085,000**
- Other claims (e.g., service quality): up to **A$5,425,000**

**All five brokers in our ranking are AFCA members.**

<Tip>
**Keep records of all communications** with your broker --- including emails, chat transcripts, trade confirmations, and platform screenshots. AFCA relies on documented evidence when adjudicating complaints. Well-documented cases are resolved faster and more favourably.
</Tip>

### Capital Gains Tax (CGT) and Forex Trading

Forex trading profits in Australia are generally assessable under the **Capital Gains Tax (CGT)** regime or as **ordinary income**, depending on how the ATO classifies your activity:

| Factor | CGT Treatment (Investor) | Ordinary Income Treatment (Trader) |
|--------|--------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Tax rate** | Marginal rate on net capital gain (50% CGT discount available for positions held 12+ months) | 100% taxable at your marginal tax rate |
| **Trading frequency** | Occasional, infrequent trades | Regular, systematic trading activity |
| **Holding period** | Longer-term positions | Short-term, intraday or high-frequency |
| **Intent** | Investment or speculation | Business-like operation with profit motive |
| **Loss treatment** | Capital losses offset capital gains only (carry forward indefinitely) | Trading losses deductible against all income |

### Key ATO Considerations

- **Forex on capital account:** If you trade occasionally and hold positions for days or weeks, the ATO will likely treat gains as capital gains. You pay CGT at your marginal rate, with a 50% discount for positions held over 12 months.
- **Forex as a business:** If trading is your primary activity, you trade daily, and you operate in a business-like manner, the ATO may classify you as carrying on a business. Profits are assessable as ordinary income at your full marginal rate, but you can deduct trading-related expenses (software, data feeds, home office) against all income.
- **Section 775 rules:** The ATO's foreign currency gain/loss rules under Division 775 of the *Income Tax Assessment Act 1997* may apply to forex gains that arise from conversion of foreign currency amounts. These rules are complex and may interact with CGT rules.
- **Record keeping:** The ATO requires you to maintain records of all trades for at least five years. All five brokers in our ranking provide downloadable trade histories and annual statements.

<Warning>
**The ATO has increased scrutiny of forex and CFD trading.** Data-matching programs now cross-reference broker-reported transaction data with individual tax returns. Ensure you report all forex profits accurately. Consult a qualified Australian tax professional (CPA or Chartered Accountant) for advice specific to your trading activity and volume.
</Warning>
