### Client Protection Under ASIC

Australian retail clients trading with eToro receive several mandatory protections that offshore unregulated brokers do not provide. Client funds are held in segregated accounts, completely separate from eToro's operational funds, ensuring your capital cannot be used for corporate expenses. ASIC's Product Intervention Order caps retail leverage at 30:1 on major forex pairs, 20:1 on minors, and 15:1 on exotics — reducing the risk of catastrophic losses. ## Copy Trading Explained

### How CopyTrader Works

CopyTrader is eToro's flagship feature and the primary reason most Australian beginners choose the platform. It allows you to automatically replicate the trades of experienced Popular Investors in real time. When a copied trader opens or closes a position, the same action is proportionally mirrored in your account based on your allocated capital. You maintain full control — you can pause, modify, or stop copying at any time, and you can set a Copy Stop Loss to limit maximum drawdown per copied trader.

The process is straightforward: browse Popular Investors filtered by performance, risk score, asset focus, and region. Allocate a minimum of US$200 per copied trader. Every subsequent trade is automatically replicated in your account proportional to your allocation. The service is free to use — eToro does not charge additional fees for copy trading beyond the standard spread.

### Selecting Popular Investors

<CollapsibleSection title="What to Look For in a Popular Investor" count={7}>

- **Track record** — Minimum 12 months of verified performance history
- **Risk score** — Target 3-6 on the 1-10 scale for moderate, sustainable risk
- **Maximum drawdown** — Historical peak-to-trough decline under 20% is ideal
- **Monthly consistency** — Even distribution of returns rather than extreme swings
- **Copier count** — Social proof, though not a performance guarantee
- **Asset focus** — Confirm they trade forex specifically, not just stocks or crypto
- **Region** — Consider Australian-based traders for AUD-focused strategies

</CollapsibleSection>

Beyond individual CopyTrader, eToro offers Smart Portfolios — curated, diversified portfolios managed by eToro's investment team. The CurrencyTrader portfolio focuses on forex-related strategies with a minimum investment of US$500 and automatic periodic rebalancing with full transparency of holdings.

<Warning>
**Past performance is not a guarantee of future results.** CopyTrader makes it easy to follow successful traders, but markets change and strategies fail. Never allocate more than 20% of your total capital to a single copied trader, and always set a Copy Stop Loss. Monitor copied traders monthly and remove underperformers promptly.
</Warning>

Execution quality was solid throughout our testing period. ### CopyTrader Performance

Our 90-day CopyTrader test across five Popular Investors yielded mixed but instructive results. Four out of five copied traders were profitable, with returns ranging from +3.8% to +6.2%. The one losing trader (-2.3%) had the highest risk score at 7/10 and the highest trade frequency at 312 trades. ### Spread Consistency

Spreads remained stable during standard sessions — EUR/USD held at 1.0 pips consistently during London hours and expanded to 1.0-1.2 pips during Sydney session. During major news releases, spreads widened to 2.0-5.0 pips on EUR/USD and 2.5-6.0 pips on AUD/USD. Weekend open gaps were the most volatile, with spreads reaching 3.0-8.0 pips on majors and 4.0-10.0 pips on AUD pairs.

<Tip>
**Avoid trading during the first 30 minutes after weekend market open** (Monday 7:00am AEST). Spreads are at their widest and liquidity is at its lowest. Wait for the Asian session to stabilise before placing new positions. For news trading, use limit orders with defined entry prices rather than market orders to avoid excessive slippage.
</Tip>
