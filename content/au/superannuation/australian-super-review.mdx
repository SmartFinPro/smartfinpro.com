## AustralianSuper: Australia's Benchmark Superannuation Fund

AustralianSuper is Australia's largest superannuation fund by assets under management — with over AUD 330 billion in member assets and more than 3.4 million members as of early 2026. Founded in 2006 through the merger of two industry super funds, AustralianSuper has grown into a dominant force in Australia's retirement savings landscape, delivering a long-term investment track record powered by scale that enables access to direct infrastructure, private equity, and international property investments not available to smaller funds. For the trailing 10-year period to mid-2025, AustralianSuper's Balanced investment option delivered approximately 8.9% p.a. — placing it in the top decile of all [APRA](https://www.apra.gov.au/)-regulated super funds and above the median return of retail super funds by approximately 1.5-2.0% p.a. over long periods.

The compounding impact of this outperformance on a typical Australian's super balance over 30+ years of accumulation is substantial — frequently cited as hundreds of thousands of dollars in additional retirement wealth. As a profit-to-member industry fund, there are no external shareholders extracting profits from your returns. Every dollar earned after fees and costs flows directly to member accounts. If you are comparing this against the broader [superannuation landscape in Australia](/au/superannuation/), or exploring the [best super funds for 2026](/au/superannuation/best-super-funds-australia), AustralianSuper consistently ranks at or near the top on return-focused comparisons. The [ATO's YourSuper comparison tool](https://www.ato.gov.au/calculators-and-tools/yoursuper-comparison-tool) independently tracks and reports AustralianSuper's performance and fees, providing an authoritative third-party benchmark.

## Australian Pricing & Fee Structure 2026

AustralianSuper's fee structure is straightforward compared to many retail super funds. The fund charges a flat weekly administration fee regardless of balance size, plus a percentage-based investment fee that varies by option. Current fees are published in the [AustralianSuper Product Disclosure Statement](https://www.australiansuper.com/tools-and-advice/learn/pds-and-important-documents) and are independently reported via the [ATO YourSuper comparison tool](https://www.ato.gov.au/calculators-and-tools/yoursuper-comparison-tool). The Balanced option — which is the default and most popular choice among AustralianSuper's 3.4 million members — carries total fees that are competitive by industry standards, particularly when measured against the value delivered through long-term returns.

| Fee Component | Amount | Notes |
|---------------|--------|-------|
| **Administration fee** | AUD 1.50/week (AUD 78/year) | Flat fee regardless of balance |
| **Investment fee (Balanced)** | ~0.39% p.a. | Covers fund management costs |
| **Indirect cost ratio (Balanced)** | ~0.07% p.a. | Transaction and operational costs |
| **Total at AUD 50,000 (Balanced)** | ~AUD 385/year | AUD 78 admin + ~AUD 307 investment |
| **Total at AUD 100,000 (Balanced)** | ~AUD 539/year | AUD 78 admin + ~AUD 461 investment |
| **Total at AUD 200,000 (Balanced)** | ~AUD 998/year | AUD 78 admin + ~AUD 920 investment |

AustralianSuper's fee on the Balanced option at AUD 50,000 is approximately AUD 385 per year — competitively positioned relative to the Australian super fund median of approximately AUD 510/year. This is comparable to [Australian Retirement Trust](/au/superannuation/australian-retirement-trust-review) at approximately AUD 371/year for the same balance. The flat AUD 78/year administration fee means lower-balance members pay proportionally more in percentage terms, while larger balances benefit from the fixed-cost structure. For members with balances exceeding AUD 200,000, the percentage-based investment fee becomes the dominant cost driver.

### Indexed Options for Cost-Conscious Members

AustralianSuper also offers indexed investment options at significantly lower fees than the actively managed Balanced option. Members who prefer passive management and want to minimise fees can access these alternatives while remaining within the AustralianSuper ecosystem, benefiting from the fund's administrative scale and member services without paying for active management.

| Option | Investment Fee | Indirect Cost | Total at AUD 50,000 |
|--------|---------------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Australian Shares Indexed** | ~0.03% p.a. | ~0.01% p.a. | ~AUD 98/year |
| **International Shares Indexed** | ~0.10% p.a. | ~0.02% p.a. | ~AUD 138/year |
| **Balanced (Active, default)** | ~0.39% p.a. | ~0.07% p.a. | ~AUD 385/year |

The indexed options narrow the fee gap with Hostplus Indexed Balanced considerably. An AustralianSuper member choosing the International Shares Indexed option at AUD 50,000 would pay approximately AUD 138/year — competitive with Hostplus Indexed Balanced at approximately AUD 187/year. The trade-off is that indexed options do not benefit from AustralianSuper's direct infrastructure and private equity allocations, which have historically contributed to the Balanced option's outperformance.

### Break-Even Analysis: Active vs. Passive

The central question for many AustralianSuper members is whether the higher fees on the Balanced option justify the active management premium. Over the trailing 10-year period, the data supports the active approach — but past performance is not guaranteed to continue.

| Balance | AustralianSuper Balanced Fee | AustralianSuper Indexed Fee | Fee Difference | Return Needed to Justify |
|---------|--------|--------|---------|---------|
| AUD 50,000 | ~AUD 385/yr | ~AUD 138/yr | ~AUD 247/yr | ~0.49% p.a. return premium |
| AUD 100,000 | ~AUD 539/yr | ~AUD 198/yr | ~AUD 341/yr | ~0.34% p.a. return premium |
| AUD 200,000 | ~AUD 998/yr | ~AUD 318/yr | ~AUD 680/yr | ~0.34% p.a. return premium |

With the Balanced option historically outperforming passive alternatives by approximately 0.4-0.5% p.a. after fees, the active management premium has more than paid for itself over the past decade. However, members should understand that this outperformance is not guaranteed and that the gap may narrow in future periods. The break-even return premium required is modest at larger balances, which tilts the value proposition toward the active Balanced option for long-term members.

## Key Features for Australian Members

### 1. Investment Scale Advantages

AustralianSuper's AUD 330+ billion in assets under management provides access to investment opportunities that are simply unavailable to smaller funds. This scale advantage is the fund's most significant structural moat — it enables direct ownership of assets that generate inflation-linked returns and diversification beyond what public markets alone can deliver. Direct infrastructure holdings include stakes in airports (including Heathrow), toll roads, ports, and utilities globally. These assets provide inflation-linked returns and long-term cash flows that complement the fund's equity and fixed interest allocations. Private equity investments include direct positions in private companies, pre-IPO opportunities, and growth equity — asset classes that have historically delivered returns above public market equivalents. International real estate holdings span commercial, logistics, and residential assets across the US, UK, Europe, and Asia, providing geographic diversification and exposure to global property markets.

These direct investment capabilities are typically reserved for sovereign wealth funds and very large pension funds. AustralianSuper members benefit from institutional-grade access through their everyday super account — without needing to meet the multi-million-dollar minimum investment thresholds that direct infrastructure and private equity funds typically require. If you are managing your broader financial strategy alongside superannuation, our guide to [personal finance in Australia](/au/personal-finance/) covers how super fits within a diversified wealth-building approach.

AustralianSuper offers six pre-mixed options and five DIY single-asset-class options, giving members flexibility to tailor their investment allocation to their risk tolerance and retirement timeframe. ### 4. Advice Services

AustralianSuper provides tiered financial advice to members. Free basic super advice is available over the phone or via the app, covering super-related decisions including investment option selection, contribution strategies, and insurance settings. This is limited to advice about your AustralianSuper account and does not extend to broader financial planning. Comprehensive financial advice is available through AustralianSuper's advice service for a fee, covering broader topics including tax planning, estate planning, and non-super investments. For members approaching retirement, this comprehensive service can help navigate the complex transition from accumulation to pension phase.

### 5. Retirement Income Product

AustralianSuper's Choice Income account enables members who have reached their preservation age to draw flexible income from their super balance while the remaining balance continues to earn investment returns. Features include flexible drawdown frequency from weekly to annual payments, continued access to all investment options, and the ability to adjust payment amounts as your needs change. This product competes directly with account-based pensions offered by other major industry funds, with the key advantage being continued access to AustralianSuper's investment scale and active management capabilities during the retirement drawdown phase.

## AustralianSuper Features Deep-Dive

### The Scale Advantage in Practice

Understanding why AustralianSuper's scale matters requires looking beyond the headline return figure. With AUD 330+ billion under management, AustralianSuper can negotiate institutional-grade fee arrangements with external fund managers, access co-investment opportunities alongside sovereign wealth funds, and build an in-house investment team that would be uneconomical for smaller funds. The fund employs over 200 investment professionals managing assets across 50+ countries. This internal capability reduces reliance on external managers and their associated fee layers, directly benefiting members through lower costs and more aligned investment decisions.

Direct infrastructure is perhaps the most tangible example of the scale advantage. AustralianSuper holds direct stakes in assets including Heathrow Airport, WestConnex (Sydney motorway), Ausgrid (NSW electricity distributor), and port infrastructure across Australia and globally. These assets generate stable, inflation-linked cash flows that are fundamentally different from listed equities — they provide portfolio diversification that cannot be replicated by simply buying infrastructure ETFs on the ASX. The returns from these direct holdings have historically exceeded listed infrastructure benchmarks, and the illiquidity premium they capture is a direct consequence of AustralianSuper's scale and long-term investment horizon.

### Performance Track Record Across Options

While the Balanced option's 8.9% p.a. 10-year return attracts the most attention, AustralianSuper's other options have also performed competitively. The High Growth option, with its ~90% growth allocation, has delivered even higher absolute returns over the 10-year period, though with greater volatility. The Conservative Balanced option has provided solid risk-adjusted returns for members closer to retirement who cannot tolerate significant drawdowns. Understanding performance across all options is important because members who are 20+ years from retirement may benefit from the higher-growth options, while those within 5-10 years of retirement may prefer the stability of Conservative Balanced or Stable.

| Option | 10-Year Return (approx.) | Risk Level | Typical Member |
|--------|--------------------------|------------|----------------|
| **High Growth** | ~9.5-10.0% p.a. | High | 20+ years to retirement |
| **Balanced** | ~8.9% p.a. | Medium-High | Default, 10+ years to retirement |
| **Socially Aware** | ~8.6% p.a. | Medium-High | ESG-conscious members |
| **Conservative Balanced** | ~7.0% p.a. | Medium | 5-10 years to retirement |
| **Stable** | ~5.5% p.a. | Low-Medium | Near or in retirement |

The spread between High Growth (~10% p.a.) and Stable (~5.5% p.a.) illustrates the significant impact of asset allocation on long-term outcomes. A member who switched from Stable to Balanced 10 years ago would have seen materially higher returns — but they also would have experienced larger drawdowns during market corrections. The right option depends on your individual circumstances, risk tolerance, and time to retirement. If you are exploring how superannuation fits alongside other [savings strategies in Australia](/au/savings/), understanding your super's asset allocation is an essential first step.

### Compounding Impact: The Case Study

To illustrate the real-world impact of AustralianSuper's performance advantage, consider the following scenario drawn from the SEO analysis. A 35-year-old Melbourne teacher switched from a retail super fund (estimated 6.8% p.a. 10-year return, AUD 800/year fees on AUD 80,000 balance) to AustralianSuper (estimated 8.9% p.a., AUD 469/year fees) in 2025. ### APRA Annual Performance Test

[APRA's annual performance test](https://www.apra.gov.au/annual-superannuation-performance-test) is the most rigorous public assessment of superannuation fund performance in Australia. Funds that fail the test must notify their members and face potential restrictions on accepting new members. ### Member Asset Protection

Member assets are held by independent custodians — legally separate from AustralianSuper's operational capital. This custodian separation means that if AustralianSuper (the trustee entity) were to face financial difficulties, member assets would remain ring-fenced and accessible to members. The trustee has fiduciary obligations to act in members' best financial interests — a legal duty enforced by both [APRA](https://www.apra.gov.au/) and [ASIC](https://asic.gov.au/).

It is important to understand that super fund assets are not covered by the [Australian Government Financial Claims Scheme](https://www.apra.gov.au/financial-claims-scheme-for-adis), which guarantees bank deposits up to AUD 250,000 per depositor at Authorised Deposit-taking Institutions. Superannuation is an investment, not a deposit — your balance will fluctuate with market performance. However, APRA's prudential framework, the requirement for independent custodians, and the trustee's fiduciary duties provide strong structural protection that is materially different from, but complementary to, deposit insurance.

## How AustralianSuper Makes Money

Understanding AustralianSuper's revenue model is important context for evaluating its trustworthiness and alignment with your interests. As a profit-to-member fund, AustralianSuper's financial model is fundamentally different from retail super funds operated by listed financial services companies.

AustralianSuper generates revenue through **four primary channels:**

1. **Administration fees** — the AUD 1.50/week (AUD 78/year) flat fee charged to every member covers operational costs including technology, member services, compliance, and corporate overheads. This fee does not vary with your balance size.

2. **Investment fees** — the percentage-based fee (e.g., ~0.39% p.a. for Balanced) covers the cost of managing investments, including payments to external fund managers, custodian fees, and the cost of AustralianSuper's in-house investment team. A portion of these fees represents transaction costs incurred in buying and selling assets.

3. **Insurance premiums** — while AustralianSuper does not profit from insurance (premiums are passed through to external insurers), the administration of group insurance generates some administrative revenue and provides a service that supports member retention.

4. **Operational scale efficiencies** — as the fund grows, fixed costs are spread across more members, reducing the per-member cost base. This creates a virtuous cycle where scale enables lower fees, which attracts more members, which further reduces costs.

The critical difference from retail funds is what is absent: there are no shareholder dividends, no executive bonuses tied to fee revenue targets, and no profit margin extracted before returns reach members. Every dollar earned after operating costs and investment fees flows to member accounts. This structural alignment means AustralianSuper is genuinely incentivised to maximise net returns to members — higher returns attract more members, which increases scale, which enables lower costs and better investment access.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<details>
<summary>Is AustralianSuper Australia's largest super fund?</summary>

Yes. AustralianSuper is Australia's largest superannuation fund by both assets under management (approximately $340 billion) and membership (3.3 million members as of 2026). It was formed in 2006 through the merger of Australian Retirement Fund and Superannuation Trust of Australia, and operates as a profit-for-members industry fund regulated by APRA.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What are AustralianSuper's fees?</summary>

AustralianSuper charges an administration fee of $1 per week ($52 per year) plus a percentage administration fee of 0.10% p.a. (capped at $350 for balances above $350,000). Investment fees vary by option (approximately 0.56% p.a. for the Balanced option). Total fees for a $50,000 balance are approximately 0.68% p.a. — among the lowest of any large Australian super fund.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How has AustralianSuper's Balanced option performed?</summary>

AustralianSuper's Balanced option (the default MySuper product) has delivered approximately 8.7% p.a. over 10 years to March 2026, consistently ranking in the top quartile of comparable APRA-regulated MySuper products. Performance varies year to year — check APRA's heatmap and SuperRatings data for the most current independent rankings. Past returns do not guarantee future performance.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I choose my investments in AustralianSuper?</summary>

Yes. AustralianSuper offers 8 pre-mixed options (including Balanced, Growth, Stable, and Socially Aware) and a range of single-asset class options for more experienced investors. Members can also use AustralianSuper's Member Direct option to invest in ASX-listed shares, ETFs, and term deposits directly through their super account. Self-directed investment is available for members with sufficient knowledge and balance.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Is AustralianSuper subject to APRA's performance test?</summary>

Yes. AustralianSuper's MySuper Balanced product is assessed annually under APRA's Performance Test. The fund has passed consistently since the test was introduced in 2021. AustralianSuper is also assessed in APRA's heatmap comparing fees and performance across all APRA-regulated funds. The fund's scale (3.3M members) provides significant fee bargaining power with investment managers.

</details>
