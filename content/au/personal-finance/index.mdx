## Fixed vs. Variable Rates: The 2026 Guide for Australian Homeowners

With the RBA cash rate holding at 4.35% and forward markets pricing in potential cuts later in 2026, the fixed-versus-variable decision is more nuanced than ever.

### Variable Rate: When It Makes Sense

Variable rates move with the market. When rates fall, your repayments decrease automatically. When rates rise, so do your costs.

**Choose variable if:**
- You expect the RBA to cut rates in the coming 12-24 months
- You want the flexibility to make unlimited extra repayments and redraw at any time
- You plan to sell or refinance within the next 2-3 years (avoiding fixed rate break costs)
- You want access to offset account functionality (only available on variable at most lenders)

**Current best variable rates:** Athena from 5.99% | ubank from 6.09% | ANZ from 6.29%

### Fixed Rate: When It Makes Sense

Fixed rates lock in your repayments for a set period (typically 1-5 years), providing certainty regardless of RBA movements.

**Choose fixed if:**
- You want repayment certainty for budgeting — your rate will not change during the fixed period
- You believe rates will remain elevated or increase further
- You do not plan to sell or refinance during the fixed period (break costs can be substantial)
- You can accept reduced flexibility (most fixed rates limit extra repayments to A$10,000-A$20,000/year)

**Current best fixed rates:** Westpac 2yr fixed from 5.89% | CommBank 2yr fixed from 5.99%

### Split Loan Strategy

A split loan divides your mortgage into variable and fixed portions. This hedges your rate risk:

- **Example:** A$600,000 loan split 50/50 — A$300,000 fixed at 5.89% + A$300,000 variable at 6.09%
- **Benefit:** Partial rate protection with continued offset/redraw access on the variable portion
- **Available at:** CommBank, Westpac, ANZ, NAB (not available at Athena or ubank)

<Info>
**2026 rate outlook:** Forward interest rate swap markets suggest the RBA may begin easing in late 2026 if inflation continues to moderate. If you fix now at 5.89% and the RBA cuts 0.75% over the next 2 years, variable rates could fall below your fixed rate. This is why many advisers currently favour variable or a split approach — but nobody can predict the future with certainty.
</Info>

## The Australian Home Loan Challenge

### Rising Rates and the Serviceability Buffer

Since the RBA began its tightening cycle, Australian borrowers have faced significant challenges. Lenders now apply a **serviceability buffer of 3 percentage points** above the loan rate when assessing your borrowing capacity, as mandated by APRA's prudential standards.

This means that even if you are applying for a loan at 6.00%, the lender will assess whether you can afford repayments at **9.00%**. For a dual-income household earning A$180,000 combined, this buffer can reduce maximum borrowing capacity by A$150,000 or more compared to pre-tightening assessments.

**Key market dynamics in 2026:**
- RBA cash rate remains elevated, putting pressure on variable rate borrowers
- Fixed rates have become competitive again as markets price in potential rate cuts
- Lenders are offering aggressive cashback and rate discounts to attract refinancers
- LMI costs continue to add significant expense for borrowers with less than 20% deposit
- First home buyer schemes provide meaningful support but come with property price caps

<Warning>
**APRA serviceability buffer:** All authorised deposit-taking institutions (ADIs) must assess your capacity to repay at a minimum of 3 percentage points above the loan product rate, or a floor rate of 5.50% — whichever is higher. This directly impacts how much you can borrow, regardless of your actual income and expenses.
</Warning>

### Variable Rate vs. Comparison Rate

In Australia, ASIC requires all lenders to display a **comparison rate** alongside the headline interest rate. The comparison rate includes the effect of fees and charges on the total cost of the loan, calculated on a A$150,000 loan over 25 years.

**Why the comparison rate matters:**
- A lender advertising 5.99% with A$395/year in fees may have a comparison rate of 6.20%
- A lender advertising 6.10% with zero fees may have a comparison rate of 6.10%
- The second option is actually cheaper despite the higher headline rate

### Offset Account vs. Redraw: Which Saves You More?

Two of the most powerful features in Australian home loans are the **offset account** and the **redraw facility**. Understanding the difference can save you tens of thousands:

| Feature | Offset Account | Redraw Facility |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| **How it works** | Savings balance offsets your loan balance for interest calculation | Extra repayments available to withdraw |
| **Tax benefit** | No interest earned = no tax payable (superior for investors) | Interest savings are not considered taxable income |
| **Access** | Instant access via debit card and BPAY | Usually via app or phone — may take 1-2 days |
| **Investment properties** | Preserves tax deductibility of loan interest | Redrawing may affect tax deductibility |
| **Best for** | Daily banking, salary crediting, investment property owners | Borrowers who want lower rates and occasional access to surplus funds |

<Tip>
**Offset strategy:** Credit your salary directly into your offset account. Even parking A$50,000 in an offset against a 6.19% loan saves you **A$3,095 in interest per year** — more than the A$395 annual package fee. The longer you maintain a high offset balance, the more years you shave off your loan term.
</Tip>

### First Home Super Saver Scheme (FHSSS)

The FHSSS allows first home buyers to save for a deposit inside their superannuation fund, benefiting from concessional tax treatment:

- **Maximum contributions:** A$15,000 per financial year, up to A$50,000 total
- **Tax benefit:** Voluntary contributions are taxed at 15% (super rate) instead of your marginal tax rate
- **Savings example:** On a marginal tax rate of 32.5%, saving A$50,000 via FHSSS saves approximately **A$8,750 in tax** compared to saving in a standard bank account
- **Withdrawal:** Apply to the ATO for a FHSSS determination, then request release of funds

<Warning>
**FHSSS timing:** Once you request a FHSSS determination from the ATO, you must sign a contract to purchase or construct a home within **12 months** (extensions may be granted). Plan your property search timeline before applying for release.
</Warning>

### Home Guarantee Scheme (HGS)

The Australian Government's Home Guarantee Scheme helps eligible buyers purchase with as little as a 2% deposit without paying Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI):

- **First Home Guarantee (FHBG):** 35,000 places per year for first home buyers with 5% deposit (no LMI)
- **Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee:** 10,000 places per year for regional buyers
- **Family Home Guarantee:** 5,000 places per year for single parents with 2% deposit
- **Property price caps:** Vary by location (e.g., A$900,000 in Sydney, A$800,000 in Melbourne)

**LMI savings example:** On a A$700,000 property with a 5% deposit (A$35,000), LMI would typically cost **A$25,000-A$35,000**. The Home Guarantee Scheme eliminates this cost entirely.

### When to Consider Refinancing

Refinancing makes financial sense when the savings outweigh the costs. As a general rule, if you can secure a rate **at least 0.50% lower** than your current loan, the numbers usually work in your favour.

**Signs it is time to refinance:**
- Your rate is more than 0.50% above the best available rate for your LVR bracket
- You have been on your lender's standard variable rate (SVR) without negotiating
- Your property value has increased, reducing your LVR below 80% (avoiding LMI)
- You are still paying an annual fee on a loan that no longer offers competitive value
- Your fixed rate period is about to expire and the revert rate is significantly higher

### Refinancing Cost-Benefit Analysis

| Cost | Typical Amount | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|
| **Discharge fee** | A$150-A$400 | Charged by your current lender |
| **Break cost (fixed rate)** | Varies widely | Can be A$0-A$30,000+ depending on rate differential and remaining term |
| **Government fees** | A$150-A$500 | Mortgage registration and discharge fees (vary by state) |

### 1. Ignoring the Comparison Rate

**The problem:** Focusing on the headline variable rate without accounting for fees. A loan advertised at 5.99% with A$600 in annual fees may cost more than a loan at 6.10% with zero fees.

**The fix:** Always compare using the comparison rate, and for larger loans (A$500,000+), calculate the total cost of borrowing over your expected loan term.

### 2. Not Negotiating Your Rate

**The problem:** Staying on your lender's standard variable rate (SVR) without asking for a discount. The SVR is typically 0.50-1.50% above the best available rate from the same lender.

**The fix:** Call your lender's retention team every 12 months with competitor rates in hand. Most lenders will match or come close to avoid losing your business. If they refuse, refinance.

### 3. Paying Lenders Mortgage Insurance When You Could Avoid It

**The problem:** Purchasing with less than a 20% deposit triggers LMI, which can cost A$10,000-A$40,000 depending on your loan size and LVR.

**The fix:** Explore the Home Guarantee Scheme, save to 20%, or consider a family guarantee arrangement where a parent's property provides additional security.

### 4. Choosing Fixed Rate Without Understanding Break Costs

**The problem:** Locking into a fixed rate for security, then needing to sell or refinance before the term expires. Fixed rate break costs in Australia can be **A$10,000-A$30,000+** depending on the rate differential and remaining term.

**The fix:** Only fix if you are confident you will not need to sell, refinance, or make repayments above the allowed extra repayment limit during the fixed period. Consider splitting your loan — part fixed, part variable.

### 5. Not Using Your Offset Account Effectively

**The problem:** Having an offset account but keeping savings in a separate savings account that earns taxable interest at a lower rate than your home loan rate.

**The fix:** Keep all liquid funds in your offset account. At a loan rate of 6.19%, every dollar in offset effectively earns 6.19% tax-free — far better than any savings account after tax.
