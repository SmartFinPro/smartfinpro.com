### How the Offset Account Works

The ANZ offset account functions as a standard transaction account with a debit card, internet banking, and direct debit capabilities. It is linked directly to your home loan, and interest is calculated daily on the net balance after offset. Every dollar sitting in the offset account reduces the amount on which you pay interest.

<Tip>
**Salary crediting strategy:** Have your salary paid directly into your offset account. Even though you spend throughout the month, the daily interest calculation means every dollar sitting in offset — even temporarily — reduces your interest. This maximises the time your money spends working against your loan balance.
</Tip>

### Offset vs Redraw — Key Differences

For investors, this distinction is particularly important for tax deductibility:

| Feature | Offset Account | Redraw Facility |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Accessibility** | Instant via debit card | Transfer required |
| **Tax implications** | No tax event | May reduce deductible debt for investors |
| **Transaction account** | Yes — full functionality | No — loan facility only |
| **Direct debits** | Yes | No |
| **Investment property** | Preserves tax deductibility | May compromise deductible portion |

<Warning>
**Important for property investors:** If your home loan is for an investment property, using an offset account preserves the tax deductibility of your loan interest. Placing spare funds in a redraw facility and then withdrawing them for personal use could reduce the tax-deductible portion of your investment loan. Seek advice from a qualified tax professional before choosing between offset and redraw on investment debt.
</Warning>

## ANZ Home Loan Application & Approval Timeline

Applying for an ANZ Simplicity PLUS Home Loan can be done entirely online, through a mobile lender, or via one of ANZ's 600+ branches. The broker channel is also available — ANZ works with accredited mortgage brokers across Australia who can manage the application on your behalf.

**Step 1 — Pre-approval (conditional approval).** The fastest path to pre-approval is the ANZ online application at anz.com.au. For straightforward PAYG applicants, this can be completed in under 30 minutes. ANZ uses automated credit decisioning for eligible applications, meaning initial assessment results are often returned within minutes. Our testing showed an average of 4 business days for written conditional approval, which is consistent with ANZ's published timeframes of 3–5 business days.

**Step 2 — Document submission.** Required documents for most applicants include the last 2 payslips, 3 months of bank statements, 2 years of tax returns (self-employed), a copy of the property contract of sale (for purchases), and proof of existing liabilities. ANZ's digital document upload via the app or online portal is straightforward and avoids the need to fax or post physical copies.

**Step 3 — Property valuation.** ANZ orders a valuation through their panel valuers. For standard residential properties in metro areas, this typically takes 3–5 business days. The valuation determines your final LVR tier and the rate you will pay. For properties in regional or rural areas, allow 5–7 business days for the valuation to be arranged.

**Step 4 — Unconditional approval.** Full unconditional approval follows within 7–14 business days of a successful valuation, based on our 6-month testing period. ANZ's mobile lender team proactively manages this stage — having a dedicated mobile lender consistently accelerated our process by 2–3 business days compared to managing the application online only.

**Step 5 — Settlement.** Settlement is coordinated by ANZ's settlements team working with the vendor's solicitors. For purchases, ANZ targets 30 days from unconditional approval to settlement. For refinancers, ANZ manages the discharge with your existing lender and targets a 21-business-day settlement.

<Tip>
**Use a mobile lender.** ANZ's mobile lenders are available evenings and weekends, have authority to expedite documentation reviews, and provide a consistent point of contact through the approval process. Request one at the start of your application rather than relying solely on the generic call centre.
</Tip>
