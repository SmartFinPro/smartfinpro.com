ubank is not a separate entity — it operates under NAB's Australian Credit Licence (ACL 12345) and APRA registration as an Authorised Deposit-taking Institution (ADI). Your money has the same protections as a NAB customer, including the **Financial Claims Scheme covering deposits up to A$250,000 per account holder**.

The brand targets digitally-savvy Australians who want competitive rates without the fees and complexity of traditional big four packages. With the **Neat Variable Home Loan** as its flagship product, ubank strips away the annual package fees, application fees, and ongoing charges that typically accompany big bank home loans.

<Info>
**Why does NAB backing matter?** Unlike standalone fintech lenders, ubank operates as a division of an APRA-regulated ADI. This means NAB's balance sheet, capital reserves, and prudential oversight stand behind every ubank home loan. In a credit crisis, this institutional backing provides a level of stability that non-ADI lenders cannot match.
</Info>

## Neat Variable Home Loan Explained

The "Neat" branding reflects ubank's philosophy: **one simple product, no hidden fees, no package tiers**.

### What Makes Neat Different

1. **Single product, zero complexity:** Unlike CommBank (5+ home loan products) or Westpac (multiple packages), ubank offers one variable home loan. This simplifies decision-making and eliminates the "which package?" confusion.

2. **Rate = comparison rate:** Because there are no fees, the advertised rate and the ASIC-mandated comparison rate are identical. This is rare among Australian lenders.

3. **No annual fee saves A$11,850:** Over 30 years, avoiding a typical A$395 annual package fee saves you **A$11,850** — money that compounds when redirected into extra repayments or a savings offset.

4. **NAB backbone:** ubank uses NAB's lending infrastructure, credit assessment systems, and regulatory framework. You get fintech pricing with big four systems.

5. **Redraw as offset alternative:** While there is no offset account, the unlimited free redraw facility provides a similar interest-saving effect. Extra repayments reduce your loan balance and therefore your interest charges.

<Warning>
**Redraw vs offset — key difference:** With an offset account, your money sits in a separate transaction account and reduces the loan balance for interest calculation purposes only. With redraw, your extra repayments go directly onto the loan balance. The practical difference: redrawing requires a request (typically processed within 24 hours), whereas offset funds are available instantly via your transaction account. For most borrowers, this distinction is minor.
</Warning>
