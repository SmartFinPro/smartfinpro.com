## Australian Pricing & Trading Costs 2026

CMC Markets uses a spread-only pricing model for most instruments, which means you pay no separate commission — the cost is embedded in the bid/ask spread. This approach is simpler than the raw spread + commission model used by ECN brokers, but it results in marginally wider headline spreads on major forex pairs. For share CFDs, CMC charges a percentage-based spread with a minimum fee, while CMC Invest (direct shares) uses a flat brokerage model. All figures below are sourced from the [official CMC Markets pricing page](https://www.cmcmarkets.com/en-au/cfd-trading/pricing) and verified during our 90-day testing period.

| **EUR/USD** | ~0.7 pips | No commission | 0.01 lots |
| **AUD/USD** | ~0.6 pips | No commission | 0.01 lots |

| **Crypto CFDs** | From 1.0% | No commission | Varies |

### CMC Invest: Direct Share Trading

CMC Invest is CMC Markets' direct share trading product — not CFDs, but actual ASX share purchases at A$0 brokerage for the first 30 days, then A$11 per trade (or 0.10%, whichever is greater). This is CMC's entry into the low-cost direct investing space, competing with SelfWealth and Superhero. It is important to note that CMC Invest shares are held in custody, not CHESS-sponsored — meaning your shares are registered in CMC's name rather than your own name on the ASX's CHESS register. For traders who prioritise direct ownership, SelfWealth or CommSec offer CHESS-sponsored holding.

## How CMC Markets Makes Money

Understanding CMC Markets' revenue model helps you anticipate where costs may increase and where the platform's incentives align — or conflict — with yours as a trader. As an LSE-listed company, CMC's revenue breakdown is publicly available through quarterly financial reporting, providing unusual transparency for a retail broker.

CMC Markets generates revenue through **five primary channels** in Australia:

1. **Spread income from CFD trading** — The primary revenue driver. CMC earns the difference between the bid and ask price (the spread) on every CFD trade. At ~0.7 pips on EUR/USD, each standard lot trade generates approximately A$7 for CMC. Across millions of trades globally, this represents the bulk of CMC's revenue
2. **Overnight financing charges** — When traders hold CFD positions overnight, CMC charges a financing fee calculated as the benchmark interest rate plus a markup (typically 2.5% p.a.). For traders who hold positions for days or weeks, this cost compounds significantly
3. **Currency conversion fees** — A 0.7% fee applies when the profit/loss on a trade is denominated in a currency other than AUD. For traders with significant international share CFD positions, this is a material ongoing cost
4. **CMC Invest brokerage** — A$11 per trade on direct ASX share purchases (after the initial 30-day free period). As CMC grows its Invest product, this revenue stream is expected to increase
5. **Stockbroking and institutional services** — CMC also provides institutional-grade liquidity and execution services, though this is less relevant to retail traders

This model means CMC is incentivised to attract active traders who execute frequently (spread income) and hold positions overnight (financing income). The spread-only model is simpler than raw spread + commission, but it also means CMC's revenue is directly tied to the width of its spreads — which is why the 0.7 pip EUR/USD spread is unlikely to narrow to match ECN competitors.
