## Understanding CFD Trading

### What is a CFD?

A CFD (Contract for Difference) is a derivative that lets you speculate on price movements without owning the underlying asset.

| Aspect | CFDs | Real Assets |
|--------|------|-------------|
| **Ownership** | No | Yes |
| **Dividends** | Adjustment, not real | Real payments |
| **Voting rights** | No | Yes (shares) |
| **Leverage** | Yes (up to 30:1 retail) | Limited |
| **Short selling** | Easy | Complex/costly |
| **Overnight costs** | Yes | No (shares) |

### ASIC Leverage Limits for Retail Clients

Since 2021, ASIC's Product Intervention Order caps leverage for retail CFD traders:

| Instrument | Maximum Leverage |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Major forex pairs | 30:1 |
| Minor forex pairs, gold, major indices | 20:1 |
| Other commodities, minor indices | 10:1 |
| Shares CFDs | 5:1 |
| Crypto CFDs | 2:1 |

<Info>
These limits protect Australian retail traders from excessive losses. Professional clients may apply for higher leverage, but must meet strict eligibility criteria.
</Info>

### CFD Risks


**Key risks:**
- Leverage amplifies losses as well as gains
- Overnight financing erodes long-term positions
- Spreads widen during volatility
- No ownership benefits (dividends, voting)
