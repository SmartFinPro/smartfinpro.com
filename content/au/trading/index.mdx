## ASIC Regulation: What Australian Traders Need to Know

### ASIC's Product Intervention Powers

Since October 2021, ASIC has exercised its product intervention powers to impose strict conditions on retail CFD and forex trading in Australia. These rules are designed to protect retail clients and have meaningfully reduced losses across the industry.

**Key ASIC requirements for all brokers we recommend:**

- **Australian Financial Services Licence (AFSL):** Every broker must hold a valid AFSL to offer financial products to Australian retail clients. You can verify any broker's AFSL on ASIC Connect.
- **Client money segregation:** All client funds must be held in segregated trust accounts at authorised Australian deposit-taking institutions (ADIs) — separate from the broker's own operating capital.
- **Negative balance protection:** Mandatory for all retail accounts. Your losses cannot exceed your account balance — you will never owe more than you deposited.
- **Risk warnings:** ASIC requires standardised risk warnings displaying the percentage of retail clients who lose money with each provider.

### ASIC Leverage Restrictions (Retail Clients)

ASIC's leverage caps apply to all retail client accounts at ASIC-regulated brokers:

| Asset Class | Maximum Leverage | Margin Required |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Major currency pairs** (e.g., AUD/USD, EUR/USD) | 30:1 | 3.33% |
| **Minor currency pairs** (e.g., AUD/NZD, GBP/AUD) | 20:1 | 5.00% |
| **Gold** | 20:1 | 5.00% |
| **Other commodities** (e.g., oil, silver) | 15:1 | 6.67% |
| **Equity indices** (e.g., ASX 200, S&P 500) | 10:1 | 10.00% |
| **Cryptocurrency CFDs** | 2:1 | 50.00% |
| **Individual equity CFDs** | 5:1 | 20.00% |

<Info>
**Professional client classification:** If you meet ASIC's criteria for a wholesale or professional client (generally A$2.5M+ in net assets or A$250K+ gross income for the past two years), you may be eligible for higher leverage. However, this waives many retail protections including negative balance protection. We recommend most traders maintain retail status.
</Info>

### AFCA: Your Dispute Resolution Safety Net

The Australian Financial Complaints Authority (AFCA) provides free, independent dispute resolution for complaints against ASIC-regulated financial services providers:

- **Coverage:** Complaints up to A$1,085,000 (financial loss) and A$5,425,000 (other claims)
- **Process:** Lodge a complaint online at afca.org.au — the service is free for consumers
- **Timeframe:** Most complaints are resolved within 60–90 days
- **Binding decisions:** AFCA rulings are binding on the financial firm but not on the consumer — you retain the right to pursue the matter further in court

**All five brokers in our ranking are AFCA members**, which is a mandatory requirement for ASIC-regulated entities offering services to retail clients.

### Verifying Your Broker's ASIC Registration

Before depositing funds with any broker, verify their credentials:

1. Visit **ASIC Connect** (connectonline.asic.gov.au)
2. Search by company name or AFSL number
3. Confirm the licence status is **"Current"**
4. Check the **"Authorised services"** include dealing in financial products

## Tax Considerations for Australian Traders

Australian traders should be aware of the following tax implications:

- **Capital Gains Tax (CGT):** Profits from trading are generally assessable income. Forex and CFD gains are typically taxed as ordinary income at your marginal tax rate.
- **CGT discount:** If you hold positions for more than 12 months, you may be eligible for a 50% CGT discount on the gain (for individuals — not applicable to companies or trusts without specific rules).
- **Losses:** Trading losses can generally be offset against other capital gains. Net capital losses can be carried forward indefinitely.
- **GST:** Financial supplies (including forex and CFD trading) are input-taxed, meaning GST generally does not apply to trading fees.
- **Record keeping:** The ATO requires you to maintain records of all trades for at least five years. Most brokers provide downloadable trade histories and annual statements.

<Warning>
**Important:** Tax treatment of trading income depends on whether you are classified as a trader or an investor by the ATO. If trading is your primary income source, profits may be treated as ordinary income rather than capital gains. Consult a qualified Australian tax professional for personalised advice.
</Warning>

<Tip>
**The 1–2% Rule:** Never risk more than 1–2% of your account on a single trade. With ASIC's 30:1 leverage cap on major pairs, this is especially important — a single poorly managed trade can still cause significant drawdown. At 2% risk per trade, you can sustain 50 consecutive losses before account depletion, providing the runway needed to refine your strategy.
</Tip>
