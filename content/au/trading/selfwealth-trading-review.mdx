## Key Features for Australian Investors

### 1. CHESS Sponsorship — The Core Differentiator

Every ASX share purchased through SelfWealth is CHESS-sponsored — directly registered on [ASX's electronic share register](https://www2.asx.com.au/markets/clearing-and-settlement) in your name. This is the fundamental distinction between SelfWealth and cheaper custodial alternatives like Superhero (AUD 5/trade) or Stake (AUD 3/trade for US shares). When you buy shares through SelfWealth, you receive a Holder Identification Number (HIN) confirming your status as the direct legal holder on the CHESS (Clearing House Electronic Subregister System) system. This has four critical implications for Australian investors that justify the AUD 4.50 premium over Superhero's custodial model.

Portability is the most important practical benefit of CHESS sponsorship. If SelfWealth ceased operations — or you simply decided to switch brokers — your shares are already registered in your name on CHESS. You can transfer your HIN to any other CHESS-participant broker (CommSec, NAB Trade, Interactive Brokers, etc.) without selling a single share. This is fundamentally different from custodial brokers, where you may need to sell all holdings and repurchase them through a new platform, potentially triggering capital gains tax events.

Corporate actions access is the second major advantage. As a CHESS-registered shareholder, you receive dividend reinvestment plans (DRP), share purchase plans (SPP), rights issues, and entitlement offers directly — exactly as you would through a Big 4 bank broker. Custodial holders may not have access to all corporate actions, or may face delays in processing them. For investors building long-term positions in dividend-paying companies like BHP, CBA, or Telstra, DRP eligibility alone can compound returns significantly over a decade.

## SelfWealth Features Deep-Dive

### CHESS Sponsorship: What Happens If SelfWealth Goes Under?

This is the question every investor should ask before committing to any broker, and CHESS sponsorship provides the clearest answer in the Australian market. When you hold CHESS-sponsored shares through SelfWealth, your shares are registered on ASX's electronic subregister in your name — not in SelfWealth's name. Your Holder Identification Number (HIN) is your proof of direct legal ownership. If SelfWealth were to cease operations, enter administration, or be acquired by another entity, your CHESS-registered shares are legally yours. You can instruct another CHESS-participant broker to take over sponsorship of your HIN, and your holdings transfer seamlessly without any need to sell and repurchase.

This contrasts sharply with custodial-model brokers like Superhero, Stake, or eToro, where shares are held in an omnibus account in the broker's name (or their custodian's name) on your behalf. In a custodial broker insolvency, your shares become part of a trust that must be unwound through the insolvency process — a potentially lengthy and uncertain procedure. For investors with significant portfolios (AUD 50,000+), or for SMSF trustees who must demonstrate clear asset ownership for audit purposes, CHESS sponsorship is not merely a preference — it is a risk management essential.

### FX Conversion on US Share Trades

SelfWealth's US share trading is priced at USD 9.50 flat brokerage, but the true cost includes the FX conversion spread of approximately 0.60% above the mid-market rate. To illustrate what this means in practice, consider an investor purchasing USD 20,000 worth of US shares. The brokerage is USD 9.50, and the FX spread on converting AUD to USD is approximately 0.60% — roughly AUD 120 on a USD 20,000 purchase. The total effective cost is therefore approximately AUD 135 (brokerage + FX spread), or about 0.67% of the trade value.

SelfWealth's US trading is best used as a complement to an ASX-focused portfolio rather than a standalone US trading platform. If you are looking for [forex broker options in Australia](/au/forex/) with tighter FX spreads, specialist platforms typically offer better rates on currency conversion.

### Community Benchmarking: How It Works

SelfWealth's community feature aggregates anonymised data from 120,000+ member accounts to create a real-time benchmarking tool. The practical value of this feature depends on your investment style. For passive index investors, it provides confirmation that your ETF-based approach is performing in line with the market. The most-held stocks list also functions as a useful research starting point — if a stock appears in the top 20 most-held SelfWealth positions, it has passed at least the basic scrutiny of 120,000+ fellow Australian investors, though this should never substitute for your own due diligence.
