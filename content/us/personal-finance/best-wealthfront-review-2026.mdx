## Key Features Breakdown

### Automated Portfolio Management

Wealthfront builds diversified portfolios using low-cost ETFs across 11 asset classes, including US stocks, international developed markets, emerging markets, bonds, real estate (REITs), and natural resources. The platform uses Modern Portfolio Theory and automatically rebalances as your portfolio drifts from target allocations. You can customize your risk score on a scale of 1 to 10, and the system adjusts your allocation accordingly. Rebalancing events are also used as tax-loss harvesting opportunities, so even routine portfolio maintenance generates potential tax savings in taxable accounts.

### Path Financial Planning

Wealthfront's free financial planning tool connects to your external accounts (bank, brokerage, 401k, credit cards) and provides detailed projections for retirement, home buying, college savings, and time off. It factors in your income growth trajectory, Social Security benefits, expected spending, and investment returns to create a comprehensive picture. Path is one of the most sophisticated free planning tools available from any robo-advisor, and it updates dynamically as your financial situation changes.

### Self-Driving Money

This feature automatically routes your paycheck to the right accounts based on rules you set: fixed expenses, savings goals, and investment contributions. It eliminates the need to manually transfer money between accounts each month and ensures you are consistently investing without having to think about it. Self-Driving Money integrates with direct deposit and categorizes spending automatically.

### Stock-Level Tax-Loss Harvesting (Direct Indexing)

For accounts over $100,000, Wealthfront purchases the individual stocks that make up an index instead of the corresponding ETF. This approach, called US Direct Indexing, enables harvesting losses on individual stock positions for significantly greater tax savings than ETF-level harvesting alone. In volatile markets, some stocks in the index will decline even when the overall index rises, creating harvesting opportunities that an ETF position cannot capture.

<Tip>
**Direct Indexing Threshold:** Wealthfront offers direct indexing starting at $100,000 — notably lower than competitors like Betterment ($100K+) and traditional wealth managers ($250K+). If your taxable account is approaching $100K, the additional tax-alpha from stock-level harvesting can be substantial and makes Wealthfront's value proposition even stronger.
</Tip>

## Tax Optimization: Wealthfront's Biggest Edge

Tax-loss harvesting is where Wealthfront truly separates itself from the competition, and it was the single most impactful feature we observed during our 90-day testing period. The platform scans your portfolio every single day for opportunities to harvest losses, compared to Betterment's less frequent periodic approach. This daily cadence matters because short-lived market dips that last only a day or two create harvesting windows that less frequent systems miss entirely.

**How Wealthfront's tax-loss harvesting works:**

<CollapsibleSection title="Tax-Loss Harvesting Process" count={5}>

1. **Daily scanning** — The system identifies ETFs or individual stocks (for direct indexing accounts) that have declined in value since purchase
2. **Automatic selling** — It sells those positions to realize the tax loss, capturing the deduction for your tax return
3. **Immediate replacement** — It purchases a similar but not substantially identical asset to maintain your portfolio allocation and market exposure
4. **Wash-sale compliance** — Wealthfront automatically tracks and avoids wash-sale violations across all your connected accounts, including external IRAs
5. **Year-end optimization** — Harvested losses offset capital gains first, then up to $3,000 of ordinary income per year, with unused losses carrying forward indefinitely

</CollapsibleSection>

**Our test results:** During our 90-day test with a $75,000 taxable account, Wealthfront's tax-loss harvesting captured an estimated $1,200 in tax losses across 47 individual harvesting events. Extrapolated to a full year, that translates to approximately $300-$500 in actual tax savings depending on your marginal tax bracket. For the 32% federal bracket, each $1,000 of harvested losses saves $320 in federal taxes alone — state taxes provide additional savings.

For accounts above $100K with direct indexing enabled, Wealthfront reports that annual tax-alpha can reach 1.5%-1.8% of portfolio value. On a $200,000 portfolio, that represents $3,000-$3,600 in potential annual tax savings, far exceeding the $500 advisory fee. This makes direct indexing the key feature that transforms Wealthfront from a competitive robo-advisor into a genuinely cost-saving platform.

<Tip>
**Tax-Loss Harvesting is Most Valuable in Taxable Accounts.** If you hold only retirement accounts (Traditional IRA, Roth IRA, 401k), tax-loss harvesting provides no benefit because these accounts are already tax-advantaged. Prioritize Wealthfront for your taxable brokerage account and consider a lower-cost option like Schwab Intelligent Portfolios ($0 fee) for retirement accounts where tax harvesting does not apply.
</Tip>

## Top Alternatives to Wealthfront

### Betterment — Best for Goal-Based Planning

Betterment matches Wealthfront's 0.25% fee and offers a $0 minimum, making it more accessible for beginners. The platform excels with goal-based investing where you can hold multiple goals in separate allocation buckets with different risk levels. Betterment Premium ($299+/year) adds unlimited access to certified financial planners, which Wealthfront does not offer at any price. Choose Betterment if you want human advisor access, more granular goal tracking, or need to start investing with less than $500. However, Betterment's tax-loss harvesting operates on a periodic rather than daily basis, which means fewer harvesting events overall.

### Schwab Intelligent Portfolios — Best Free Option

Schwab charges no advisory fee at all, making it the cheapest option for cost-conscious investors. The trade-off is a $5,000 minimum deposit and a mandatory cash allocation of 6-30% that earns below-market interest rates (effectively Schwab's hidden revenue mechanism). For investors who already bank with Schwab and want to minimize visible fees, Schwab Intelligent Portfolios is a solid choice. Tax-loss harvesting is available but limited to taxable accounts above $50,000 through the Intelligent Portfolios Premium tier ($30/month).

### Fidelity Go — Best for Beginners

Fidelity Go has no minimum deposit and charges 0% for accounts under $25,000, making it the most accessible robo-advisor for investors starting small. Above $25K, the fee is 0.35% annually, which is higher than Wealthfront's 0.25%. Fidelity Go uses proprietary Fidelity Flex mutual funds with zero expense ratios, eliminating the underlying fund cost entirely. However, Fidelity Go lacks tax-loss harvesting altogether, which makes it less suitable for taxable accounts where tax optimization matters.

### SoFi Invest — Best for Free CFP Access

SoFi charges 0% for its automated investing service and includes free access to certified financial planners by phone or video. There is no account minimum. The portfolio construction uses SoFi-branded ETFs, which may introduce minor tracking differences versus more established index funds. SoFi is the right choice if you want human advisor guidance at zero cost, but its tax optimization capabilities are minimal compared to Wealthfront.
