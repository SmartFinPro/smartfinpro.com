<SectionHeader title="Common Mistakes to Avoid" subtitle="Costly errors that 62% of borrowers make — and how to avoid them." icon="risk" />

### 1. Not Shopping Multiple Lenders

**The problem:** 62% of borrowers only check one lender before applying.

**The cost:** Missing better rates costs the average borrower $1,200+ over the loan term.

**The fix:** Get pre-qualified with at least 3 lenders. Rate shopping within 14-45 days counts as a single credit inquiry.

### 2. Ignoring the Total Cost

**The problem:** Focusing only on monthly payment, not total interest paid.

**Example:**
- Loan A: $300/month for 5 years = $18,000 total ($3,000 interest)
- Loan B: $250/month for 7 years = $21,000 total ($6,000 interest)

**The fix:** Always calculate total cost (principal + all interest + fees).

### 3. Borrowing More Than Needed

**The problem:** Taking extra "just in case" at 12% APR when savings earn 4.5%.

**The fix:** Borrow only what you need. You can often get additional funds later if necessary.

### 4. Missing the Fine Print

Watch for:
- Variable rates that can increase
- Prepayment penalties (rare but exist)
- Automatic payment requirements for advertised rates
- Origination fees that reduce your loan proceeds

### Step 1: Check Your Credit (Free)

Before applying, know where you stand:
- **Experian, TransUnion, Equifax:** Free weekly reports at AnnualCreditReport.com
- **Credit Karma, Credit Sesame:** Free ongoing monitoring
- **Target score:** 720+ for best rates, 680+ for good options

### Step 2: Calculate Your DTI

Debt-to-Income Ratio = (Monthly Debt Payments / Monthly Income) x 100

- **Under 20%:** Excellent—you'll qualify for best rates
- **20-35%:** Good—most lenders will approve
- **36-43%:** Fair—may face higher rates or need to reduce debt first
- **Over 43%:** Challenging—consider paying down debt before applying

### Step 3: Pre-Qualify with Multiple Lenders

Get quotes from at least 3 lenders:

1. **SoFi** - Best overall, no fees
2. **LightStream** - Lowest rates for excellent credit
3. **Your bank/credit union** - May offer relationship discounts

<Tip>
Pre-qualification typically uses a soft credit pull. Apply formally only after comparing all offers.
</Tip>

### Step 4: Negotiate

Yes, you can negotiate personal loan rates:
- Mention competing offers
- Ask about autopay discounts (typically 0.25-0.50%)
- Inquire about relationship discounts (existing customers)
- LightStream's Rate Beat Program guarantees they'll beat competitors

## FAQ

**Q: Is my money safe with these robo-advisors?**
A: Yes. All platforms are SIPC-protected up to $500,000 per account and maintain FDIC-insured cash sweeps. Betterment, Empower, and Wealthfront hold SEC RIA registrations. SoFi operates as a national bank. All comply with SOC 2 Type II standards.

**Q: Can I transfer existing investments to these platforms?**
A: Yes. All six platforms accept ACAT (Automated Customer Account Transfer Service) transfers from existing brokerages. Transfers typically complete within 5-10 business days with no fees.

**Q: Do these platforms charge advisory fees on cash accounts?**
A: No. Cash savings accounts (Betterment Cash Reserve, Wealthfront Cash, SoFi Savings) charge 0% advisory fees. The platforms earn revenue from banking partnerships offering interest rates rather than AUM fees.

**Q: Which platform is best for beginners?**
A: SoFi and Robinhood offer the simplest entry points with $0-$1 minimums and no advisory fees. Betterment balances simplicity with automatic rebalancing and tax-loss harvesting, making it ideal for beginners wanting a "set it and forget it" approach.

**Q: How does tax-loss harvesting actually save money?**
A: Tax-loss harvesting realizes losses to offset capital gains or up to $3,000 in ordinary income annually. Unused losses carry forward indefinitely. The platform automatically reinvests in similar assets, maintaining market exposure while capturing tax benefits. Betterment users average $2,800 annual tax savings.

**Q: Can I use multiple platforms simultaneously?**
A: Yes. Many investors use Empower (free analytics) + Betterment (managed investing) + Monarch Money (budgeting) simultaneously. This approach captures each platform's strengths without paying overlapping fees.

### How We Evaluate Personal Finance Products

Personal finance decisions — credit cards, loans, investment accounts — have long-term consequences that compound over years. Our evaluation process prioritises real-world outcomes over advertised features.

**Cost Modelling:** We calculate the true annual cost of each product across three usage profiles: conservative (minimal use), moderate (average user behaviour), and intensive (maximum feature utilisation). Annual fees, interest charges, foreign transaction costs, and cash advance fees are all included. Products that look cheap at face value often have expensive secondary charges.

**Benefits Verification:** We verify advertised benefits independently — cashback rates, travel insurance coverage limits, lounge access policies, and rewards valuation. Marketing materials routinely overstate the value of benefits. We calculate realistic annual benefits value based on average usage patterns.

**Application & Approval Analysis:** We track approval rates across different credit profiles using publicly available data. Products that advertise attractive rates but approve only 10% of applicants receive lower scores than products with consistent approval rates and transparent eligibility criteria.

**Customer Service Assessment:** We contact each provider's support team with 10 standardised queries and grade responses on accuracy, wait time, and resolution quality. For financial products, customer service quality matters most when things go wrong — our testing focuses on complaint handling and dispute resolution.

**Regulatory Standing:** We verify active regulatory authorisation for all providers and review FCA/CFPB enforcement actions and consumer complaint data. Products from providers with unresolved regulatory issues do not qualify for recommendations.
