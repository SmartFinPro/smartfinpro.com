### How Lenders Set Your Rate

Your personal loan APR depends on several factors:

| Factor | Impact on Rate | How to Improve |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **Credit Score** | Primary factor (up to 15% APR difference) | Pay bills on time, reduce utilization |
| **Income** | Higher income = lower risk = better rates | Document all income sources |
| **Debt-to-Income** | DTI above 40% hurts rates | Pay down existing debt first |
| **Loan Amount** | Very small or large loans may have higher rates | Stay in lender's "sweet spot" |
| **Loan Purpose** | Debt consolidation often gets best rates | Be specific about loan use |

<SectionHeader title="Common Mistakes to Avoid" subtitle="Costly errors that 62% of borrowers make — and how to avoid them." icon="risk" />

### 1. Not Shopping Multiple Lenders

**The problem:** 62% of borrowers only check one lender before applying.

**The cost:** Missing better rates costs the average borrower $1,200+ over the loan term.

**The fix:** Get pre-qualified with at least 3 lenders. Rate shopping within 14-45 days counts as a single credit inquiry.

### 2. Ignoring the Total Cost

**The problem:** Focusing only on monthly payment, not total interest paid.

**Example:**
- Loan A: $300/month for 5 years = $18,000 total ($3,000 interest)
- Loan B: $250/month for 7 years = $21,000 total ($6,000 interest)

**The fix:** Always calculate total cost (principal + all interest + fees).

### 3. Borrowing More Than Needed

**The problem:** Taking extra "just in case" at 12% APR when savings earn 4.5%.

**The fix:** Borrow only what you need. You can often get additional funds later if necessary.

### 4. Missing the Fine Print

Watch for:
- Variable rates that can increase
- Prepayment penalties (rare but exist)
- Automatic payment requirements for advertised rates
- Origination fees that reduce your loan proceeds

<SectionHeader title="How to Get the Best Rate" subtitle="Step-by-step guide to securing the lowest possible APR." icon="guide" />

### Step 1: Check Your Credit (Free)

Before applying, know where you stand:
- **Experian, TransUnion, Equifax:** Free weekly reports at AnnualCreditReport.com
- **Credit Karma, Credit Sesame:** Free ongoing monitoring
- **Target score:** 720+ for best rates, 680+ for good options

### Step 2: Calculate Your DTI

Debt-to-Income Ratio = (Monthly Debt Payments / Monthly Income) x 100

- **Under 20%:** Excellent—you'll qualify for best rates
- **20-35%:** Good—most lenders will approve
- **36-43%:** Fair—may face higher rates or need to reduce debt first
- **Over 43%:** Challenging—consider paying down debt before applying

### Step 3: Pre-Qualify with Multiple Lenders

Get quotes from at least 3 lenders:

1. **SoFi** - Best overall, no fees
2. **LightStream** - Lowest rates for excellent credit
3. **Your bank/credit union** - May offer relationship discounts

<Tip>
Pre-qualification typically uses a soft credit pull. Apply formally only after comparing all offers.
</Tip>

### Step 4: Negotiate

Yes, you can negotiate personal loan rates:
- Mention competing offers
- Ask about autopay discounts (typically 0.25-0.50%)
- Inquire about relationship discounts (existing customers)
- LightStream's Rate Beat Program guarantees they'll beat competitors
