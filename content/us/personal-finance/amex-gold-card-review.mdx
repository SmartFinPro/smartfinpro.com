### What Counts as "Dining"

Amex's dining category is broadly defined and includes:
- Sit-down restaurants
- Fast food and takeout
- Coffee shops (Starbucks, etc.)
- Food delivery apps (DoorDash, Grubhub)
- Bars and nightclubs

<Info>
The dining 4x multiplier is uncapped—whether you spend $200 or $2,000 per month on restaurants, you always earn 4x points. This is rare among premium cards.
</Info>

## Annual Credits

The Amex Gold comes with $240 in annual statement credits that effectively reduce the annual fee:

### $120 Dining Credit ($10/month)

- **$10 monthly credit** at select restaurants
- Auto-applied when you use your card at participating partners
- Partners include: Grubhub, The Cheesecake Factory, Goldbelly, Wine.com, and select Resy restaurants
- **Use it or lose it:** Monthly credits do not roll over

### $120 Uber Cash ($10/month)

- **$10 monthly Uber Cash** added automatically
- Works for Uber rides and Uber Eats
- **December bonus:** $20 Uber Cash instead of $10
- Credits expire if not used within the month

<Warning>
Both credits are "use it or lose it" each month. If you don't use Uber or the participating dining partners, these credits have zero value for you. Factor this into your fee calculation.
</Warning>

### Effective Annual Fee Calculation

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Annual fee | -$325 |
| Dining credit (if fully used) | +$120 |
| Uber Cash (if fully used) | +$120 |
| **Effective fee** | **$85** |

## Our Verdict

After 12 months of daily use, the Amex Gold Card earns **4.7 out of 5 stars**.

**Bottom line:** The Amex Gold is the undisputed champion for dining rewards. If you spend $500+ monthly at restaurants and use the annual credits, the card delivers $300-500+ in net annual value. The Membership Rewards ecosystem with 20+ transfer partners adds strategic depth that budget cards simply cannot match. However, if you don't dine out frequently or can't use the monthly credits, the $325 fee may not justify itself.

**Final Rating: 4.7/5**
