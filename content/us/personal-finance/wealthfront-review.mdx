## Key Features for US Investors

### 1. Daily Tax-Loss Harvesting

Wealthfront's proprietary algorithm continuously monitors your portfolio — even during intraday trading — for positions trading below cost basis. When a harvesting opportunity is identified, the platform automatically sells the declining position and immediately reinvests in a similar but not substantially identical security to maintain your target allocation. Unlike Betterment's quarterly-plus-dividend harvesting approach, Wealthfront's daily monitoring captures more opportunities during volatile trading sessions. According to Wealthfront's published data, daily harvesting can realize 1.5-2.5% per year in additional after-tax returns on taxable accounts compared to quarterly approaches.

The mechanics are straightforward but the impact compounds significantly over time. Consider a $200,000 taxable portfolio in a year with normal market volatility: Wealthfront's daily approach might harvest $6,000-$8,000 in losses across dozens of small transactions throughout the year, while a quarterly approach might capture only $3,000-$4,000 in the same period. At a 32% federal tax rate plus state taxes, that difference translates to $1,000-$2,000 in additional annual tax savings — money that stays invested and compounds over decades.

### 2. Direct Indexing ($100K+)

Wealthfront's US Direct Indexing replaces broad market ETFs with custom-selected individual stocks mirroring index composition. Instead of owning a single Nasdaq 100 ETF, you own the 100 individual Nasdaq stocks proportionally weighted to match the index. This approach enables tax-loss harvesting at the individual stock level rather than the ETF level, custom sector tilts based on personal conviction or existing exposure, tax-aware rebalancing that sells loss-making positions first, and individual stock cost basis tracking for precise tax optimization. The average tax alpha from direct indexing ranges from 2-4% annually on accounts over $500,000, making it the single most powerful feature for high-net-worth investors.

## How Wealthfront Makes Money

Understanding Wealthfront's revenue model helps you anticipate where the platform's incentives align with yours as an investor, and where potential conflicts may arise.

Wealthfront generates revenue through **three primary channels** in the United States:

1. **Advisory fees** — The 0.25% annual management fee on invested assets is Wealthfront's core revenue stream, calculated daily and deducted monthly from your portfolio. On $70 billion in AUM, this generates approximately $175 million in annual advisory fee revenue. Unlike traditional advisors who charge 1-2%, Wealthfront's lower fee is sustained by automation and scale.

2. **Cash account net interest margin** — Wealthfront Cash sweeps customer deposits to partner banks, earning interest income on the spread between the rate paid to partner banks and the 5.00% APY paid to customers. While the exact margin is not disclosed, this is a material revenue stream given the high cash balances many investors maintain alongside their investment accounts.

3. **Securities lending** — Wealthfront may lend securities held in customer accounts to short sellers and other market participants, earning a fee in the process. This is standard practice among custodians and brokerages, and Wealthfront passes through a portion of lending revenue to offset the advisory fee.

This model means Wealthfront is primarily incentivized to grow your assets under management — more assets mean more advisory fee revenue. Unlike commission-based brokers who benefit from frequent trading, Wealthfront's flat percentage fee aligns its interests with long-term portfolio growth. The 0.25% fee also means Wealthfront does not benefit from churning your portfolio, which is a common conflict of interest with traditional advisors.

### Choose Betterment If...

You are a **beginning investor with under $10,000** and want a truly free platform. SoFi charges $0 in advisory fees, requires only a $1 minimum, and includes access to human financial planners. ### Choose a Traditional Financial Advisor If...

You need **personalized advice** on complex financial situations such as estate planning, business succession, charitable giving strategies, or navigating divorce-related asset division. Traditional RIAs charge 0.5-2% annually (typically $5,000-$10,000+ minimum) but provide tailored guidance that no algorithm can replicate. Many high-net-worth investors use a hybrid approach — Wealthfront for tax-optimized investing and a traditional advisor for strategic financial planning that goes beyond what Path can model.
