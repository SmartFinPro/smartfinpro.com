## US Pricing & Premium Structure 2026

APMEX's pricing follows the industry-standard model: display the current spot price, add a premium reflecting product rarity, demand, and dealer margin, then calculate the total. Understanding how premiums work is essential before purchasing — the same underlying ounce of gold can carry vastly different premiums depending on whether it is minted as a government coin, a private refinery bar, or a rare collectible. Payment method also impacts final price significantly, with bank wire offering the lowest cost and credit cards adding a 4% surcharge that can represent hundreds of dollars on larger orders. Current pricing is sourced from the [official APMEX website](https://www.apmex.com/):

| Product Type | Price Range | Premium Over Spot | Payment Method Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 oz American Gold Eagle** | $2,750-$3,000 | +$75-$150 | Wire (lowest), CC (+4%) |
| **1 oz Gold Bullion Bar** | $2,750-$2,900 | +$30-$60 | Wire (lowest), CC (+4%) |
| **1 oz Canadian Maple Leaf** | $2,750-$2,900 | +$40-$80 | Wire (lowest), CC (+4%) |
| **PAMP Suisse Gold Bar** | $2,750-$2,850 | +$20-$50 | Wire (lowest), CC (+4%) |
| **Silver (1 oz coin)** | $25-$35 | +$2-$8 | Wire (lowest), CC (+4%) |
| **Platinum (1 oz)** | $900-$1,200 | +$30-$100 | Wire (lowest), CC (+4%) |

Gold Eagle premiums ($75-$150) reflect US Mint scarcity value and collector demand — these coins carry legal tender status and are widely recognized, which commands a premium over generic bullion. Generic 1 oz gold bars from refineries like PAMP Suisse and Valcambi carry lower premiums ($20-$60) because they lack the numismatic component and are valued purely for metal content. Silver coins typically carry higher percentage premiums relative to spot price than gold products, reflecting the lower absolute price point and proportionally higher minting and distribution costs.

Gold tubes (typically 20 coins) offer similar per-unit discounts that compound significantly for investors building positions over time.

This pricing structure appeals to serious precious metals investors who purchase on regular schedules — monthly or quarterly buyers accumulating larger positions benefit most from Monster Box economics. The program effectively rewards larger order sizes while maintaining the liquidity advantage of individual coins that can be sold one at a time during portfolio rebalancing.

## APMEX Features Deep-Dive

### Segregated Allocated Storage at Citadel Vaults

APMEX Storage partners with Citadel Vaults (a Brinks-certified facility) to provide professional segregated allocated storage for investors who prefer institutional-grade custody over personal storage. Segregated allocated means your specific metals are individually identified, physically separated from other customers' holdings, and stored in your own vault compartment — unlike pooled or unallocated storage where your claim is against a general inventory. Storage fees run approximately 0.12% annually based on the current market value of stored metals, with a minimum charge of $8 per month. Annual audits and insurance are included in the storage fee, with no additional charges for routine inspections.

This option suits investors who hold significant precious metals positions (typically $10,000+) and are concerned about the security vulnerabilities of home storage. A home safe, while convenient, exposes investors to theft risk, fire damage, and insurance complications — standard homeowner policies typically limit coverage for precious metals to $1,000-$2,500 unless a specific rider is purchased. Citadel Vaults provides comprehensive insurance covering theft, fire, and natural disasters, with institutional-grade security systems that exceed what any residential installation can achieve. Withdrawal is straightforward — request your metals online and Citadel ships them to your address within 2-3 business days, with shipping insurance included.

### Competitive Buyback Program

APMEX's buyback program enables customers to sell previously purchased metals back to the dealer at competitive prices, supporting portfolio rebalancing and profit-taking strategies. Buyback prices typically reflect current spot prices minus a modest dealer spread of 0.5-2%, depending on the product — standard bullion products like American Gold Eagles and PAMP Suisse bars command tighter spreads (0.5-1%) than numismatic or collectible items (1-2%). This program eliminates the need to find individual buyers or navigate auction platforms when liquidating precious metals positions.

For traders building and actively managing precious metals allocations, the buyback program provides genuine liquidity. During the 2024-2025 gold rally, when spot prices climbed from $2,000 to above $2,700, many APMEX customers used the buyback program to realize gains on portions of their holdings. The process is straightforward: log into your account, select the items to sell, receive a quote locked for a limited time window, ship the metals to APMEX (insured shipping label provided), and receive payment via wire or check within 3-5 business days of receipt verification.

### Spot Deal Section

APMEX's Spot Deal section periodically features near-spot pricing on select inventory items when supply conditions permit. These deals offer products at premiums 3-5% below typical APMEX pricing, representing significant savings on individual purchases. The section updates regularly but unpredictably — inventory appears when APMEX acquires bulk positions at favorable prices and passes a portion of the savings to retail customers. Spot Deals reward frequent visitors and active traders who monitor the platform for advantageous buying opportunities.

The Spot Deal section has become increasingly popular among cost-conscious investors who want APMEX's selection breadth but seek to minimize premium costs. While the specific products available rotate constantly, common Spot Deal items include generic silver rounds, 1 oz gold bars from less-known refineries, and bulk silver lots. For investors willing to be flexible on exact product specifications, monitoring the Spot Deal section can reduce effective premiums to levels competitive with or below JM Bullion and SD Bullion.

## How APMEX Makes Money

Understanding APMEX's revenue model helps you anticipate where costs concentrate and where the dealer's incentives align with your interests as a buyer. Unlike financial advisors who may earn commissions on products you do not need, APMEX's model is straightforward — they profit from the spread between their buy price (wholesale) and sell price (retail).

APMEX generates revenue through **five primary channels** in the United States:

1. **Premiums over spot price** — the core revenue driver. APMEX purchases metals at or near spot (from mints, refineries, and other dealers) and sells to retail customers at spot-plus-premium. Premiums vary by product ($20-$150 per ounce on gold) and generate the majority of dealer revenue
2. **Buyback spreads** — when customers sell metals back to APMEX, the dealer pays spot minus a 0.5-2% spread. This two-way market generates profit on both buying and selling sides
3. **Storage fees** — the 0.12% annual fee on Citadel Vaults-stored metals provides recurring revenue. On $100 million in stored assets, this generates approximately $120,000 per year
4. **Payment method surcharges** — while the 4% credit card fee primarily covers processing costs, it likely includes modest margin. APMEX effectively incentivizes wire payment (which has lower processing costs) through price differentiation
5. **Numismatic premiums** — rare and collectible coins carry significantly higher premiums than bullion (sometimes 50-200% above spot), providing higher margins on a per-unit basis than commodity products

This model means APMEX is primarily incentivized to maximize transaction volume and encourage larger purchases — Monster Box pricing, Spot Deals, and the buyback program all serve this goal. The alignment with investor interests is generally positive: APMEX profits when you buy, and you benefit from competitive pricing driven by their $2 billion+ annual volume and purchasing power.
