## How JM Bullion Makes Money

Understanding JM Bullion's revenue model helps investors assess whether the platform's incentives align with their interests. Unlike subscription-based financial platforms that earn recurring fees regardless of customer outcomes, JM Bullion's revenue is fundamentally tied to transaction volume — meaning the company benefits when customers buy and sell actively. This alignment creates a natural incentive to maintain competitive pricing, since overpriced products reduce purchase volume.

1. **Dealer premiums above spot price** — the primary revenue source. Each product is priced at spot + premium, with premiums ranging from +$15 (PAMP Suisse bars) to +$130 (Gold Eagles). Higher-demand products carry higher premiums.

2. **Buyback spread** — when JM Bullion repurchases metals from investors at 1.5–3% below spot, the dealer spread becomes revenue upon resale at spot + premium. Active buying and selling creates a continuous margin cycle.

3. **Payment method optimization** — credit card purchases carry higher premiums than wire/crypto, reflecting processing fee recovery. The differential encourages cost-effective payment methods while generating additional margin on convenience-oriented purchases.

4. **Subscription revenue** — the JMB subscription program generates committed monthly volume at slightly reduced premiums. While per-unit margins are lower, guaranteed recurring volume provides predictable revenue and improved inventory planning.

5. **Volume-based mint and refiner pricing** — JM Bullion's $800+ million annual volume commands bulk pricing from mints and refiners, creating margin advantages that smaller dealers cannot replicate. These savings are partially passed to customers (competitive pricing) and partially retained as profit.
