## How tastyfx Makes Money

Understanding tastyfx's revenue model helps you anticipate where costs may evolve and where the platform's incentives align — or conflict — with yours as a trader.

tastyfx generates revenue through **five primary channels** in the United States:

1. **Spread revenue** — The 0.8-pip bid-ask spread on EUR/USD (and similar spreads across all 80+ pairs) is the primary revenue source. tastyfx acts as market maker, earning the spread on each trade. Higher trading volume directly increases platform revenue, which incentivizes tastyfx to encourage active trading
2. **Swap/rollover fees** — Overnight position charges generate revenue from traders holding positions past the daily rollover point (5 PM EST). Swap rates vary by currency pair and position direction, reflecting interest rate differentials plus a markup
3. **tastytrade ecosystem cross-selling** — Forex traders who discover tastyfx may open tastytrade accounts for options, stocks, and futures trading, generating additional commission and fee revenue across the broader platform ecosystem
4. **Spread widening during volatility** — While not a deliberate revenue strategy, the variable spread model means tastyfx earns higher per-trade revenue during volatile market conditions when spreads expand from 0.8 pips to 2.0-3.0+ pips
5. **Payment processing margins** — Small margins on ACH processing and bank wire transfers contribute incremental revenue, particularly on larger deposits and withdrawals

This revenue model means tastyfx is incentivized to attract active traders and maximize trade volume. The zero-commission, low-spread structure is designed to reduce barriers to trading and encourage frequency — which benefits cost-conscious traders who would trade the same volume regardless, but may encourage overtrading for less disciplined participants.
