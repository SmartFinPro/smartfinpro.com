## Key Features for US Traders

### 1. Commission-Based Pricing Model

Forex.com's commission structure fundamentally differentiates it from spread-only competitors in the US market. Rather than variable spreads expanding unpredictably during volatile news events like Non-Farm Payrolls or FOMC announcements, commissions remain fixed at $5 per 100,000 units, enabling precise cost calculation before every trade. A standard 100k EUR/USD position costs exactly $5 in commission plus the current 0.2-pip spread (approximately $2), totaling roughly $7 per trade. This transparency is invaluable for traders building algorithmic systems, analyzing cost-per-trade metrics, or optimizing position sizing models around known execution costs.

For scalpers targeting 10 to 20 pip profits on each trade, fixed commissions enable profitable small-margin trading where variable 1.5-pip or wider spreads would completely eliminate profitability. A scalper capturing 15 pips on a 100k position earns $150 gross. At Forex.com's Commission account, the $7 total cost leaves $143 net. At a spread-only broker charging 1.5 pips ($15 per trade), the net drops to $135. Across 60 monthly trades, that difference compounds to $480 per month or $5,760 annually. For serious scalpers and day traders, this pricing advantage justifies any additional complexity in understanding the split commission-plus-spread billing model.

### 2. Advanced Trader Platform

The proprietary [Advanced Trader platform](https://www.forex.com/en-us/trading-platforms/advanced-trading-platform/) distinguishes Forex.com from MT4-only competitors that dominate the US retail forex space. This web-based charting solution includes over 80 technical indicators, economic event calendars with real-time updates, and news feeds from Reuters and other premium sources delivered directly into the trading interface. Traders can apply complex analytical techniques including Elliott Wave analysis, Ichimoku cloud systems, supply-demand zone mapping, and Fibonacci extensions using advanced drawing tools and studies that rival standalone subscription platforms.

The platform supports multi-chart layouts, allowing simultaneous monitoring of multiple currency pairs and timeframes on a single screen. A day trader might display a 5-minute EUR/USD chart alongside a 1-hour GBP/USD chart, a 4-hour USD/JPY chart, and the economic calendar, making cross-pair correlation analysis seamless. Mobile apps replicate core Advanced Trader functionality, enabling trade management, price alert configuration, and position monitoring away from desktop terminals. The mobile app holds a 4.1/5 rating from approximately 800 reviews on the [Apple App Store](https://apps.apple.com/), with traders particularly appreciating real-time alerts and one-touch trading capabilities.

### 3. 80+ Currency Pairs

Forex.com provides access to 80 or more currency pairs spanning major pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY), minors (EUR/GBP, GBP/JPY, AUD/NZD), and exotics including emerging market currencies like the Mexican Peso (USD/MXN), Indian Rupee (USD/INR), and Brazilian Real (USD/BRL). This breadth enables traders pursuing forex carry trade strategies, emerging market hedging, and geographic diversification that is simply impossible at smaller brokers offering only 30 to 40 major and minor pairs. Cross-pair trading (such as EUR/GBP or AUD/NZD) often provides superior risk-reward ratios compared to dollar-centric pairs during periods of US economic uncertainty.

The exotic pair selection is particularly valuable for traders looking to diversify beyond the major currency pairs that most US retail brokers limit themselves to. If you also trade equities or options through a [trading platform](/trading/), maintaining a dedicated forex account at Forex.com gives you cleaner cost tracking and specialized execution for currency positions rather than mixing them into a multi-asset brokerage account.

### 4. MetaTrader 4 Integration

While Advanced Trader represents Forex.com's flagship platform, the firm also offers [MetaTrader 4](https://www.forex.com/en-us/trading-platforms/metatrader-4/) integration for traders preferring the industry-standard platform with its massive ecosystem of custom tools. MT4 compatibility enables algorithmic trading via Expert Advisors (EAs), custom indicator development, and comprehensive strategy backtesting against historical data. This appeals to technical traders with existing MT4-based trading systems who do not want to rebuild their automation infrastructure on a new platform.

However, it is worth noting that MT4 integration provides real-time data feeds but executes trades through Forex.com's own order matching systems rather than independent MT4 servers. This means execution quality depends on Forex.com's liquidity providers and order routing, not the MT4 infrastructure itself. For traders who rely heavily on TradingView for charting and analysis, the lack of TradingView integration at Forex.com is a meaningful gap. OANDA currently offers TradingView connectivity, which is a significant competitive advantage for chart-focused traders who have built their analytical workflows around that platform.

### 5. FIFO Compliance & Trade Execution

Forex.com implements strict FIFO (first-in-first-out) order execution, closing positions in the exact sequence they were opened as required by [NFA](https://www.nfa.futures.org/) regulations. This means US retail traders cannot maintain simultaneous long and short positions in the same currency pair, eliminating hedging as a risk management strategy. While this restriction frustrates traders accustomed to international brokers that permit hedging, it simplifies tax reporting by avoiding complex hedge-adjusted P&L calculations and protects less experienced traders from the over-leverage risks that simultaneous opposing positions can create.

The FIFO system automatically closes the oldest position first when a trader attempts to place a conflicting trade. For example, if you hold a long EUR/USD position from Monday and attempt to short EUR/USD on Wednesday, the system will first close your Monday long position before opening the new short. This requires disciplined position management and forward planning, particularly for traders running multiple strategies across different timeframes on the same currency pair.

### Leverage Structure and Margin Requirements

US forex leverage is strictly regulated by the [CFTC](https://www.cftc.gov/) and [NFA](https://www.nfa.futures.org/), and Forex.com adheres precisely to these limits. Major currency pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY, USD/CHF, USD/CAD, AUD/USD, NZD/USD, EUR/JPY, GBP/JPY) carry a maximum leverage of 50:1, requiring 2% margin. Minor and exotic pairs are limited to 20:1 leverage, requiring 5% margin. These limits apply uniformly across all US-regulated brokers, meaning Forex.com cannot offer higher leverage than competitors like OANDA or Interactive Brokers. The competitive differentiation lies entirely in execution quality, platform features, and pricing structure rather than leverage ratios.

## How Forex.com Makes Money

Understanding Forex.com's revenue model helps you anticipate where costs may evolve over time and where the platform's business incentives align or conflict with your interests as a trader. Transparency about broker revenue sources is a critical factor in long-term trust.

Forex.com generates revenue through **five primary channels** in the US market:

1. **Spread revenue (Standard accounts)** — the 1.0-pip EUR/USD spread on Standard accounts is Forex.com's primary revenue source from casual traders, generating approximately $10 per standard lot in broker revenue embedded invisibly in the bid-ask spread
2. **Fixed commissions (Commission accounts)** — the $5 per 100k commission is a transparent, predictable revenue stream that scales linearly with trading volume and incentivizes Forex.com to keep active traders on the platform
3. **Overnight financing (swap rates)** — traders holding positions past 5 PM ET pay or receive overnight financing based on interest rate differentials between the two currencies in the pair, generating net positive revenue for the broker
4. **Inactivity fees** — the $25/month charge on dormant accounts recovers the cost of maintaining unused accounts and nudges inactive traders to either resume trading or close their accounts
5. **VIP account management** — negotiated rates on VIP accounts still generate revenue through volume-based rebates from liquidity providers, even when headline rates are reduced for the trader

This model means Forex.com is financially incentivized to keep you trading actively on the Commission account, where their $5-per-trade revenue is clearly defined. The alignment is healthy for active traders: Forex.com profits from your volume, and you benefit from lower per-trade costs as your volume increases. The misalignment occurs with the inactivity fee, which penalizes traders who take breaks regardless of their historical trading volume or account balance.
