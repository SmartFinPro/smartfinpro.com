For example, a professional trader purchasing $5 million EUR/USD would use VWAP ordering to spread the purchase throughout market hours, minimizing price movement from the sudden appearance of a large order in the market.

These execution capabilities matter because market impact is a real cost for traders executing size. A 100-lot EUR/USD market order at a retail broker might slip 0.5–1 pip, costing $50–$100 in execution slippage. The same order executed via IBKR's TWAP algorithm over 30 minutes might achieve the volume-weighted average price with near-zero slippage. ## Interactive Brokers Features Deep-Dive

### Commission Structure in Detail

The tiered commission model is Interactive Brokers' primary cost differentiator, but understanding exactly how the tiers work is essential for accurate cost projection. IBKR calculates your commission tier based on your previous month's total forex trading volume. Tier one ($0.08 per 1,000 units) applies to traders executing $100+ million in monthly forex volume — a threshold accessible primarily to hedge funds and high-frequency traders. Most retail traders managing $50,000–$500,000 accounts qualify for tier three or four, paying approximately $0.10–$0.15 per 1,000 units.

To put these costs in practical terms: a trader executing 100 standard lot trades (100,000 units each) per month at tier three ($0.12 per 1,000 units) pays $12 per trade, totaling $1,200 monthly in commissions. The same trader at [Forex.com](/forex/forex-com-review) using the commission account would pay $5 per 100k lot, totaling $500 per month. At this volume, Forex.com is actually cheaper. However, the IBKR trader also benefits from lower margin rates (Benchmark + 1.5% versus typically higher rates at competitors), multi-asset integration eliminating separate platform fees, and superior execution quality that reduces slippage costs. For traders executing 200+ monthly trades or managing multi-asset portfolios, the total cost of ownership at IBKR typically falls below competitors once you account for all expenses beyond headline commissions.

### Margin Rates: Industry's Lowest Borrowing Costs

IBKR's margin rate of Benchmark + 1.5% represents the industry's lowest borrowing cost for leveraged trading positions. For context, this means if the federal funds benchmark rate is 4.5%, your effective margin rate is 6.0% — substantially below the 8–12% rates common at retail forex brokers. Traders maintaining larger accounts may negotiate rates even lower than Benchmark + 1.5%, creating additional cost advantages at scale.

The impact of lower margin rates is most visible for swing traders and position traders who hold leveraged positions for days or weeks. A trader maintaining $500,000 in leveraged EUR/USD exposure for 30 days saves roughly $500–$1,000 per month at IBKR's margin rate versus a competitor charging Benchmark + 5.0%. Over a year, that margin rate differential alone can exceed $6,000–$12,000 — more than enough to justify the platform's complexity for traders who regularly carry overnight exposure.

### NFA Leverage and FIFO Rules

All forex trading through Interactive Brokers follows [NFA](https://www.nfa.futures.org/) leverage restrictions: 50:1 on major currency pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY, USD/CHF, USD/CAD, AUD/USD, NZD/USD) and 20:1 on all minor and exotic pairs. These limits apply uniformly to all US-regulated forex brokers — IBKR cannot and does not offer higher leverage regardless of account size or trading experience.

NFA rules also prohibit hedging for US retail traders. Interactive Brokers enforces FIFO (First In, First Out) order sequencing, meaning positions are automatically closed in the order they were opened. A trader who buys 100,000 EUR/USD and later wants to sell 100,000 EUR/USD will close the original long position rather than open a new short position alongside it. This restriction eliminates certain risk management strategies used by traders in jurisdictions without FIFO requirements, and is a structural limitation that all US forex traders must accept.

## How Interactive Brokers Makes Money

Understanding IBKR's revenue model helps you anticipate where costs may change and where the firm's incentives align with yours as a trader. 2. **Net interest income** — Interest earned on customer margin balances and cash deposits generates the largest revenue share (approximately 45–50% of total). IBKR lends at Benchmark + 1.5% while earning institutional rates on deposited funds. This is the firm's dominant profit center

4. **Market data and subscription fees** — Premium data packages, research subscriptions, and advanced platform features contribute a smaller but growing revenue stream
5. **Currency conversion fees** — The 0.002% (minimum $2) fee on currency conversions within multi-currency accounts generates revenue from international portfolio management

This revenue model means IBKR is incentivized to attract high-volume traders (more commission revenue) and hold large customer cash balances (more net interest income). For traders, this alignment is generally positive — IBKR profits when you trade more, which motivates the firm to keep execution costs competitive and platform tools best-in-class.
