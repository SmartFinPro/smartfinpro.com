|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard Account** | $0 | 0.8 pips | None | $0 |
| **Active Trader Account** | $0 | 0.6 pips | $5 per lot | $0 |
| **ProRealTime Bundle** | $0 | 0.8 pips | None | $9.99/mo |
| **Demo Account** | $0 | Live spreads | None | Free |

Standard accounts feature spread-only pricing starting at 0.8 pips for EUR/USD -- competitive among US brokers but wider than some commission-based alternatives. The spread-only model works well for traders who prefer simple, transparent pricing without calculating per-trade commission impact. For a typical 1-lot EUR/USD trade (100,000 units), the 0.8-pip spread equates to approximately $8 in trading cost, which is competitive with most US retail forex brokers.

Active Trader accounts reduce spreads to 0.6 pips while adding a $5 per standard lot commission, creating a hybrid cost structure similar to [Forex.com's](https://www.forex.com/) commission account. For traders executing 50+ monthly trades with $100,000 average position sizes, the commission account typically saves 15-20% versus the spread-only alternative. The crossover point depends on your average position size and frequency -- smaller positions with lower lot counts benefit less from the reduced spread because the fixed commission becomes proportionally larger.

ProRealTime, IG Markets' advanced charting platform, requires a $9.99/month subscription separate from forex trading fees. This investment transforms the basic web interface into a professional workstation with 340+ technical indicators, automated backtesting, and algorithmic trading capabilities via ProRealTime scripts. The subscription cost is negligible compared to standalone professional charting platforms that typically charge $100+/month for comparable functionality. Traders who rely heavily on technical analysis, pattern recognition, and strategy backtesting will find this subscription delivers outsized value.

## How IG Markets Makes Money

Understanding IG Markets' revenue model helps you anticipate where costs may increase and where the platform's incentives align -- or conflict -- with yours as a trader. Transparency about broker revenue streams is an underappreciated factor in platform selection because it reveals the financial dynamics that drive pricing decisions and platform development priorities.

IG Markets US generates revenue through **five primary channels**:

1. **Bid-ask spreads** -- The difference between the buy and sell price on each currency pair is IG Markets' primary revenue source. On Standard accounts (0.8-pip EUR/USD), the entire spread is revenue. On Active Trader accounts, the reduced 0.6-pip spread is supplemented by the $5/lot commission
2. **Per-trade commissions** -- Active Trader accounts generate $5 per standard lot commission in addition to the reduced spread, creating a predictable revenue stream that benefits both the broker and high-volume traders through lower effective spreads
3. **Overnight financing (swap rates)** -- Positions held past 5pm ET incur tom-next financing charges that generate income for the broker. The rate varies by currency pair and interest rate differential, with exotic pairs carrying higher financing costs
4. **ProRealTime subscriptions** -- The $9.99/month subscription for advanced charting creates a recurring SaaS-like revenue stream from traders who value professional analytical tools
5. **Spread widening during volatility** -- While not a deliberate revenue strategy, wider spreads during volatile market events (NFP, CPI, FOMC) generate higher per-trade revenue. IG Markets does not guarantee fixed spreads

This model means IG Markets is incentivised to encourage higher trading volume and to convert Standard account holders to Active Trader accounts where the commission creates a more predictable revenue stream. The ProRealTime subscription aligns IG Markets' interests with providing excellent analytical tools -- better tools drive more engaged trading.
