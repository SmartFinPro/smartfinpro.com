## US Forex Trading: Regulation, Leverage & What Makes It Different {#forex-overview}

The [National Futures Association (NFA)](https://www.nfa.futures.org/) and [Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC)](https://www.cftc.gov/) require all US forex brokers to maintain full registration, pass annual financial audits, hold specified net capital reserves, and segregate customer funds in separate bank accounts. These rules were established after widespread retail trader losses from unregulated offshore platforms in the early 2000s, and they shape every aspect of how US forex trading operates today.

[NFA Rule 2-43(b)](https://www.nfa.futures.org/rulebook/rules.aspx?Section=4&RuleID=RULE%202-43) caps leverage at 50:1 for major pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY) and 20:1 for minors and exotics. Unlike international brokers offering 100:1 or 500:1, US regulations restrict leverage to prevent catastrophic losses that frequently wipe out overleveraged accounts. The same rule prohibits hedging -- holding simultaneous long and short positions in the same pair -- and enforces FIFO (first-in-first-out) execution. These constraints limit strategies available internationally, but they also mean US brokers operate under the most protective framework available to retail traders.

US forex accounts do not receive [SIPC](https://www.sipc.org/) protection -- the $500,000 insurance covering stock accounts explicitly excludes forex. Brokers segregate funds per NFA regulations, and some enhance security through private insurance (OANDA via Lloyd's of London) or excess capital reserves (Interactive Brokers holds capital well above regulatory minimums). However, there is no federal backstop for forex losses, making broker solvency and CFTC/NFA compliance your primary safeguards. Always verify registration at [NFA BASIC](https://www.nfa.futures.org/basicnet/) before depositing funds.

<Warning>
**US forex accounts lack SIPC insurance.** Unlike stock brokerage accounts protected up to $500,000, forex deposits have no federal insurance mechanism. Broker solvency and CFTC/NFA registration are your primary safeguards. Verify registration at [NFA BASIC](https://www.nfa.futures.org/basicnet/) before funding any account.
</Warning>
