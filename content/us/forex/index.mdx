## The US Forex Landscape: Regulation, Leverage & FIFO {#us-forex-landscape}

All featured brokers in this guide maintain full CFTC/NFA registration, a non-negotiable requirement for legal US forex trading. The regulatory framework governing American forex markets is among the strictest in the world, and for good reason -- it was designed in the aftermath of numerous retail trader losses caused by unregulated offshore platforms in the early 2000s. The [NFA Rule 2-43(b)](https://www.nfa.futures.org/rulebook/rules.aspx?Section=4&RuleID=RULE%202-43) establishes maximum leverage at 50:1 for major currency pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY) and 20:1 for minor pairs and exotics. Unlike international brokers offering 100:1 or even 500:1 leverage, US regulations intentionally restrict leverage to reduce the catastrophic losses that frequently wipe out overleveraged retail accounts.

US forex accounts do not receive SIPC protection -- the Securities Investor Protection Corporation covers stocks and options but explicitly excludes forex accounts. Brokers maintain segregated customer funds in separate bank accounts as required by NFA regulations, but this is an operational safeguard rather than a regulatory insurance mechanism comparable to the FDIC or SIPC. Several major US brokers enhance security through private insurance solutions or maintaining capital reserves well above regulatory minimums. [OANDA](https://www.oanda.com/) maintains supplemental insurance through Lloyd's of London, while [Interactive Brokers](https://www.interactivebrokers.com/) holds significantly larger excess capital above CFTC requirements, providing additional layers of protection that vary by firm.

The FIFO (first-in-first-out) rule prohibits US retail traders from holding both long and short positions in the same currency pair simultaneously -- hedging is explicitly prohibited under US law. This contrasts sharply with international forex markets where hedging is a common risk management technique used by both retail and institutional traders. Additionally, all six brokers discussed in this guide offer variable spreads, as the regulatory environment discourages fixed-spread models that create conflicts of interest. These constraints fundamentally shape how successful US forex traders structure their strategies, and understanding them before choosing a broker prevents frustration and costly mistakes down the road.

<Warning>
**US forex accounts lack SIPC insurance.** Unlike stock brokerage accounts protected up to $500,000, forex deposits have no federal insurance mechanism. Broker solvency and CFTC/NFA registration are your primary safeguards. Always verify your broker's registration at [NFA BASIC](https://www.nfa.futures.org/basicnet/).
</Warning>

## Security & Investor Protection {#security}

Regulatory oversight through the CFTC and NFA provides the primary security mechanism for US forex trading. [NFA registration](https://www.nfa.futures.org/registration-membership/index.html) requires brokers to pass annual financial audits, maintain specified net capital requirements, and segregate customer funds in separate bank accounts held at qualifying depository institutions. However, unlike stocks protected by [SIPC](https://www.sipc.org/) up to $500,000, forex accounts lack any federal insurance mechanism, making the financial strength and regulatory compliance of your chosen broker critically important.

Two-factor authentication (2FA) is standard across all six brokers featured in this guide. Fund transfers via ACH (automated clearing house) and bank wire are encrypted and settled through federal banking networks with standard fraud protections. OANDA maintains supplemental insurance through Lloyd's of London, while Interactive Brokers holds excess capital significantly above regulatory minimums, providing additional layers of protection that vary by firm. Cryptocurrency deposits, offered by some international competitors, are not available through CFTC-regulated US brokers -- all funding must flow through traditional banking channels, which adds processing time but eliminates the volatility risk associated with crypto-funded trading accounts.
