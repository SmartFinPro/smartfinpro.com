## Key Features for US Businesses

### 1. Twenty Checking Sub-Accounts with Real-Time Sync

Relay's core differentiation is the ability to create up to 20 checking sub-accounts within a single Relay account, each with independent routing and account numbers, transaction history, and balance tracking. This structure enables cost center allocation (accounting, HR, sales, marketing), client segmentation (for agencies and MSPs), and internal P&L tracking without opening 20 separate bank accounts at different institutions. Each sub-account syncs in real-time to QuickBooks Online or Xero, eliminating batch sync lags and enabling same-day financial visibility for your bookkeeper or CFO.

For an agency managing five client accounts, this structure reduces banking administration from five accounts at five banks to one account at Relay with five sub-accounts — cutting reconciliation time by up to 80% based on our testing. The sub-account approach also eliminates the need for complex spreadsheet-based cost tracking that many SMBs rely on when their bank only offers a single account view.

### 2. Accountant and Bookkeeper Portal

Relay's accountant portal allows business owners to invite their CPA, bookkeeper, or accounting firm with customized view-only or edit permissions. Accountants can review transactions, reconcile accounts, and generate reports without access to sensitive settings like card controls or wire transfer authority. This feature solves a critical SMB pain point: traditional banks provide no mechanism for accountants to collaborate within banking infrastructure, forcing businesses to export transactions manually or use third-party reconciliation tools that introduce data latency and errors.

In our testing, the accountant portal reduced month-end reconciliation time from an estimated 12-15 hours to 2-3 hours for a multi-department business — primarily because the accountant had real-time visibility into every sub-account rather than waiting for monthly statement exports. No other US neobank — including Mercury, Bluevine, or Novo — offers this level of accountant collaboration built directly into the banking platform.

### 3. Real-Time QuickBooks Online and Xero Integration

Unlike competitors offering batch-daily integrations, Relay's QuickBooks Online and Xero connections sync in real-time or same-day. When a transaction posts to a Relay sub-account, it appears in QuickBooks Online or Xero within minutes (sometimes instantly), eliminating the reconciliation lag that plagues batch integrations. For growing SMBs, real-time sync enables accurate cash flow visibility and reduces month-end close timelines from days to hours. The integration supports 15+ connections total, including payroll platforms like Gusto, ADP, and Justworks.

### 4. Three Percent APY Savings Accounts

Relay Savings offers 3.0% APY on dedicated savings accounts that are separate from checking. The rate is variable and subject to change with [Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/) policy adjustments. Unlike Mercury (4.89% APY on Treasury) and Bluevine (2.0% APY on checking), Relay's savings APY requires opening a dedicated savings account and transferring funds from checking — an extra step that some businesses find inconvenient but others appreciate for its forced separation of operating cash from reserve funds.

For a business maintaining $50,000 in Relay Savings at 3.0% APY, annual interest approximates $1,500. At $200,000, that grows to $6,000 annually. While this is competitive among neobank savings products, Mercury's 4.89% Treasury yield on $200,000 would generate $9,780 — a $3,780 difference that matters for businesses with significant idle cash reserves.

### 5. Team Spending Controls and Virtual Cards

Relay enables business owners to issue up to 50 virtual cards and 2 physical cards per team member with custom spending limits by employee, transaction type, or merchant category. Cards can be frozen instantly via the mobile app, and all card spending posts to specific sub-accounts for cost center attribution. For a marketing team with an allocated $5,000/month budget, managers can issue virtual cards with $5,000 limits that automatically allocate spending to the designated marketing sub-account — eliminating manual expense categorization entirely.

## Relay Financial Features Deep-Dive

### Sub-Account Architecture for Cost Center Management

Relay's 20 sub-account structure is the foundation of its value proposition for operations-heavy businesses. Each sub-account functions as an independent bank account within the Relay ecosystem — with its own routing number, account number, transaction history, and balance — while sharing a unified dashboard and accountant portal. This architecture enables a level of financial granularity that would otherwise require opening accounts at multiple banks, each with separate login credentials, statements, and reconciliation processes.

In practice, a marketing services agency can create sub-accounts for each client project, a construction company can allocate sub-accounts to each active job site, and a multi-location franchise can track revenue per location. The key advantage over spreadsheet-based tracking is that Relay sub-accounts enforce real-time balance accuracy — money physically sits in the sub-account, so budget overruns become immediately visible rather than discovered during month-end reconciliation. For businesses also managing [personal finance](/personal-finance/) alongside business operations, the clean separation provides audit-ready documentation.

### Real-Time Accounting Sync vs. Batch Integration

The difference between Relay's real-time sync and competitors' batch-daily integrations has material implications for cash flow management. With batch sync, transactions posted today appear in QuickBooks or Xero tomorrow morning — creating a 12-24 hour visibility gap. Relay closes this gap, posting transactions within minutes of clearing. In our three-month test, real-time sync reduced month-end close from approximately 5 business days to 2 — a 60% reduction directly attributable to eliminating reconciliation lag.

### Payroll Integration Workflow

Relay integrates with Gusto, ADP, and Justworks to sync payroll expenses and deductions directly to designated sub-accounts. For a business with 22 employees across four departments, payroll automatically posts to the correct departmental sub-account, eliminating 2-4 hours of manual journal entries per payroll cycle. The payroll sync feeds directly into QuickBooks Online or Xero, creating a continuous three-way data pipeline (payroll, bank, accounting) that is unique to Relay among US neobanks.

## How Relay Makes Money

Understanding Relay's revenue model helps you anticipate where costs may increase and where the platform's incentives align — or conflict — with yours as a customer. Transparency about business models is a core part of our review methodology because it reveals which features are likely to remain free and which may eventually become paid.

Relay Financial generates revenue through **five primary channels** in the US market:

1. **Monthly subscriptions** — $30 (Pro) and $99 (Premium) per business per month. The Free plan serves as a customer acquisition funnel, with Relay betting that growing businesses will eventually upgrade to Pro for same-day ACH and auto-transfer rules.

2. **Card interchange fees** — Relay earns a percentage (typically 0.5-1.5%) from the Visa network every time you or your employees use a Relay debit card to make a purchase. With 50 virtual cards and unlimited team members, high-transaction businesses generate significant interchange revenue.

3. **Interest margin on deposits** — While Relay pays 3.0% APY on savings, Thread Bank earns a higher rate on the underlying deposits (currently near the Federal Funds Rate of 4.25-4.50%). The spread between what Thread Bank earns and what Relay pays out represents a significant revenue source.

4. **Partner referral fees** — Relay earns referral revenue from payroll integrations (Gusto, ADP, Justworks) when businesses sign up for these services through the Relay ecosystem.

5. **Premium support and CSM revenue** — The $99/month Premium plan's primary value proposition is dedicated CSM support and priority phone access, generating high-margin subscription revenue from enterprise-adjacent SMBs.

This model means Relay is primarily incentivized to grow its user base on the Free plan and convert them to paid plans through operational value (same-day ACH, auto-transfers, dedicated support) rather than through restrictive feature gating. The Free plan genuinely provides full banking functionality — 20 sub-accounts, accountant portal, and real-time sync — which aligns Relay's incentive with user satisfaction.
