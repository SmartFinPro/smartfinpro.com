## Key Features for US Businesses

### 1. Instant Stripe Payouts via Novo Boost

Novo Boost is the platform's signature feature and the primary reason e-commerce sellers choose Novo over competitors. The feature accelerates Stripe merchant payouts from the standard 2-day ACH settlement to within 5-15 minutes. For e-commerce sellers, SaaS companies, and digital service providers operating on tight cash flow margins, this eliminates the working capital friction of waiting 48+ hours for customer payments to clear. Novo Boost is included at no additional cost with all Novo accounts — there are no fees or percentage-based charges for instant payouts. For a Shopify store processing $50,000/month in sales, Novo Boost accelerates approximately $100,000 in annual working capital availability, enabling more efficient inventory management and reducing reliance on business credit cards for short-term cash flow gaps.

### 2. Novo Reserves: 10 Savings Buckets

Novo Reserves allows business owners to create up to 10 named savings buckets within the primary checking account for earmarking capital — tax reserve, equipment fund, emergency fund, quarterly estimated payments, and more. Each bucket earns 0% APY (no yield) but provides organizational clarity that prevents accidental spending of earmarked capital. Transfers between buckets are free and instant. This feature appeals particularly to sole proprietors and partnerships managing multiple financial priorities without the complexity of opening separate bank accounts at different institutions. While the lack of yield is a notable limitation compared to Relay's interest-bearing sub-accounts, the simplicity and zero-cost structure make Reserves valuable for organizational discipline.

### 3. Refunded ATM Fees Worldwide

Novo refunds 100% of ATM withdrawal fees charged by non-Novo ATM operators in any location worldwide, including international ATMs across 200+ countries. This eliminates the friction of seeking specific ATM networks while traveling or operating internationally. For a business owner making 100+ ATM withdrawals annually, the refunds typically amount to $50-150 in network fees. More significantly, the feature removes the cognitive burden of planning ATM usage around specific networks — a benefit that digital nomads and internationally-focused solopreneurs consistently rate as one of Novo's top differentiators.

### 4. Built-In Invoicing Platform

Novo Invoice, integrated directly into the mobile app and web platform, allows business owners to generate, send, and track invoices without switching to separate accounting software. Invoices can include custom branding, itemized line items, and automatic payment reminders sent on a configurable schedule. Payment collection links enable clients to pay invoices directly via Novo, with payments settling immediately to the checking account. For freelancers sending 15+ invoices per month, this integration saves approximately 7 hours monthly compared to managing a separate invoicing tool — while also reducing the number of paid software subscriptions.

### 5. Deep E-Commerce Integration

Novo integrates natively with the platforms that matter most for online sellers. Connections to Shopify, Stripe, PayPal, Square, Etsy, Amazon, and WooCommerce enable real-time transaction visibility, automated reconciliation, and instant payout acceleration via Novo Boost for Stripe merchants. The App Marketplace extends this further with integrated discounts and perks on third-party services including Stripe processing fee discounts, Google Cloud credits, HubSpot pro-rated billing, Notion Team plan discounts, and Mailchimp extended free tier access. For Shopify store owners specifically, Novo offers marketplace discounts and expedited setup, cementing its positioning as the e-commerce banking platform of choice.

## Novo Features Deep-Dive

### Novo Boost: How Instant Stripe Payouts Work

The mechanics of Novo Boost deserve closer examination because the speed differential is substantial. With a standard Stripe settlement, your customer pays on Monday, Stripe batches the transaction, and funds arrive in your bank account on Wednesday — a 48-hour lag that creates genuine working capital pressure for businesses with thin margins or rapid inventory turnover. Novo Boost bypasses this entirely: once your Stripe account is connected to Novo, completed transactions deposit to your Novo checking account within 5-15 minutes of processing. There is no additional setup beyond the initial Stripe connection, no per-transaction fee, and no percentage-based charge.

The practical impact is most visible for high-volume e-commerce sellers. Consider a Shopify store processing $50,000 per month in sales — roughly $1,667 per day. Under standard 48-hour Stripe settlement, that store has approximately $3,334 in transit at any given time, unavailable for inventory purchases, advertising spend, or supplier payments. With Novo Boost, that working capital is available within minutes, effectively unlocking $3,334 in additional working capital availability at all times. Over a year, this acceleration improves cash flow positioning by approximately $100,000 in faster capital recycling — not additional money, but dramatically faster access to money already earned.

Where traditional banks and competitors like Mercury settle Stripe payouts in the standard 48-hour window, Novo Boost deposits funds within minutes. This is not a marginal improvement — it fundamentally changes cash flow dynamics for businesses operating on tight payment cycles. For SaaS companies collecting monthly subscriptions, the benefit is less pronounced since revenue is more predictable. But for e-commerce sellers with variable daily sales, seasonal spikes, and inventory-dependent margins, Novo Boost is genuinely transformative.

### Novo Reserves: Organizational Banking Without Complexity

The 10-bucket savings structure addresses a common pain point for sole proprietors and single-owner LLCs: the tendency to commingle operating cash with reserved funds. By creating named buckets — "Q1 Estimated Taxes," "Equipment Upgrade," "Emergency Fund 3-Month," "Marketing Budget" — business owners enforce spending discipline without the overhead of managing multiple bank accounts across different institutions. Transfers between the main checking account and any bucket are instant and free, with no daily limits on the number of moves.

The key limitation is yield: Novo Reserves earns 0% APY on all buckets. For comparison, Relay offers 20 sub-accounts with a 3.0% savings APY, meaning a $30,000 tax reserve earns $900/year on Relay and $0 on Novo. If you maintain substantial reserved balances, the yield gap is material. However, if your reserved amounts are small (under $10,000 total) and organizational clarity is your primary need, Novo Reserves delivers the structure without any monthly cost.

<Warning>
**No Yield on Reserves:** All 10 savings buckets earn 0% APY. If you are earmarking $20,000+ for taxes or other reserves, consider maintaining those funds in a high-yield business savings account (Bluevine 2.0%, Relay 3.0%) and using Novo primarily for operational spending and Stripe payout acceleration.
</Warning>

### Built-In Invoicing: Replacing Third-Party Tools

Novo Invoice eliminates the need for a separate invoicing subscription for many freelancers and service providers. The feature supports custom branding (logo, colors, business details), itemized line items with quantity and rate fields, automatic payment reminders on configurable schedules, and direct payment collection through Novo — payments from clients settle immediately to the checking account without an intermediary payment processor taking a cut. For a freelancer sending 15 invoices per month, Novo estimates time savings of approximately 28 minutes per invoice (from 30 minutes to 2 minutes), equating to 7 hours per month in administrative labor reduction.

## How Novo Makes Money

Understanding Novo's revenue model helps you anticipate where costs may increase over time and where the platform's business incentives align — or potentially conflict — with yours as a customer. Novo generates revenue through channels that do not involve charging monthly account fees, which explains the genuinely free pricing structure.

Novo Business Banking generates revenue through **five primary channels**:

1. **Card interchange fees** — Novo earns a percentage (typically 0.3-0.8%) from the Visa card network every time you or your employees use a Novo debit card to make a purchase. This is the platform's primary revenue driver and explains why Novo encourages card usage and offers unlimited virtual cards — each transaction generates interchange revenue.

2. **ATM fee refund economics** — While Novo refunds all ATM fees to customers, the platform negotiates wholesale ATM network rates that are lower than the retail fees charged. The spread between the negotiated rate and refunded amount is managed as a customer acquisition and retention cost rather than a profit center.

3. **App Marketplace revenue sharing** — Novo earns referral commissions and revenue shares from partner platforms in its App Marketplace. When a Novo customer activates a Stripe discount, Google Cloud credit, or HubSpot offer through the Novo ecosystem, Novo receives a commission from the partner vendor.

4. **Interest on customer deposits (net interest margin)** — Like all banks, Middlesex Federal Savings Bank earns interest on the deposits held in Novo accounts by lending those funds. Novo receives a portion of this net interest margin as part of its banking partnership agreement — which is also why Novo does not pay APY to customers, unlike competitors that pass through a portion of the earned interest.

5. **Premium feature pipeline** — Novo has signaled intentions to introduce premium features and potential paid tiers for advanced business tools, though as of March 2026, all core features remain free. This pipeline represents future, not current, revenue.

This model means Novo is incentivized to maximize card transaction volume and keep you actively spending through Novo cards rather than holding idle cash. This aligns well with the e-commerce use case (high transaction velocity, low idle balances) but creates a misalignment for businesses that primarily need deposit storage.
