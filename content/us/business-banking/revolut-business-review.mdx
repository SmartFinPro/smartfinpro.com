## What is Revolut Business?

Revolut launched in 2015 as a travel money app in London and has since evolved into a comprehensive business banking platform serving more than 500,000 business customers globally. The US version launched in 2020 and has steadily expanded its feature set, though it still trails the more mature UK and EU offerings in certain areas. Revolut operates under state money transmitter licenses in the US rather than a traditional banking charter, which means your funds are safeguarded at partner banks but are not FDIC insured.

What sets Revolut apart from domestic-focused competitors like Mercury or Relay is its international DNA. The platform was designed from the ground up for cross-border commerce, supporting multi-currency accounts in more than 30 currencies with local receiving details in multiple countries. For businesses that regularly pay suppliers, contractors, or subsidiaries overseas, this architecture eliminates the correspondent banking fees and delays that plague traditional wire transfers. During our testing, transfers within the Revolut network settled instantly, while external transfers to non-Revolut bank accounts typically arrived within one to three business days.

## Pricing Tiers Explained

Revolut's tiered pricing is where most reviews fall short, because the headline numbers obscure meaningful differences in transfer limits, FX markups, and card allowances. Understanding these tiers thoroughly is essential before committing to a plan, since upgrading or downgrading mid-month does not prorate costs.

### Free Plan ($0/month)

| Feature | Limit |
|---------|-------|
| Free local transfers | 5/month |
| Free international transfers | 5/month |
| FX fee | 0.4% markup |
| Physical cards | 1 |
| Virtual cards | 0 |
| Team members | 1 |

The Free plan works exclusively for testing the platform or for very low-volume businesses that send fewer than five international payments monthly. Beyond those five transfers, each additional transaction costs $3 to $5 depending on the corridor, and the 0.4 percent FX markup applies to every conversion. Most businesses will outgrow this plan within the first month.

### Grow Plan ($25/month)

| Feature | Limit |
|---------|-------|
| Free local transfers | 100/month |
| Free international transfers | 10/month |
| FX fee | 0.4% (reduced from Free tier rates) |
| Physical cards | 3 |
| Virtual cards | 20 |
| Team members | 3 |

The Grow plan represents the sweet spot for businesses doing $10,000 to $50,000 monthly in international transfers. The jump from 5 to 10 free international transfers per month, combined with 20 virtual cards and three team member seats, provides enough room for a growing operation. The FX markup remains at 0.4 percent, which is competitive with Wise but not as aggressive as the Scale plan.

### Scale Plan ($99/month)

| Feature | Limit |
|---------|-------|
| Free local transfers | **Unlimited** |
| Free international transfers | **Unlimited** |
| FX fee | **0% (interbank rate)** |
| Physical cards | Unlimited |
| Virtual cards | Unlimited |
| Team members | 10+ |
| API access | Full REST API + webhooks |

The Scale plan is where Revolut truly differentiates itself. Unlimited international transfers at the raw interbank exchange rate with zero FX markup is a proposition that no other fintech platform matches at this price point. The full API access also unlocks payment automation capabilities that are typically reserved for enterprise treasury platforms costing thousands per month.


<Warning>
**Critical detail:** "Free" transfers beyond your plan limit cost $3-5 each. On the Free plan, your 6th transfer costs money. On Grow, your 11th international transfer incurs a fee. Always calculate your true monthly cost based on actual transaction volume before selecting a plan.
</Warning>

## Multi-Currency Accounts & FX

### Instant FX Conversion

One of Revolut's strongest capabilities is real-time currency conversion across more than 30 currencies at competitive rates. On the Scale plan, conversions execute at the raw interbank mid-market rate with zero markup. Lower tiers apply a 0.4 percent spread, which is still substantially better than the 1.5 to 4 percent markup charged by traditional US banks. During our testing period, we converted funds across 14 different currency pairs and confirmed that Scale plan rates consistently matched the mid-market rate shown on independent sources like XE.com within a few basis points.

The conversion process itself is nearly instantaneous. Select the source and target currencies, enter the amount, confirm, and the converted funds appear in your target currency balance within seconds. There is no waiting for settlement or batch processing. This immediacy is particularly valuable for businesses that need to lock in favorable rates quickly during volatile market conditions.

### Multi-Currency Account Details

Revolut provides local receiving account details in multiple jurisdictions, which means your clients and partners can pay you in their local currency without incurring international wire fees on their end.

- **US:** ACH routing number + account number for domestic USD receipts
- **UK:** Sort code + account number for GBP receipts
- **Europe:** Belgian IBAN for EUR receipts via SEPA
- **Singapore:** Local account details for SGD receipts

This multi-rail receiving capability eliminates the friction that typically causes international clients to delay payments. Rather than navigating their bank's international wire process, your overseas clients simply send a local transfer to your Revolut account details in their country.


<Warning>
**US vs. UK difference:** Revolut holds a full banking license in the UK and EU but operates as a money transmitter in the United States. Your funds are safeguarded at FDIC-insured partner banks, but the safeguarding itself is not FDIC insurance on your deposits. For large USD balances requiring direct FDIC protection, consider pairing Revolut with Mercury or a traditional bank for your domestic treasury.
</Warning>

Revolut's native expense management tools eliminate the need for a separate platform like Expensify or Brex for many small to mid-sized businesses. Team members can photograph receipts directly in the Revolut app immediately after making a purchase, and the system automatically matches the receipt image to the corresponding card transaction. ## Security & Regulatory Status

### US Regulatory Framework

| Aspect | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **Licensing** | State-by-state Money Transmitter licenses |
| **Federal registration** | FinCEN-registered MSB |
| **Funds protection** | Safeguarded at FDIC-insured partner banks |
| **FDIC insurance on deposits** | No (e-money institution, not a bank) |
| **Two-factor authentication** | Required for all accounts |
| **Fraud monitoring** | 24/7 automated transaction screening |
| **SOC 2 compliance** | Type II certified |

Revolut's regulatory position in the US is fundamentally different from traditional banks. As a licensed money transmitter rather than a chartered bank, Revolut is required to safeguard customer funds at FDIC-insured partner institutions, but this safeguarding arrangement does not extend FDIC pass-through insurance to your individual deposit. In practical terms, this means your funds are held at FDIC-insured banks on Revolut's behalf, but the coverage applies to Revolut as the depositor rather than to you as the end customer. For businesses holding large idle USD balances, this distinction matters and is worth discussing with your accountant or financial advisor.

## How to Open a Revolut Business Account

### Requirements

- **EIN** (Employer Identification Number) for your LLC or corporation
- **Business registration documents** (Articles of Incorporation or Organization)
- **Proof of business address** (utility bill or lease agreement)
- **Personal identification** for all directors and beneficial owners (passport or driver's license)

### Application Process (10-15 minutes)

1. **Download** the Revolut Business app or visit the web portal at business.revolut.com
2. **Enter** your business details including legal name, EIN, industry classification, and expected monthly volume
3. **Verify** the identity of all directors and beneficial owners by uploading government-issued ID
4. **Select** your plan tier (you can start with Free and upgrade at any time)
5. **Fund** your account via ACH transfer from your existing US bank account

Approval typically takes same-day to three business days. Revolut may request additional documentation for businesses in regulated industries or with complex ownership structures. Once approved, your US account details are available immediately and you can begin receiving and sending funds.

### Our Verdict: 4.7/5

Revolut Business Scale is the strongest choice for US businesses with significant international payment volume. At $99 per month for unlimited transfers at raw interbank exchange rates, it delivers the best unit economics of any business banking platform currently available for cross-border operations. The combination of multi-currency accounts, unlimited virtual cards, built-in expense management, and a full developer API makes it a comprehensive treasury solution for internationally oriented businesses.

However, Revolut is not a universal recommendation. Its lack of FDIC insurance, limited domestic banking features, and non-bank regulatory status make it a poor fit as your sole business bank if you operate primarily in USD. The optimal strategy for most businesses with meaningful international exposure is to pair Revolut Scale for cross-border payments with a domestic FDIC-insured bank like Mercury or Relay for USD treasury management.

**Choose Revolut Business if:**
- Your business processes $50,000 or more monthly in international transfers
- You need multi-currency accounts with local receiving details in multiple countries
- Your team requires unlimited virtual cards with per-card spending controls
- You want API access for automating payment workflows and reconciliation

**Consider alternatives if:**
- You operate primarily in USD with minimal international exposure (choose Mercury)
- You need FDIC-insured deposits as a regulatory or investor requirement (choose Mercury or Relay)
- Your international volume is low or unpredictable (choose Wise pay-as-you-go)
- You follow profit-first budgeting and need multiple sub-accounts (choose Relay)
