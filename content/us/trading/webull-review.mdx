### Hidden Costs to Watch

Webull's headline pricing is genuinely $0 across the board, which is rare in the brokerage industry. However, there are indirect costs and revenue mechanisms that traders should understand before assuming the platform is completely free. The most significant is payment for order flow (PFOF) — Webull routes a portion of its orders to market makers who pay for the privilege, which can result in slightly worse execution prices compared to direct-market-access brokers like [Interactive Brokers](/trading/interactive-brokers-review). For the average retail trader executing fewer than 50 trades per month, the execution quality difference is negligible — typically fractions of a penny per share. For high-frequency traders executing thousands of shares daily, the cumulative impact of price improvement differences can reach hundreds of dollars annually.

| Fee Type | Amount | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| **Payment for order flow** | Indirect (via execution) | Orders routed to market makers — standard for zero-commission brokers |
| **Crypto spread markup** | Varies (0.1-1.0%) | Built into bid/ask spread despite 0% stated commission |
| **Margin interest** | 6.99%+ | Competitive but higher than IBKR's 5.83% |

## How Webull Makes Money

Understanding Webull's revenue model helps you anticipate where costs may be passed through and where the platform's incentives align — or conflict — with yours as a trader.

Webull generates revenue through **five primary channels** in the United States:

1. **Payment for order flow (PFOF)** — Webull receives compensation from market makers (Citadel Securities, Virtu Financial, and others) for routing customer orders to them for execution. This is the primary revenue source and is the same model Robinhood uses. The SEC requires disclosure of PFOF practices in quarterly 606 reports.
2. **Margin interest** — When traders borrow money to trade on margin, Webull charges interest rates starting at 6.99%. With millions of active traders, margin lending is a significant and reliable revenue stream.
3. **Crypto spread markup** — Despite advertising 0% commission on cryptocurrency trades, Webull earns revenue through the bid-ask spread markup on crypto transactions. The effective cost to traders varies by cryptocurrency and market conditions but typically ranges from 0.1% to 1.0%.
4. **Securities lending** — Webull lends out shares held in customer margin accounts to short sellers, earning a lending fee. Customers enrolled in Webull's share lending program receive a portion of this revenue.
5. **Cash sweep interest** — Cash held in Webull accounts is swept to partner banks, where Webull earns interest on the deposits. FDIC insurance protects these swept funds.

This revenue model means Webull is incentivized to attract and retain active traders who generate order flow, use margin, and hold cash balances. The zero-commission structure is sustainable because PFOF and margin interest generate sufficient revenue — but traders should understand that "free" trading includes indirect costs through execution quality.
