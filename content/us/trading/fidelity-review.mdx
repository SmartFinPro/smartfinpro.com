## Key Features for US Investors

### 1. Fidelity ZERO Funds: Industry-Unique 0% Expense Ratios

Fidelity's ZERO fund family eliminates expense ratios entirely — a feat no other fund provider has matched since their launch, which disrupted the entire index fund industry and forced competitors to lower fees across the board. The ZERO lineup includes FZROX (Fidelity ZERO Total Market Index Fund), providing exposure to the entire US stock market equivalent to Vanguard's VTI but at 0% versus VTI's 0.03% expense ratio. FZILX (Fidelity ZERO International Index Fund) covers international stocks equivalent to Vanguard's VXUS at 0% versus 0.08%. FZIPX (Fidelity ZERO US Bond Index Fund) tracks the US total bond market at 0% versus BND's 0.03%. The practical impact is significant: on a $500,000 portfolio, even a 0.05% fee difference compounds to over $50,000 in additional wealth over 40 years. For retirement savers building portfolios over decades, ZERO funds provide the mathematically optimal cost structure available in the market today.

### 2. Active Trader Pro: Professional Charting Platform

Active Trader Pro offers 175+ technical studies, real-time streaming market data, customizable multi-chart layouts, and backtesting capabilities that allow you to test strategies against historical data before committing capital. The platform rivals [Charles Schwab's](/trading/charles-schwab-review) thinkorswim in core capability, though thinkorswim offers more indicators (400+ versus 175+) and includes paper trading that Fidelity does not. Active Trader Pro is available free to all Fidelity customers with no minimum balance or trade requirements. The directed trading feature allows advanced users to route orders to specific exchanges for potential price improvement, and the real-time analytics overlay provides Level II quotes and time-and-sales data for momentum-based strategies.

### 3. Morningstar Integration & Research Tools

Fidelity provides institutional-quality research that saves investors hundreds of dollars annually compared to subscribing to research services independently. The platform integrates Morningstar stock and mutual fund ratings, Fidelity's proprietary Equity Summary Score (a composite rating aggregating data from over 20 research providers), independent analyst reports, and earnings call transcripts. The Stock Screener filters 3,000+ stocks by fundamental criteria (P/E ratio, dividend yield, market capitalization, revenue growth) and technical criteria (RSI, MACD, moving averages). For fund investors, the Mutual Fund Evaluator scores thousands of funds across performance, cost, risk, and manager tenure — making it straightforward to identify the best funds in any category.

### 4. Fidelity Go: Entry-Level Robo-Advisor

Fidelity Go provides algorithmic portfolio allocation starting at $0 for accounts under $25,000 — one of the lowest entry points for automated investing. Above $25,000, the annual fee is 0.35% of assets, which includes automatic rebalancing and tax-loss harvesting on taxable accounts over $10,000. While Fidelity Go is effective for hands-off investors who want basic automated portfolio management, it lacks the sophistication of dedicated robo-advisors like [Betterment](/personal-finance/betterment-review) or [Wealthfront](/personal-finance/wealthfront-review) in areas like direct indexing, advanced tax optimization, and customizable ESG portfolio construction. The key advantage is integration — Fidelity Go sits alongside your self-directed brokerage, ZERO fund holdings, and Cash Management Account in a single unified dashboard.

### 5. Youth Account: Financial Education for Ages 13–17

Fidelity Youth Account enables parents to introduce young people to investing with real money under parental controls. Available to 13–17 year olds, the account includes a debit card for spending, the ability to invest in stocks and ETFs (including fractional shares), and an education portal with age-appropriate investing lessons. Parents maintain visibility through a dedicated dashboard that monitors trading activity, balances, and spending. This feature is unique among major brokers and positions Fidelity as a generational platform — acquiring customers early who may remain on the platform for decades.

### 6. Cash Management Account

Fidelity's Cash Management Account offers competitive rates of approximately 2.72% APY (as of 2024–2025, variable) with FDIC protection up to $5 million through a network of program banks via the sweep program. The account provides seamless cash positioning between checking and investing: you can write checks, use the linked debit card at any ATM (with fee reimbursement), and move money between your brokerage and cash accounts instantly. This functionality makes Fidelity a partial banking substitute, though it lacks the full banking integration (HELOC, dedicated checking with ATM reimbursement worldwide) that [Schwab Bank](/trading/charles-schwab-review) offers.

### ZERO Funds: The Long-Term Wealth Accumulation Advantage

The practical impact of ZERO funds extends far beyond a simple fee comparison. To illustrate the compounding advantage, consider a 30-year-old investor contributing $500 per month to index funds inside an IRA. Over a 35-year accumulation period with an assumed 7% average annual return, the difference between Fidelity ZERO (0% ER) and a standard index fund charging 0.05% may appear trivial on a per-year basis — but the compounding effect is substantial. Scale that to a household with $1 million+ in retirement assets, and the ZERO fund advantage over a lifetime of investing can exceed $50,000. Together, these three funds enable a complete three-fund portfolio at literally zero ongoing cost — the most efficient structure available for passive investors. If you are building a [personal finance](/personal-finance/) strategy centered on low-cost indexing, Fidelity's ZERO funds are the mathematical optimum.

Fidelity's research ecosystem is built around the Equity Summary Score — a proprietary composite rating that aggregates analysis from over 20 independent research providers and distills it into a single score for each stock. The score ranges from 0 to 10, with values above 7 indicating bullish consensus and below 3 indicating bearish consensus. Unlike relying on a single analyst's opinion, the Equity Summary Score provides a wisdom-of-crowds approach to stock evaluation that reduces individual analyst bias. Morningstar fund ratings are integrated directly into the fund screener, enabling you to filter by star rating, analyst medal (Gold, Silver, Bronze), expense ratio, category, and performance period. Independent analyst reports from over 20 providers are accessible directly from any stock or fund page — including earnings call transcripts, sector analysis, and economic research that would cost $500+ per year if purchased independently.

## How Fidelity Makes Money

Understanding Fidelity's revenue model helps you anticipate where costs may increase and where the platform's incentives align — or conflict — with yours as a customer. Unlike publicly traded competitors that face quarterly earnings pressure, Fidelity's private ownership provides more flexibility in how it balances revenue generation with customer experience investment.

Fidelity generates revenue through **five primary channels**:

1. **Net interest revenue** — Fidelity earns interest on customer cash balances swept to program banks through the Cash Management Account and money market positions. The spread between what Fidelity earns on these deposits and the rate it pays customers (approximately 2.72% APY) generates significant recurring revenue. This is why maintaining competitive cash rates matters — Fidelity must balance sweep revenue with attractive customer rates to prevent cash outflows to higher-yielding alternatives
2. **Asset management fees** — Fidelity Go (0.35%/yr above $25K), Fidelity Wealth Services, and Fidelity Private Wealth Management generate advisory revenue from higher-balance clients. The ZERO funds generate no direct revenue — they serve as loss leaders to attract and retain assets that generate revenue through other channels
3. **Fund distribution revenue** — Mutual fund companies pay Fidelity for inclusion in the NTF fund library through 12b-1 fees, sub-transfer agent fees, and revenue sharing agreements. This is how Fidelity can offer 4,000 "no-fee" funds — the fund companies absorb the distribution cost rather than the investor paying a transaction fee
4. **Trading revenue** — While stock/ETF commissions are $0, Fidelity earns $0.65 per options contract and receives payment for order flow (PFOF) on certain equity orders routed to market makers
5. **Lending revenue** — Margin lending (approximately 12.3% on $100K), securities lending (lending customer-held stocks to short sellers for a fee), and Cash Management Account debit card interchange revenue provide additional income streams

This model means Fidelity is incentivized to attract large asset bases rather than to generate transaction revenue — aligning well with long-term, buy-and-hold investors. The ZERO funds serve as powerful customer acquisition tools that bring assets onto the platform, where they generate revenue through adjacent services. This incentive alignment is one reason Fidelity's customer experience tends to prioritize long-term relationships over short-term monetization.
