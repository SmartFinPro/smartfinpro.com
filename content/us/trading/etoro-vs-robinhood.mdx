## Fee Breakdown: The Real Cost of Trading

| Fee Type | eToro |
|---|---|
| US Stocks & ETFs | $0 |
| Options | Limited availability |
| Crypto | Variable spread (1%+) |
| Withdrawal | $5 per withdrawal |
| Inactivity | $10/month after 12 months |
| Currency Conversion | 1.5% (USD to foreign) |
| Minimum Deposit | $10 |

### Robinhood Fee Structure

| Fee Type | Robinhood |
|---|---|
| US Stocks & ETFs | $0 |
| Options | $0/contract |
| Crypto | ~1% spread |
| Withdrawal | $0 |
| Inactivity | $0 |
| Robinhood Gold | $5/month (APY varies; check current rate) |
| Minimum Deposit | $0 |

<Tip>
**Cost Winner:** For active traders making frequent withdrawals, Robinhood saves $5 per transaction. eToro's 1.5% currency conversion fee is irrelevant for US-only stock trading but matters for international assets. Both platforms earn revenue through payment for order flow (PFOF) — the "free" trading is subsidized, not charitable.
</Tip>

## Robinhood Deep Dive: Radical Simplicity

Robinhood pioneered commission-free trading in 2013 and remains the fastest-to-execute mobile trading app. With 24 million funded accounts, true $0 minimums, and fractional shares from $1, it has democratized access to financial markets for an entire generation.

### What Makes Robinhood Different

<CollapsibleSection title="Robinhood Key Features" count={6}>

- **Fractional Shares:** Buy any stock or ETF from just $1 — true micro-investing
- **$0 Everything:** Zero commissions on stocks, ETFs, and options ($0/contract)
- **Robinhood Gold ($5/mo):** Competitive APY on uninvested cash (rate varies with Fed policy), 3% IRA match, larger instant deposits
- **Robinhood Legend:** New desktop platform with advanced charting and Level 2 market data
- **Instant Deposits:** Up to $1,000 instantly (more with Gold) — no waiting for bank transfers
- **Options Trading:** Clean interface with visual P&L projections and $0/contract pricing

</CollapsibleSection>

### Who Should Choose Robinhood?

- **Absolute beginners** wanting the simplest path to their first investment
- **Budget investors** who want to invest as little as $1 per trade
- **Options beginners** seeking $0/contract pricing with a visual interface
- **Cash yield seekers** — Robinhood Gold's competitive APY competes with high-yield savings accounts
- **Mobile-first users** who prefer trading entirely from their phone
