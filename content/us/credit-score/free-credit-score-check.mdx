A free credit score is a numerical representation of your creditworthiness (300-850 range) that you can access without paying for credit monitoring services or pulling your credit report. These scores are provided by websites, banks, and credit card companies using "soft inquiries" that don't affect your credit.

### How Free Credit Scores Work

**Traditional model (paid):**
- Consumers pay $15-40/month for credit monitoring
- Access to all three bureaus (Experian, Equifax, TransUnion)
- Get FICO scores used by lenders

**Free model (advertising-supported):**
- Services like Credit Karma, Experian Free, bank apps provide scores for $0
- They make money by showing you credit card/loan offers
- You get free scores + credit monitoring, they get referral revenue when you apply

**What "free" includes:**
✅ Credit score (updated weekly or monthly)
✅ Credit report summary (accounts, balances, payment history)
✅ Credit monitoring (alerts for new accounts, inquiries, balances)
✅ Score simulator (test how actions affect score)
✅ Educational content

**What "free" does NOT include:**
❌ All 28 FICO score variations (need myFICO subscription for that)
❌ Daily updates (weekly or monthly only)
❌ Full credit reports with all details (need AnnualCreditReport.com for that)
❌ Advanced identity theft insurance (need paid services)

## FICO vs VantageScore: What's the Difference?

### Two Competing Scoring Models

**FICO Score** (created by Fair Isaac Corporation)
- Used by **90% of lenders** for credit decisions
- Range: 300-850
- Multiple versions: FICO 2, 4, 5 (mortgages), FICO 8 (credit cards), FICO 9 (auto loans)
- Factors: Payment history (35%), amounts owed (30%), length of history (15%), new credit (10%), credit mix (10%)

**VantageScore** (created by Experian, Equifax, TransUnion)
- Used by **10% of lenders** and most free credit score websites
- Range: 300-850 (VantageScore 3.0 and 4.0)
- One version used across all credit types
- Factors: Similar to FICO but weighted differently

### Key Differences

| Factor | FICO | VantageScore |
|--------|------|--------------|
| **Usage by lenders** | 90% (mortgages, auto, credit cards) | 10% (some credit cards, personal loans) |
| **Free availability** | Limited (Experian, Discover, some banks) | Widely free (Credit Karma, most apps) |
| **Score range** | 300-850 | 300-850 |
| **Late payment impact** | Moderate | High (more sensitive to recent late payments) |
| **Credit utilization** | High impact | Very high impact |
| **Paid collections** | FICO 9 ignores them; older versions count them | VantageScore 3.0+ ignores paid collections |
| **New credit** | Recent inquiries matter less | Very sensitive to new inquiries |

### Which Score Should You Monitor?

**For credit applications:** FICO matters most. If applying for a mortgage, auto loan, or credit card, lenders pull FICO scores (typically FICO 2, 4, 5 for mortgages, FICO 8 for credit cards).

**For monitoring trends:** VantageScore works fine. If your VantageScore increases from 650 → 700, your FICO likely increased similarly (maybe 640 → 690). Track monthly changes, not exact numbers.

**Example:**
- Credit Karma (VantageScore 3.0): 692
- Experian Free (FICO 8): 672
- **Difference: 20 points** (normal variance)

Both show upward trend—that's what matters.

| **Credit Karma** | VantageScore 3.0 | TransUnion, Equifax | 10% | Free monitoring, trend tracking |

Free services don't show these scores—you need paid myFICO ($40/month) or wait for lender to pull. For credit cards and auto loans, free FICO 8 from Experian is accurate enough.

## What Affects Your Credit Score (5 Factors)

### 1. Payment History (35% of Score)

**What it is:** On-time vs late payments over last 7 years

**Impact:**
- **One 30-day late payment:** -60 to -110 points
- **One 90-day late payment:** -80 to -130 points
- **Collection account:** -50 to -100 points

**How to improve:**
- Set up auto-pay on all accounts
- Pay at least minimum by due date every month
- If you miss a payment, pay ASAP (30 days late is better than 60)

---

### 2. Amounts Owed / Credit Utilization (30% of Score)

**What it is:** How much you owe vs total available credit

**Formula:** (Total balances ÷ Total credit limits) × 100

**Impact:**
- **Under 10% utilization:** Excellent (720-850 scores)
- **10-30% utilization:** Good (670-719 scores)
- **30-50% utilization:** Fair (620-669 scores)
- **50-75% utilization:** Poor (580-619 scores)
- **Over 75% utilization:** Bad (&lt;580 scores)

**Example:**
- Credit card 1: $2,000 balance / $5,000 limit = 40%
- Credit card 2: $1,500 balance / $10,000 limit = 15%
- **Total utilization:** $3,500 / $15,000 = **23%** (Good)

**How to improve:**
- Pay down balances below 30% of limits (+20-50 points in 30 days)
- Request credit limit increases (lowers utilization without paying debt)
- Pay cards before statement closing date (lowers reported balance)

---

### 3. Length of Credit History (15% of Score)

**What it is:** Average age of all accounts + age of oldest account

**Impact:**
- **0-2 years:** Limited history (580-650 scores)
- **3-5 years:** Fair history (650-700 scores)
- **6-10 years:** Good history (700-750 scores)
- **10+ years:** Excellent history (750-850 scores)

**How to improve:**
- Keep old accounts open (even if unused)
- Become authorized user on parent/spouse's old card (+20-40 points instantly)
- Don't close oldest credit card

---

### 4. New Credit / Hard Inquiries (10% of Score)

**What it is:** Recent credit applications and new accounts

**Impact:**
- **One hard inquiry:** -5 to -10 points (recovers in 12 months)
- **Multiple inquiries (5+) in 6 months:** -20 to -50 points
- **New account opened:** -10 to -30 points (temporary, recovers in 6-12 months)

**How to improve:**
- Limit credit applications to 1-2 per 6 months
- Shop for loans within 14-day window (counts as one inquiry)
- Wait 6 months between credit card applications

---

### 5. Credit Mix (10% of Score)

**What it is:** Variety of credit types (credit cards, auto loans, mortgages, personal loans)

**Impact:**
- **Revolving only (credit cards):** -10 to -20 points
- **Installment + revolving (car loan + credit cards):** +10 to +20 points
- **Mortgage + installment + revolving:** +20 to +40 points

**How to improve:**
- Don't open new credit just for mix (not worth it)
- If you already have variety, maintain it
- Focus on payment history and utilization instead (bigger impact)

## Frequently Asked Questions (Extended)

### Is there a truly free credit score with no strings attached?

Yes. Credit Karma, Chase Credit Journey, and Experian Free require NO credit card, NO trial, NO hidden fees. They're advertising-supported (show you credit offers), but you're never charged. If a service asks for credit card info to "unlock" your score, it's a trial—avoid it.

### Why is my Credit Karma score different from my mortgage lender's score?

Credit Karma shows VantageScore 3.0. Mortgage lenders pull FICO 2 (Experian), FICO 5 (Equifax), and FICO 4 (TransUnion). Different scoring models + different bureau data = scores can differ by 20-50 points. This is normal. Use Credit Karma for tracking trends, not exact pre-approval numbers.

### Can I trust free credit score websites?

Yes, if they're reputable (Credit Karma, Experian, Chase, Discover, bank apps). These are legitimate companies. However, avoid: (1) Sites requiring credit card for "free trial," (2) Sites promising "instant credit repair" if you sign up, (3) Pop-up ads offering "free scores" (phishing scams).

### How do I check all three credit scores for free?

**Option 1 (Free VantageScore):**
- Credit Karma: TransUnion & Equifax VantageScore 3.0
- Experian Free: Experian VantageScore 3.0
- **Total: All 3 bureaus, $0**

**Option 2 (Free FICO):**
- Experian Free: Experian FICO 8
- Discover Credit Scorecard: Experian FICO 8 (same score)
- Bank app (if you have Citi, Chase, etc.): Equifax or TransUnion FICO 8
- **Total: 2 bureaus FICO, $0**

**Option 3 (Paid FICO - all 3 bureaus):**
- myFICO Premier: All 3 bureaus, 28 FICO scores
- **Cost: $40/month** (cancel after 1 month if you only need one-time check)

### What if I find an error on my credit report?

1. Document the error (screenshot, notes)
2. Gather evidence (bank statements, payment records)
3. Dispute with credit bureau (Experian.com/disputes, Equifax.com/disputes, TransUnion.com/disputes)
4. Bureau investigates within 30 days
5. If error is removed, score improves immediately
6. If error is verified as accurate, it stays

**Alternatively:** Hire credit repair company (The Credit People, Lexington Law) to handle disputes for you.

### Should I pay for credit monitoring or use free services?

**Use free if:**
- You check your score monthly yourself
- No recent identity theft concerns
- Budget-conscious

**Pay for monitoring ($10-40/month) if:**
- Want daily monitoring across all 3 bureaus
- Need dark web scanning for personal info
- Want identity theft insurance ($1M+ coverage)
- Prefer hands-off approach

**Recommendation:** Start with free (Credit Karma + Experian). Upgrade to paid only if you experience identity theft or need all-bureau monitoring.
