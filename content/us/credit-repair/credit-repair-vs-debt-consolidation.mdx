## What is Credit Repair?

Credit repair is the process of identifying and removing inaccurate, outdated, or unverifiable information from your credit reports at Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion.

### What Credit Repair Fixes

✅ **Collections that aren't yours** - Identity theft or mixed credit files

✅ **Duplicate accounts** - Same debt reported by original creditor AND collection agency

✅ **Outdated items** - Collections older than 7 years, bankruptcies older than 10 years

✅ **Incorrect late payments** - Payments marked late when you paid on time

✅ **Unauthorized hard inquiries** - Credit checks you didn't authorize

✅ **Wrong account balances** - $5,000 balance reported as $15,000

### What Credit Repair Does NOT Fix

❌ **Accurate negative items** - Legitimate late payments from last year

❌ **Valid collections** - Debt you actually owe that's correctly reported

❌ **Current debt balances** - Credit repair doesn't pay off debt

❌ **Bankruptcy within 7-10 years** - Accurate bankruptcies stay on report

❌ **Foreclosures within 7 years** - Accurate foreclosures remain

### How Credit Repair Works

1. **Pull credit reports** from all three bureaus (free at annualcreditreport.com)
2. **Identify errors** - Review line-by-line for inaccurate items
3. **Gather evidence** - Bank statements, payment records, documentation
4. **File disputes** - Submit challenges to credit bureaus
5. **Monitor results** - Bureaus investigate within 30 days
6. **Re-dispute if needed** - Items not removed are challenged again

## What is Debt Consolidation?

Debt consolidation combines multiple debts (credit cards, personal loans, medical bills) into one new loan, ideally at a lower interest rate.

### What Debt Consolidation Does

✅ **Simplifies payments** - One monthly payment instead of 5-10

✅ **Reduces interest rates** - Combine 18-24% credit cards into 7-12% personal loan

✅ **Lowers monthly payment** - Extended term reduces monthly obligation (but increases total interest if term is longer)

✅ **Stops collection calls** - Paying off collections eliminates harassment

✅ **Improves credit utilization** - Paying off credit cards lowers utilization ratio

### What Debt Consolidation Does NOT Do

❌ **Reduce principal owed** - You still owe the full amount (just at lower interest)

❌ **Remove negative items** - Late payments and collections stay on report for 7 years

❌ **Guarantee approval** - Requires credit score 600-650+ for good rates

❌ **Fix spending habits** - If you re-borrow on paid-off cards, you'll have OLD debt + NEW debt

### How Debt Consolidation Works

1. **Calculate total debt** - Add up all credit card balances, personal loans, collections
2. **Check credit score** - Determine what interest rate you'll qualify for
3. **Apply for consolidation loan** - Personal loan, balance transfer card, or home equity loan
4. **Use loan to pay off debts** - Pay off all old accounts in full
5. **Make single monthly payment** - Repay new loan over 2-5 years

### Debt Consolidation Options

| Type | APR Range | Best Credit | Pros | Cons |
|------|-----------|------------|------|------|
| **Personal loan** | 7-36% | 650+ | Fixed rate, no collateral | Origination fees 1-8% |
| **Balance transfer card** | 0% intro (6-21mo), then 15-25% | 700+ | 0% intro rate | 3-5% transfer fee, must pay off in intro period |
| **Home equity loan** | 6-12% | 600+ | Low rate | Home at risk if you default |
| **401(k) loan** | 5-7% | N/A | Low rate, no credit check | Reduces retirement savings |

### Example: Consolidation Savings

**Before consolidation:**
- Credit card 1: $8,000 at 22% APR = $147/month interest
- Credit card 2: $5,000 at 19% APR = $79/month interest
- Personal loan: $4,000 at 16% APR = $53/month interest
- **Total: $17,000 debt, $279/month in interest alone**

**After consolidation:**
- Personal loan: $17,000 at 10% APR = $142/month interest
- **Monthly interest savings: $137**
- **5-year savings: $8,220**

<Warning>
Close paid-off credit cards after consolidation or you'll be tempted to re-borrow. 68% of people who consolidate without closing cards end up with MORE debt within 2 years.
</Warning>

## When to Choose Credit Repair

### Choose Credit Repair If:

✅ **You have inaccurate items on your credit report** - Pull reports from annualcreditreport.com and review for errors

✅ **Credit score is low due to errors, not behavior** - Collections that aren't yours, duplicate accounts, outdated items

✅ **You're planning to apply for credit soon** - Mortgage, auto loan, credit card

✅ **You have time before applying for credit** - Credit repair takes 3-6 months

✅ **Debt is manageable** - You can afford current monthly payments; issue is just credit score

## When to Use BOTH Strategies (Sequential Approach)

### The Optimal Strategy: Credit Repair → Debt Consolidation

If you have BOTH credit report errors AND high-interest debt, do them sequentially for maximum savings.

### Step-by-Step Sequential Strategy

**Month 1-6: Credit Repair Phase**

1. Pull credit reports from all three bureaus
2. Identify all inaccurate items (collections, late payments, inquiries)
3. File disputes (DIY or hire The Credit People)
4. Monitor removals monthly
5. Target: Increase score by 50-100 points

**Expected outcome:** Score increases from 620 → 690

**Month 7: Apply for Debt Consolidation**

1. With improved score (690 vs 620), qualify for better rates
2. Shop for personal loans from 3-5 lenders
3. Compare APRs (690 score gets 9-11% vs 620 score at 16-20%)
4. Choose best loan and consolidate debt

**Expected outcome:** Save $4,000-8,000 in interest vs consolidating at lower score

**Month 8-36: Debt Payoff Phase**

1. Make on-time payments on consolidation loan
2. Monitor credit score (should continue climbing)
3. Avoid re-borrowing on paid-off credit cards
4. Track progress toward debt freedom

**Expected outcome:** Debt-free in 2-4 years with 720+ credit score

**Starting point:**

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I consolidate debt if I have bad credit (under 600)?

Possibly, but rates will be 18-25% APR—barely better than credit cards. Better strategy: (1) Do credit repair to boost score to 650+, (2) THEN consolidate at 10-14% APR. You'll save $3,000-8,000 in interest.

### Will credit repair help me qualify for a mortgage?

Yes, if you have inaccurate items. Removing collections, late payments, and inquiries can increase your score by 50-100 points. Most lenders require 620+ for FHA, 640+ for conventional. If you're at 580 with errors, credit repair gets you to 650-680, qualifying you for better rates.

### Should I use a credit repair company or consolidation company?

They're different services:
- **Credit repair:** The Credit People, Lexington Law (remove credit report errors)
- **Debt consolidation:** SoFi, LightStream, Upstart (provide loans to pay off debt)

You may need BOTH—credit repair first, then consolidation.

### How long does credit repair take vs debt consolidation?

- Credit repair: 3-6 months to remove items
- Debt consolidation: 1-2 months to close loan, 2-5 years to pay off

If doing both sequentially, plan 4-7 months for credit repair, then consolidate.

### What if my debt is all accurate (no credit report errors)?

Skip credit repair. Focus on debt consolidation (if score is 650+) or debt settlement (if score is under 600 and debt is $20,000+).

### Can debt consolidation remove collections from my credit report?

No. Paying off a collection doesn't remove it from your report—it just changes status to "paid." However, newer FICO models (FICO 9, VantageScore 3.0) ignore paid collections, so paying them off can still boost your score. Ask collectors for "pay-for-delete" agreements in writing before paying.

### Is it worth paying for credit repair if I can do it myself?

If you have 1-3 errors: DIY (free).

If you have 5+ errors: Professional credit repair pays for itself. They know which dispute strategies work, handle re-disputes, and achieve 58% removal rates vs 42% for DIY. Cost: $600-900. Savings from better loan rates: $3,000-40,000.
