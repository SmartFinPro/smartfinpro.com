## What is Credit Repair?

Credit repair is the process of identifying and correcting inaccurate, outdated, or unverifiable information on your credit reports from Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion. Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), consumers have the legal right to dispute errors that harm their creditworthiness.

## How Credit Repair Works: The 5-Step Process

### Step 1: Pull Your Credit Reports (Free)

By law, you're entitled to one free report per bureau annually:

- Visit **AnnualCreditReport.com** (the only FTC-authorized site)
- Request reports from all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion)
- Download and save as PDFs
- Review each report line-by-line

<Warning>
Avoid "free credit report" sites that require credit card info. They're trial offers that auto-convert to $29.95/month subscriptions. Use the official AnnualCreditReport.com site.
</Warning>

### Step 2: Identify Errors & Inaccuracies

Common errors to flag:

| Error Type | Example | Impact on Score |
|-----------|---------|----------------|
| **Incorrect late payments** | Payment marked 60 days late when you paid on time | -60 to -110 points |
| **Accounts not yours** | Identity theft, mixed files | -50 to -150 points |
| **Duplicate accounts** | Same debt reported by original creditor and collection agency | -30 to -80 points |
| **Outdated collections** | Debt older than 7 years still reporting | -50 to -100 points |
| **Wrong balances** | $5,000 balance reported as $15,000 | -20 to -60 points |
| **Incorrect limits** | $10,000 limit shown as $5,000 (high utilization) | -10 to -40 points |

### Step 3: Gather Supporting Documentation

For each error, collect proof:

- **Bank statements** showing on-time payments
- **Canceled checks** or electronic payment confirmations
- **Court documents** if bankruptcies or judgments are inaccurate
- **Identity theft reports** from IdentityTheft.gov
- **Letters from creditors** acknowledging errors

### Step 4: File Disputes with Credit Bureaus

Three methods to dispute:

**1. Online Portals (Fastest)**
- Equifax: equifax.com/personal/credit-report-services/credit-dispute
- Experian: experian.com/disputes
- TransUnion: transunion.com/credit-disputes

**2. Mail (Creates Paper Trail)**

Sample dispute letter:

```
[Your Name]
[Address]
[City, State ZIP]
[Date]

Equifax Information Services LLC
P.O. Box 740256
Atlanta, GA 30374-0256

Re: Dispute of Inaccurate Information

Dear Equifax,

I am writing to dispute the following information in my credit report:

Account Name: Capital One Credit Card
Account Number: xxxx-xxxx-xxxx-1234
Error: Account shows 60-day late payment in July 2025. I paid on time (proof attached).

I request that you investigate this matter and remove the inaccurate late payment from my report. Per the Fair Credit Reporting Act, you have 30 days to investigate.

Enclosed: July 2025 bank statement showing payment on July 5, 2025 (due date: July 10, 2025).

Sincerely,
[Signature]
```

**3. Phone (Least Effective)**
- Only use for simple errors (name spelling, address)
- Always follow up in writing

### Step 5: Monitor Results & Re-Dispute

Credit bureaus have **30 days** to investigate. Outcomes:

- **Removed:** Error deleted, score increases
- **Updated:** Information corrected
- **Verified:** Bureau confirms item is accurate (re-dispute if you have new evidence)

<Info>
If a bureau verifies an item you believe is inaccurate, escalate to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) at consumerfinance.gov/complaint.
</Info>

## Credit Bureau Dispute Process: What Happens Behind the Scenes

### Investigation Timeline

| Day | Bureau Activity | Your Action |
|-----|----------------|-------------|
| **Day 0** | You submit dispute | Keep copies of all documents |
| **Day 1-5** | Bureau forwards dispute to data furnisher (creditor) | None |
| **Day 6-25** | Creditor investigates their records | Check mail daily for bureau updates |
| **Day 26-30** | Bureau updates report or verifies accuracy | Review results online |
| **Day 31-35** | You receive written results via mail | File re-dispute if item verified incorrectly |

### Creditor Investigation Methods

Creditors verify disputed items by:

1. **Reviewing original application documents**
2. **Checking payment history databases**
3. **Contacting collection agencies** if debt was sold
4. **Requesting account statements** from billing systems

If creditors don't respond within 30 days or can't verify, **the item must be removed**.

### Why Some Disputes Fail

Common reasons disputes are verified (not removed):

- **Insufficient evidence:** You didn't provide proof
- **Vague dispute reason:** "This isn't mine" without explanation
- **Accurate information:** The late payment is real
- **Automated verification:** Creditor has digital records

**Pro Tip:** Professional services know how to word disputes to trigger manual reviews rather than automated responses.

## Your Legal Rights Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)

### What Credit Bureaus MUST Do:

1. **Investigate disputes within 30 days** (or 45 days if you provide new evidence mid-investigation)
2. **Provide free reports annually** (more frequently if you're denied credit)
3. **Correct or delete inaccurate information** within 30 days of dispute resolution
4. **Notify you of investigation results** in writing

### What Creditors CANNOT Do:

1. **Report inaccurate information** knowingly
2. **Re-insert removed items** without notifying you first
3. **Ignore dispute investigations** from bureaus
4. **Report debt older than 7 years** (10 years for bankruptcies)

### Your Right to Sue

If credit bureaus or creditors violate FCRA, you can sue for:

- **Actual damages** (economic losses from denied credit)
- **Statutory damages** up to $1,000 per violation
- **Attorney's fees and court costs**
- **Punitive damages** for willful violations

**When to consider legal action:**

- Credit bureau fails to investigate within 30 days
- Creditor re-reports removed item without new evidence
- You're denied credit due to inaccurate information after multiple disputes

Consult a consumer rights attorney (many offer free consultations).

## Avoiding Credit Repair Scams: Red Flags

### Illegal Promises (Run Away If You Hear These):

❌ **"We'll erase bankruptcies"** → Accurate bankruptcies stay for 10 years. Period.

❌ **"100% guaranteed score increase"** → FTC forbids guarantees (results vary by individual).

❌ **"New credit identity using EIN"** → This is fraud (using Employer ID Numbers as SSNs is illegal).

❌ **"Pay upfront before any work"** → Illegal under Credit Repair Organizations Act (CROA).

❌ **"Don't contact credit bureaus yourself"** → You can always dispute directly (they want control).

### Legitimate Company Signs (Green Flags):

✅ **Written contract** detailing services, fees, timeline, cancellation rights

✅ **No payment until after services begin** (CROA requirement)

✅ **3-day cancellation period** (federal law)

✅ **Disclosures of your rights** (they inform you of DIY option)

✅ **Realistic timelines** (3-12 months, not "instant" results)

✅ **BBB accreditation** with A+ rating and few complaints

### Report Scams

If you encounter illegal credit repair practices, report to:

- **Federal Trade Commission:** ReportFraud.ftc.gov
- **Consumer Financial Protection Bureau:** consumerfinance.gov/complaint
- **Your state Attorney General:** Check your state's consumer protection office

## Beyond Dispute Letters: Advanced Credit Repair Strategies

### 1. Goodwill Adjustment Letters

**What it is:** A letter to your original creditor asking them to remove a late payment as a "goodwill gesture."

**When it works:**
- You have one late payment on an otherwise perfect payment history
- You've been a customer for 3+ years
- The late payment was due to hardship (medical emergency, job loss)

**Success rate:** 15-30% (worth trying on 1-2 accounts)

**Sample goodwill letter:**

```
[Creditor Name]
[Address]

Dear [Creditor],

I am writing to request a goodwill adjustment to my account [Account #xxxx].

I have been a loyal customer since [year], maintaining on-time payments for [X years]. Unfortunately, in [month/year], I experienced [medical emergency/job loss/explain hardship], resulting in a 30-day late payment.

Since that time, I have made every payment on time and brought my account current. I request that you remove this late payment from my credit report as a gesture of goodwill.

I value our relationship and hope to continue as a customer for years to come.

Thank you for your consideration.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
```

### 2. Pay-for-Delete Negotiations

**What it is:** Offering to pay a collection account in exchange for the creditor removing it from your credit report.

**How to negotiate:**

1. **Contact collector via certified mail** (never verbally—get it in writing)
2. **Offer 40-60% of balance** as payment-in-full if they delete
3. **Request written agreement** BEFORE paying
4. **Pay via check or money order** (keep proof)
5. **Verify deletion** within 30 days

**Success rate:** 40-60% (collectors prefer payment over nothing)

**Sample pay-for-delete letter:**

```
[Collection Agency]
[Address]

Re: Account #[xxxx] | Original Creditor: [Name] | Balance: $[amount]

Dear [Collector],

I am writing to propose a settlement. I will pay $[offer amount] (40% of balance) in exchange for your written agreement to delete this account from my Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion credit reports.

This offer is contingent upon receiving a signed letter from your company confirming deletion before I submit payment.

If you agree, please sign below and return within 14 days. Upon receipt, I will submit payment via certified check.

This is not an acknowledgment of debt validity. I reserve all rights under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]

Company Representative Signature: _______________  Date: _______
```

### 3. Debt Validation Challenges

**What it is:** Requiring collectors to prove they own the debt and have legal right to collect.

**When to use:**
- Debt is from a company you don't recognize
- Amount is incorrect
- Debt is older than 3 years (records may be lost)

**How to challenge:**

Send this letter within 30 days of first collector contact:

```
[Collection Agency]
[Address]

Re: Debt Validation Request | Account #[xxxx]

Dear [Collector],

I dispute this debt in its entirety. Under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA), I request that you provide:

1. Proof you own this debt (assignment agreement)
2. Copy of original signed contract with creditor
3. Account statements showing balance owed
4. Your license to collect in [your state]

Until you provide this validation, cease all collection activities and remove this item from my credit reports.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
```

**If they can't validate:** They must stop collection AND remove from credit reports.

**Success rate:** 25-40% (many debts are sold multiple times, records lost)

### After Bankruptcy (Chapter 7 or 13)

**Timeline:**
- Chapter 7 stays on report for **10 years**
- Chapter 13 stays on report for **7 years**

**Repair strategy:**

1. **Month 1-3:** File disputes on accounts showing balances post-discharge (should show $0)
2. **Month 4-6:** Open secured credit card, become authorized user
3. **Month 7-12:** Add credit builder loan, keep utilization under 10%
4. **Year 2-3:** Apply for unsecured credit, car loan (rebuild payment history)

**Expected score trajectory:**
- Post-discharge: 500-550
- 12 months: 580-620
- 24 months: 620-680
- 36 months: 660-720 (with perfect payment history)

### Medical Debt Collections

**2026 Update:** Medical collections now have a **1-year reporting buffer**. Debts incurred after March 2026 won't appear on credit reports until unpaid for 12 months.

**Dispute strategy:**

1. Request itemized bill from hospital (often reveals billing errors)
2. Verify insurance processed claim correctly
3. If error, get provider to withdraw from collections
4. If accurate, negotiate pay-for-delete (success rate 60% for medical debt)

**Alternative:** Contact hospital billing department for payment plans BEFORE it goes to collections.

### Identity Theft

**Immediate actions:**

1. **File police report** (required for FTC Identity Theft Report)
2. **Submit FTC Identity Theft Report** at IdentityTheft.gov
3. **Place fraud alerts** with all three bureaus (free, lasts 1 year)
4. **Freeze your credit** at all three bureaus (free, indefinite)

**Dispute fraudulent accounts:**

Use FTC Identity Theft Report + police report as evidence. Bureaus must block fraudulent accounts within 4 business days (FCRA Section 605B).

**Professional help:** Lexington Law specializes in identity theft restoration ($139/month, includes attorney consultations).

### Student Loan Defaults

**Federal student loans:**

- Defaults stay on report for **7 years** from default date
- **Rehabilitation:** Make 9 on-time payments, default is removed (one-time option)
- **Consolidation:** Doesn't remove default, but brings loan current

**Private student loans:**

- No rehabilitation option
- Negotiate pay-for-delete or settlement
- Statute of limitations varies by state (3-10 years)

**Best strategy:** Rehabilitate federal loans first (removes default entirely), then focus on other credit repair.

## Frequently Asked Questions (Extended)

### Can I repair my credit in 30 days?

Bureaus have 30 days to investigate disputes, so you'll see RESULTS in 30-45 days, but significant score improvement (50+ points) typically requires 3-6 months of consistent effort across multiple disputes and positive credit building.

### Will closing credit cards improve my score?

**No.** Closing cards:
- Increases your credit utilization ratio (less available credit)
- Shortens your average account age (after 10 years)
- Reduces your total number of accounts

**Exception:** Close cards with annual fees you can't afford, but keep your oldest card open.

### Should I settle debts for less than I owe?

**Pros:**
- Save 40-60% of balance
- Debt is paid off
- Some newer FICO models ignore settled collections

**Cons:**
- "Settled" status stays on report for 7 years
- Tax liability (forgiven debt is taxable income)
- Older FICO models still penalize

**Best approach:** Negotiate pay-for-delete (full removal) instead of settlement.

### How long do items stay on credit reports?

| Item | Duration |
|------|----------|
| Late payments (30-180 days) | 7 years from delinquency date |
| Collections | 7 years from original delinquency |
| Chapter 7 bankruptcy | 10 years from filing |
| Chapter 13 bankruptcy | 7 years from filing |
| Foreclosures | 7 years from first missed payment |
| Hard inquiries | 2 years (impact only first 12 months) |
| Closed accounts (positive) | 10 years |

### Does paying off a loan early help credit?

**Short-term:** May drop 5-10 points (you lose an active installment account)

**Long-term:** Positive. Lower debt-to-income ratio helps mortgage/loan approval. After 6 months, score rebounds.

**Verdict:** Pay off high-interest debt regardless of small score dip.
