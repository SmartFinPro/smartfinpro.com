## What is Lexington Law?

Lexington Law is the oldest and largest credit repair law firm in the United States, founded in 1991. Unlike non-attorney credit repair services, Lexington Law operates as a law firm with licensed attorneys who review cases, file disputes, and provide legal interventions for complex credit issues.

The company specializes in challenging inaccurate, outdated, or unverifiable information on credit reports from Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion. Their law firm status provides legal credibility when disputing items like bankruptcies older than reporting limits, court judgments with documentation errors, and identity theft cases.

### Key Facts

- **Founded:** 1991 (34+ years in business)
- **Legal status:** Licensed law firm in all 50 states
- **Pricing:** $89-$139/month + $99 setup fee
- **Guarantee:** 60-day money-back guarantee
- **Attorneys:** Licensed attorneys review cases on Premier plans
- **Technology:** Advanced online portal for tracking disputes
- **Specialization:** Complex legal cases, identity theft, court judgments

</AnswerBlock>

## How Lexington Law Works

### Step 1: Free Credit Consultation (Day 1-2)

1. Visit Lexington Law's website and submit basic information
2. Licensed paralegal calls to review your credit situation
3. No credit pull required—discussion only
4. Receive plan recommendation based on complexity

**Note:** This is a sales call. Be prepared for upsells to higher-tier plans.

### Step 2: Credit Report Analysis (Day 3-7)

1. Pay first month ($189-239 including setup fee)
2. Lexington Law obtains your credit reports from all three bureaus
3. Attorneys (on Premier plans) or paralegals review each item
4. Create personalized dispute strategy prioritizing high-impact items

**Example strategy:** If you have 18 negative items, Lexington Law might prioritize:
- Bankruptcies older than 7-10 years (immediate legal challenge)
- Collections with no documentation (debt validation request)
- Late payments from accounts you dispute
- Unauthorized hard inquiries

### Step 3: Dispute Filing (Week 2-4)

1. Professional dispute letters drafted using legal language
2. Letters sent via certified mail to credit bureaus
3. You receive copies in your online portal
4. Credit bureaus have 30 days to investigate

**Legal advantage:** Disputes include legal references (FCRA Section 611, 623) that signal law firm representation, potentially increasing bureau cooperation.

### Step 4: Creditor Interventions (Premier Plans, Month 1-3)

For Premier and PremierPlus plans:

1. **Debt validation letters** sent to collection agencies
2. **Creditor contact** requesting documentation or settlement
3. **Cease-and-desist letters** to stop harassment
4. **Goodwill letters** to original creditors requesting removal

**Example:** If you have a $3,000 medical collection, Lexington Law sends a debt validation request. If the collector cannot provide original creditor documentation, the item must be removed from your report.

### Step 5: Monitoring & Re-Disputes (Month 2-6)

1. Monthly credit report updates show progress
2. Items not removed in first round are re-disputed with stronger language
3. New negative items that appear are automatically challenged
4. You receive monthly progress reports via email and online portal

**Success metrics tracked:**
- Number of items challenged
- Number of items removed or updated
- Credit score changes across all three bureaus
- Estimated financial impact (mortgage savings, etc.)

### Step 6: Results & Graduation (Month 4-8)

After 4-8 months:
- Most removable items have been deleted or updated
- Credit score has improved by average of 60-95 points
- You can cancel or continue monitoring for new errors

<Info>
The process is identical to non-attorney credit repair services with one exception: on Premier plans, licensed attorneys can send legal demand letters for items like fraudulent accounts or bankruptcies beyond reporting limits. For standard disputes (late payments, collections), attorneys are rarely involved.
</Info>
