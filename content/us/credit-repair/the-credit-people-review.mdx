### Step 1: Sign Up & Consultation (Day 1-2)

1. Visit The Credit People's website and enter basic information
2. Schedule a free 15-minute consultation call
3. Discuss your credit situation, dispute strategy, and pricing

### Step 2: Credit Report Analysis (Day 3-5)

1. You're assigned a dedicated credit specialist
2. Specialist obtains your credit reports from all three bureaus
3. They identify every inaccurate, outdated, or incomplete item
4. Create a prioritized dispute strategy

**Example strategy:** If you have 15 negative items, The Credit People might prioritize the charge-offs and collections first (biggest impact on credit score), then tackle old inquiries and late payments.

### Step 3: Dispute Submission (Week 1-2)

1. Professional dispute letters are written following FCRA guidelines

1. Each month, you receive an updated credit report
2. Specialist tracks which items were removed, updated, or still disputed

### Step 5: Results & Reporting (Month 3-6)

After 4-6 months:
- You've received multiple credit report updates
- Some negative items may have been removed, depending on your specific report

## Results & Performance Analysis

Precise, verifiable statistics on The Credit People's average items removed or average credit score increase are not published by the company and could not be independently verified, so we won't present invented numbers as fact. What can be said with confidence, based on how credit disputes work under the FCRA and on how the company is reviewed publicly:

- **Removal success depends heavily on what's on your report.** Inaccurate, unverifiable, or outdated items (old hard inquiries, misattributed authorized-user accounts, stale collections with incomplete documentation) are far more likely to be removed than accurate, recent negative items.
- **Legitimate negative items cannot legally be removed just because they're disputed.** A genuine late payment or collection that's accurate and within its reporting window will typically stay on your report regardless of which credit repair company you use.
- **Timelines are bureau-driven, not company-driven.** Credit bureaus generally have up to 30 days to investigate each dispute, which is why full engagements commonly run 4-6 months rather than resolving in a single billing cycle.

<Tip>
Because removal rates depend on the mix of accurate versus inaccurate items on your report, be skeptical of any credit repair company (including this one) that promises a specific number of removals or a specific score increase before reviewing your actual credit file.
</Tip>

## A General Credit Repair Timeline

The exact pace of results depends entirely on your credit report, so treat the following as a general outline of how the dispute process typically unfolds—not a guarantee of what will happen in any given month.

### Month 1: Initial Disputes Filed
- Credit specialist analyzes your report
- First batch of disputes sent via certified mail
- You receive copies of all dispute letters
- **What to expect:** Little visible movement yet—bureaus generally have up to 30 days to respond

### Month 2: First Results May Appear
- Some disputes responded to by credit bureaus
- First items may be removed or updated, if any were inaccurate or unverifiable
- Specialist files a second round of disputes on non-responsive items

### Month 3: Momentum May Build
- More items may be resolved as bureaus finish investigations
- Your credit score may start improving, depending on what's been removed
- Specialist escalates stubborn disputes

### Month 4: Secondary Disputes
- Re-disputes sent for items that weren't removed in the first round
- Items that were updated may be re-disputed more aggressively

### Month 5: Continued Progress
- Further disputes sent for remaining items
- Focus shifts to items with better removal probability

### Month 6: Final Results
- Most items likely to be removed have typically been addressed by this point
- Specialist provides a final report and next-step recommendations
- You decide whether to continue, switch plans, or cancel
