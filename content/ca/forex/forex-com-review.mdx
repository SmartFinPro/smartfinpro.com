## Canadian Pricing & Account Structure 2026

Forex.com Canada offers two main account structures designed for different trading profiles. The Standard account uses a spread-only model with no separate commission, making cost calculation simpler but resulting in wider effective spreads. The Commission account separates the spread from a fixed commission charge, resulting in significantly lower effective trading costs for active traders — but introduces the complexity of USD-denominated commissions on a CAD-funded account.

Understanding which account type suits your trading volume is the single most important decision when opening a Forex.com Canada account. A trader executing 10 standard lots per month on EUR/USD would pay approximately CAD $100 in spread costs on the Standard account versus approximately CAD $70 on the Commission account (0.2 pips spread + $5 commission per $100k). At 50 lots per month, the Commission account saves roughly CAD $150 per month — enough to justify the added complexity of tracking USD-denominated fees.

|-------------|-----------------|------------|-------------|---------------------------|
| **Standard** | From 1.0 pip | None | CAD $100 | 50:1 |
| **Commission** | From 0.2 pip | USD $5 per 100k | CAD $100 | 50:1 |

CIRO regulations cap leverage at 50:1 for major currency pairs and 25:1 for minor pairs for all Canadian retail clients. This is a uniform regulatory requirement across all CIRO-member brokers — Forex.com cannot offer higher leverage than OANDA, Interactive Brokers, or any other regulated Canadian broker. The leverage caps exist to protect retail traders from excessive risk, and they apply regardless of account type or trading experience.

### CAD Account Funding

Forex.com Canada accepts deposits in CAD via bank wire transfer and online banking. Credit card deposits are not available for Canadian accounts, which is a CIRO compliance requirement rather than a Forex.com policy decision. The absence of credit card deposit fees removes a cost that some offshore brokers charge (typically 1.5–3%), and prevents traders from funding accounts with borrowed money — a consumer protection benefit that aligns with CIRO's mandate.

Withdrawals to Canadian bank accounts are processed within 2–5 business days. There is no explicit withdrawal fee from Forex.com, though your bank may charge incoming wire transfer fees depending on your institution and account type. For traders who value rapid access to their funds, the 2–5 business day withdrawal timeline is standard among CIRO-regulated brokers and slower than the instant transfers offered by some offshore platforms — a trade-off for the regulatory protections that CIRO membership provides.

## CIRO Compliance & Security

### Regulatory Status

Forex.com Canada is operated by GAIN Capital — Forex.com Canada Ltd., registered as an Investment Dealer and a member of [CIRO (Canadian Investment Regulatory Organization)](https://www.ciro.ca/). CIRO is the merged successor to IIROC and MFDA, which consolidated in January 2023 to create a single self-regulatory organisation overseeing all investment dealers and mutual fund dealers in Canada. CIRO membership is the gold standard for Canadian retail forex broker regulation, and it requires Forex.com to maintain stringent capital adequacy requirements, submit to regular compliance audits, and adhere to conduct standards designed to protect retail investors.

The regulatory framework provides several layers of protection that Canadian traders should understand. CIPF membership protects client funds up to CAD $1 million per account category in the event of broker insolvency — this is not protection against trading losses, but against the broker itself failing. Segregated client funds are held separately from Forex.com Canada's operating capital in Canadian Schedule I bank accounts, which means that even if Forex.com were to face financial difficulties, client funds would not be available to the firm's creditors. [FINTRAC](https://www.fintrac-canafe.gc.ca/) registration ensures compliance with Canada's anti-money laundering and know-your-customer requirements, and [FCAC](https://www.canada.ca/en/financial-consumer-agency.html) compliance subjects Forex.com to consumer protection obligations.

No CIRO disciplinary actions, FCAC public notices, or Competition Bureau proceedings are on record for Forex.com Canada in 2025–2026. This clean regulatory history is a meaningful differentiator from offshore brokers that operate without Canadian regulatory oversight and offer no recourse for Canadian traders in the event of disputes.

### Account Opening Process

Canadian retail accounts can be opened online in 15–30 minutes. CIRO-required KYC documentation includes government-issued photo ID and proof of address. Account funding and trading typically activate within 1–2 business days after verification is completed. The process is straightforward but requires patience — unlike some offshore brokers that offer instant account activation, CIRO compliance mandates thorough identity verification before any trading can begin.

### Security Features

Client funds at Forex.com Canada are held in segregated accounts at Canadian Schedule I banks. The CIPF protection applies in the event of Forex.com Canada's insolvency — it does not protect against trading losses. Two-factor authentication is available on all accounts. Forex.com Canada uses 256-bit SSL encryption for all web communications and implements industry-standard AML monitoring under FINTRAC requirements.

## How Forex.com Makes Money

Understanding Forex.com's revenue model helps you anticipate where costs may change and where the platform's incentives align — or conflict — with yours as a trader.

Forex.com generates revenue through several primary channels in Canada. On Standard accounts, the spread markup is the primary revenue source — the difference between the raw interbank spread and the 1.0+ pip spread charged to traders represents Forex.com's margin. On Commission accounts, the explicit USD $5 per $100,000 commission is the primary fee, with a smaller markup remaining in the raw spread. Overnight financing charges (swap rates) apply to positions held past the daily rollover, generating revenue on leveraged positions that traders maintain overnight. CFD financing on indices, commodities, and crypto positions also contributes to the fee pool.

This revenue model means Forex.com is incentivised to encourage higher trading volume and to attract traders to the Standard account where spread margins are wider. Choosing the Commission account and monitoring your swap costs are the most impactful decisions you can make to control your effective trading expenses.
