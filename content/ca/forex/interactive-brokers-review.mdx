## Canadian Tax Treatment

The Canada Revenue Agency classifies forex trading income as either **capital gains** or **business income** depending on your trading activity.

| Factor | Capital Gains | Business Income |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Tax inclusion** | 50% of gains taxable | 100% of gains taxable |
| **Trading frequency** | Occasional (<50 trades/year) | Frequent, systematic trading |
| **Holding period** | Days to weeks or longer | Minutes to hours (intraday) |
| **Loss deduction** | Against capital gains only | Against all income |
| **CRA classification** | Investment activity | Business activity |

### IBKR Tax Reporting for Canadians

Interactive Brokers Canada provides T5008 slips issued annually for all securities transactions, detailed annual activity statements with trade-by-trade reporting for CRA filing, downloadable realized P&L reports for your accountant, and multi-currency tax reports with gains and losses calculated in CAD using Bank of Canada exchange rates. The reporting tools are among the most comprehensive of any Canadian broker, which is particularly valuable for active traders with high trade volumes.

<Warning>
**Consult a Canadian CPA.** The capital gains vs. business income distinction can mean the difference between paying tax on 50% or 100% of your profits. A chartered professional accountant familiar with securities trading can help you establish the correct reporting framework and potentially save thousands annually. IBKR's detailed trade records make this conversation with your CPA much easier.
</Warning>
