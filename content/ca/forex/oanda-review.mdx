## Canadian Tax Treatment

### Tax Reporting with OANDA Canada

OANDA Canada provides the following tax documentation to simplify CRA reporting:

- **T5008 slips** — issued annually for all securities transactions
- **Annual trading statements** — detailed trade-by-trade records in CAD
- **Realized P&L summary** — downloadable reports for your accountant
- **Transaction history** — exportable to CSV for third-party tax software

### Capital Gains vs Business Income

| Factor | Capital Gains Treatment | Business Income Treatment |
|--------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Inclusion rate** | 50% of net gains taxable | 100% of net gains taxable |
| **Typical trader profile** | Fewer than 50 trades/year, holding periods of days+ | Daily trading, high frequency |
| **Loss treatment** | Deductible against capital gains only | Deductible against all income |
| **CRA classification basis** | Investment intent, occasional activity | Business-like, profit-seeking activity |

### TFSA and RRSP Considerations

OANDA Canada does not offer TFSA or RRSP accounts for leveraged forex trading. Leveraged spot forex is generally not permitted within registered accounts under CRA guidelines. Traders seeking tax-sheltered currency exposure should consider forex ETFs within registered accounts at other Canadian brokers.

<Warning>
**Tax classification matters significantly.** A Canadian trader earning C$20,000 in forex profits would owe approximately C$3,000-C$5,000 in tax under capital gains treatment versus C$6,000-C$10,000 under business income treatment (depending on province and marginal rate). Consult a CPA to establish the correct reporting framework.
</Warning>
