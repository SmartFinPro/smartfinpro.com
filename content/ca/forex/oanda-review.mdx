## Canadian Tax Treatment

### Tax Reporting with OANDA Canada

OANDA Canada provides tax documentation to simplify CRA reporting. T5008 slips are issued annually for all securities transactions. Annual trading statements provide detailed trade-by-trade records in CAD, and realized P&L summaries are downloadable for your accountant. Transaction history can be exported to CSV for third-party tax software like TurboTax or Wealthsimple Tax.

### Capital Gains vs Business Income

The CRA does not have a bright-line test for distinguishing capital gains from business income on forex trading. The classification depends on your trading frequency, holding periods, and whether trading constitutes a business-like activity.

| Factor | Capital Gains Treatment | Business Income Treatment |
|--------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Inclusion rate** | 50% of net gains taxable | 100% of net gains taxable |
| **Typical profile** | Fewer than 50 trades/year, longer holds | Daily trading, high frequency |
| **Loss treatment** | Deductible against capital gains only | Deductible against all income |

### TFSA and RRSP Considerations

OANDA Canada does not offer TFSA or RRSP accounts for leveraged forex trading. Leveraged spot forex is generally not permitted within registered accounts under CRA guidelines. Traders seeking tax-sheltered currency exposure should consider forex ETFs within registered accounts at other Canadian brokers.

<Warning>
**Tax classification matters significantly.** A Canadian trader earning C$20,000 in forex profits would owe approximately C$3,000-C$5,000 in tax under capital gains treatment versus C$6,000-C$10,000 under business income treatment, depending on province and marginal rate. Establish your reporting framework with a CPA before your first tax filing.
</Warning>
