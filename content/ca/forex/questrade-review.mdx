Questrade holds one of the most comprehensive regulatory registrations of any Canadian brokerage, covering all provinces and territories under CIRO (formerly IIROC) oversight. As a CIRO-registered investment dealer, Questrade is subject to capital adequacy requirements, conduct standards, and regular compliance audits — the same regulatory framework that governs Canada's largest banks.

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-----------|
| **Entity** | Questrade Inc. |

Questrade's CIRO membership means all eligible accounts are covered by the Canadian Investor Protection Fund. Each account category receives separate coverage, meaning a trader with multiple account types can have substantially more total protection than the C$1,000,000 headline figure suggests.

Questrade is registered as an investment dealer with the Canadian Securities Administrators (CSA) in every province and territory. This includes the Ontario Securities Commission (OSC), the Autorite des marches financiers (AMF) in Quebec with full French-language support, the BC Securities Commission (BCSC), the Alberta Securities Commission (ASC), and all remaining provinces and territories.

## Canadian Tax Treatment

### Questrade Tax Documentation

As a Canadian-registered investment dealer, Questrade provides comprehensive tax documents that simplify CRA filing. You receive consolidated T5008 slips covering forex, equity, and ETF transactions, RRSP contribution receipts for registered account holders, TFSA transaction summaries for CRA reporting, FHSA contribution tracking, and detailed trade confirmations available for download year-round.

### Capital Gains vs Business Income

| Factor | Capital Gains | Business Income |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Inclusion rate** | 50% of net gains taxable | 100% of net gains taxable |
| **Typical profile** | Occasional forex trades alongside equity investments | Dedicated, frequent forex trading |
| **RRSP/TFSA forex ETFs** | Tax-sheltered (no immediate tax) | N/A (registered accounts default to capital treatment) |
| **Loss deduction** | Against capital gains only | Against all income sources |

### Integrated Tax Advantage

Because Questrade consolidates all your trading activity under one dealer, your year-end tax preparation is simplified significantly compared to using multiple platforms. One T5008 slip covers all non-registered trading activity. Capital losses from equities can offset capital gains from forex when both are classified as capital transactions. Registered account activity (RRSP, TFSA, FHSA) is reported separately with proper tax sheltering. This consolidation saves hours during tax season and reduces the risk of missed reporting.

<Warning>
**Do not assume uniform tax treatment.** Even within a single Questrade account, the CRA may classify frequent forex trading as business income while treating your occasional equity trades as capital gains. Consult a CPA familiar with multi-asset trading for proper classification. The distinction between capital gains (50% inclusion rate) and business income (100% inclusion rate) has significant tax implications.
</Warning>
