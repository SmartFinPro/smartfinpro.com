## Key Features for Canadian Traders

### 1. Integrated Stocks, ETFs, and Forex

Questrade's core advantage is the single-account integration of Canadian equities (TSX, TSX-V), US equities (NYSE, NASDAQ, AMEX), ETFs (commission-free to buy), options, and 50+ forex pairs. A Canadian investor hedging US dollar exposure on their US equity holdings can place forex orders alongside equity orders in one screen — without transferring funds between separate accounts. The practical benefit is immediate. If you hold US-listed technology stocks in your Questrade margin account and want to hedge your USD exposure ahead of a Bank of Canada rate decision, you can sell USD/CAD in the same platform without moving funds to a separate forex broker. The transaction settles within the same account structure, and your margin is calculated across all positions — equities and forex combined. No other CIRO-regulated platform offers this level of integration between equity and forex trading.

### 2. TFSA and RRSP Forex Access

Questrade allows forex trading within registered accounts (TFSA, RRSP, RESP), a feature that is genuinely unique among Canadian CIRO-regulated brokers and one of the most significant differentiators in this review. Forex gains realized within a TFSA are completely tax-free, which can be meaningful for Canadian investors who are actively managing currency exposure as part of their long-term savings strategy. Canadians looking for competitive savings rates alongside their investment accounts may also want to consider [EQ Bank](/ca/personal-finance/eq-bank-review/), which offers high-interest TFSA savings without the forex trading component. The [CRA](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency.html) treats forex gains within a TFSA the same as equity gains — provided the account is not deemed to be carrying on a business.

However, this access comes with serious risk implications that every Canadian investor must understand. Leverage-induced losses within a TFSA permanently reduce contribution room — unlike an RRSP where losses can be used for other tax purposes, a TFSA loss simply erases room that took years to accumulate. If you deposit CAD $10,000 into your TFSA, leverage it to control a CAD $500,000 forex position, and suffer a 5% loss (CAD $25,000), you do not just lose the money — you lose the TFSA contribution room permanently. Risk management within registered accounts requires particular caution, and we strongly recommend using minimal leverage in any registered account.

### 3. Questrade Edge Platform

The Questrade Edge platform (web and desktop) provides integrated charting, technical analysis tools, and order management for both equities and forex. The platform supports multiple order types including market, limit, stop-loss, and trailing stop orders, and forex pairs stream in real-time alongside equity positions in the same watchlist. For investors who trade both equities and currencies, this unified workspace eliminates the need to switch between platforms to monitor their full portfolio.

That said, Questrade Edge is functional for active forex traders but lacks the depth of dedicated platforms. There is no MetaTrader 4 integration, no TradingView native connection, and the charting capabilities — while adequate for equity analysis — fall short of what serious technical forex traders expect. If you rely on MT4 expert advisors, custom indicators, or TradingView's Pine Script language for your [trading strategies](/ca/forex/), Questrade Edge will feel limited. The platform is best suited to investors who view forex as one component of a broader portfolio rather than their primary activity.

Questrade's IQ Edge desktop platform offers more advanced charting capabilities, real-time streaming data, and a customizable workspace for active traders. The desktop application provides Level II market data, advanced order routing, and the ability to view forex pairs alongside equities in custom layouts. ## How Questrade Makes Money

Understanding Questrade's revenue model helps you anticipate where costs may increase and where the platform's incentives align with your interests as a trader.

Questrade generates revenue through **five primary channels** in Canada:

1. **Forex spreads** — The ~1.5 pip spread on EUR/USD and equivalent markups on other pairs represent Questrade's primary forex revenue. Because the pricing is spread-only (no commission), Questrade earns on every trade executed through the built-in spread markup
2. **Equity trading commissions** — While ETF purchases are commission-free, equity trades generate commission revenue. Options trades also carry per-contract fees. These equity revenues subsidize the broader platform, including forex infrastructure
3. **Margin interest** — Questrade charges interest on borrowed margin used for both equity and forex positions. Forex leverage generates interest revenue when traders hold positions overnight (swap rates)
4. **Currency conversion spread** — When non-forex customers convert CAD to USD (or vice versa) for equity purchases, Questrade applies a conversion spread that is wider than the forex market rate. Sophisticated users bypass this by executing forex trades for currency conversion
5. **Data and premium services** — Real-time market data feeds, Level II quotes, and advanced platform features generate subscription revenue from active traders

This model means Questrade is incentivized to keep you on the platform across multiple asset classes — the wider forex spreads are partially offset by the commission-free ETF offering that drives customer acquisition. The platform works best when you use it for your entire portfolio, not just forex.
