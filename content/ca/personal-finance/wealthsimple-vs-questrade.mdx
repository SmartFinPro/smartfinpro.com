### FX Conversion: A Hidden Cost for Canadian Investors

When you buy US-listed stocks or receive USD dividends, both platforms convert your Canadian dollars to US dollars (and vice versa). This foreign exchange spread is one of the biggest hidden costs for Canadian investors.

- **Wealthsimple (free tier):** 1.50% FX conversion fee on every USD transaction
- **Wealthsimple Premium (C$0 with C$100,000+ assets):** 0% FX --- no conversion fee at all
- **Questrade:** Approximately 1.50%-2.00% FX spread, but you can use **Norbert's Gambit** (a manual currency conversion technique using dual-listed ETFs) to reduce this to nearly zero

<Tip>
If you hold a large portfolio with significant US stock or ETF holdings, Wealthsimple Premium's zero-FX benefit or Questrade's Norbert's Gambit strategy can save you hundreds or even thousands of dollars per year.
</Tip>

### Which Broker Is Cheaper?

**For buy-and-hold investors:** Wealthsimple wins decisively. Zero commissions on everything means your costs are effectively C$0 (aside from FX conversion if you buy US stocks).

**For active stock traders (20+ trades/month):** Wealthsimple still wins. At C$4.95-$9.95 per trade, Questrade's commissions add up quickly for frequent traders.

**For ETF-only investors:** Tie --- both offer free ETF purchases. Questrade charges C$4.95-$9.95 to sell, while Wealthsimple is free in both directions.

**For options traders:** Questrade is the only choice. Wealthsimple does not offer options trading.

### Managed Investing (Robo-Advisor)

Both brokers offer managed portfolio services for hands-off investors:

**Wealthsimple Managed Portfolios:**
- Management fee: 0.40% (under C$100K) or 0.50% (over C$100K)
- Underlying ETF MERs: ~0.15%-0.25%
- **Total cost:** ~0.55%-0.75% annually
- Automatic rebalancing and tax-loss harvesting (on taxable accounts)
- Socially responsible investing (SRI) and Halal portfolio options

**Questwealth Portfolios:**
- Management fee: 0.20% (under C$100K) or 0.25% (over C$100K)
- Underlying ETF MERs: ~0.15%-0.20%
- **Total cost:** ~0.35%-0.45% annually
- Automatic rebalancing
- Five portfolio types (aggressive to conservative) plus SRI options

<Tip>
On management fees alone, Questwealth is roughly half the cost of Wealthsimple Managed. On a C$100,000 portfolio, that difference is approximately C$200-C$300 per year. If you want a hands-off managed portfolio at the lowest possible fee, Questwealth has the edge.
</Tip>

### TFSA and RRSP: Which Broker Is Better?

For most Canadians, the TFSA and RRSP are the two most important accounts. Here is how each broker serves them:

**TFSA with Wealthsimple:**
- C$0 commissions on all trades inside your TFSA
- Fractional shares let you invest every dollar (no leftover cash)
- Automatic dividend reinvestment (DRIP)
- Auto-deposits from your chequing account

**TFSA with Questrade:**
- Free ETF purchases --- ideal for building an all-ETF portfolio
- Stock trades cost C$4.95-$9.95 each
- No fractional shares (must buy whole shares)
- Automatic dividend reinvestment available

**Our recommendation:** For TFSA and RRSP investors who buy primarily ETFs, both work well. If you also buy individual stocks, Wealthsimple's zero commissions make it the more cost-effective choice.
