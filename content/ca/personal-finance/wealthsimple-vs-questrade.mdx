## Fee Comparison: Where Your Money Goes

Understanding total cost is critical because fees compound against your returns over decades. Here is how every fee category breaks down between the two platforms.

### Commission Structure

| Fee Type | Wealthsimple | Questrade |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Stock Trades** | C$0 | C$4.95-$9.95/trade |
| **ETF Purchases** | C$0 | C$0 (free to buy) |
| **ETF Sales** | C$0 | C$4.95-$9.95/trade |
| **Options** | Not available | C$9.95 + C$1.00/contract |
| **Account Fees** | C$0 | C$0 |
| **Inactivity Fee** | C$0 | C$24.95/quarter (waived with C$1K+) |

For buy-and-hold investors making 5 to 10 trades per month, Wealthsimple saves roughly C$300 to C$1,200 annually in commissions alone. For ETF-only investors, both platforms offer free purchases, though Questrade charges to sell.

### FX Conversion: The Hidden Cost

When you buy US-listed stocks or receive USD dividends, both platforms convert your Canadian dollars at a markup. This foreign exchange spread is one of the biggest hidden costs for Canadian investors holding US equities.

Wealthsimple charges a 1.50% FX conversion fee on every USD transaction at the free tier. With Wealthsimple Premium (free for accounts with C$100,000 or more), the FX fee drops to zero. Questrade charges approximately 1.50% to 2.00% FX spread but supports Norbert's Gambit, a manual currency conversion technique using dual-listed ETFs like DLR/DLR.U that reduces the effective conversion cost to nearly zero for disciplined investors.

<Tip>
If you hold significant US stocks or ETFs, FX costs matter more than commissions over time. Wealthsimple Premium's zero-FX benefit at C$100,000+ or Questrade's Norbert's Gambit can save hundreds or thousands annually. Calculate your annual USD transaction volume before choosing.
</Tip>

### Managed Investing Fees

Both brokers offer robo-advisor services for hands-off investors. Wealthsimple Managed charges 0.40% to 0.50% management fees plus approximately 0.15% to 0.25% in underlying ETF MERs, bringing total cost to roughly 0.55% to 0.75% annually. Wealthsimple also offers socially responsible investing and Halal portfolio options that Questrade does not match.

Questwealth Portfolios charges 0.20% to 0.25% management fees plus approximately 0.15% to 0.20% in ETF MERs, totalling roughly 0.35% to 0.45% annually. On a C$100,000 portfolio, Questwealth saves approximately C$200 to C$300 per year compared to Wealthsimple Managed.

<Warning>
Do not overlook robo-advisor fees when comparing these platforms. On a C$200,000 managed portfolio held for 20 years, the 0.20% to 0.30% annual fee difference between Questwealth and Wealthsimple Managed compounds to over C$15,000 in lost returns. If you want managed investing at the lowest cost, Questwealth has the edge.
</Warning>

## Registered Account Types

Both Wealthsimple and Questrade support every major Canadian registered account: TFSA, RRSP, Spousal RRSP, RESP, FHSA, LIRA, RRIF, and non-registered taxable accounts. Both also offer corporate investment accounts. The key difference is not account availability but how each platform serves these accounts.

### TFSA and RRSP Performance

For most Canadians, the TFSA and RRSP are the two most important accounts. Within a Wealthsimple TFSA or RRSP, you get zero commissions on all trades, fractional shares that invest every dollar with no leftover cash, automatic dividend reinvestment, and auto-deposits from your chequing account. Within a Questrade TFSA or RRSP, ETF purchases are free but individual stock trades cost C$4.95 to C$9.95 each, there are no fractional shares, and automatic dividend reinvestment is available.

If you build a portfolio primarily from ETFs and rarely buy individual stocks, both platforms perform well. If you also buy individual stocks inside your registered accounts, Wealthsimple's zero commissions make it the more cost-effective choice.

### FHSA Integration

Both platforms fully support the First Home Savings Account, Canada's newest registered account combining RRSP tax deductions with TFSA tax-free withdrawals. Both allow self-directed investing within FHSA and support RRSP-to-FHSA transfers. Wealthsimple's zero-commission structure means every dollar inside your FHSA works harder without trading costs reducing your down payment savings. Questrade's free ETF purchases accomplish the same for ETF-only FHSA strategies.

<Tip>
Open your FHSA as early as possible regardless of which broker you choose. Contribution room ($8,000 per year, $40,000 lifetime) only starts accumulating the year you open the account. Delaying by even one year permanently reduces your tax-advantaged home buying power. You can always transfer the FHSA between brokers later.
</Tip>

### Margin Trading

Questrade offers more robust margin features with competitive borrowing rates and full short-selling capabilities. Wealthsimple offers limited margin with fewer advanced features. If margin trading is important to your strategy, Questrade is the stronger platform. Most registered account investors (TFSA, RRSP, FHSA) cannot use margin regardless of broker, so this difference only matters for non-registered taxable accounts.

## Transferring Between Brokers

Both Wealthsimple and Questrade support in-kind transfers, which move your actual stock and ETF holdings without selling them. This avoids triggering taxable events in non-registered accounts. Wealthsimple covers transfer fees for accounts of C$5,000 or more, while Questrade covers fees for C$25,000 or more. Both typically complete transfers within 5 to 15 business days and support partial transfers if you want to keep some holdings at your current broker.

<Warning>
Always request an in-kind transfer rather than selling and re-buying when switching brokers. Selling triggers capital gains tax in non-registered accounts and uses contribution room in registered accounts. An in-kind transfer preserves your cost basis and avoids unnecessary tax consequences.
</Warning>

## CIRO Regulation, CIPF Protection, and Investor Safety

Both Wealthsimple and Questrade are regulated by the Canadian Investment Regulatory Organization (CIRO), which was formed in January 2023 through the merger of the Investment Industry Regulatory Organization of Canada (IIROC) and the Mutual Fund Dealers Association (MFDA). CIRO sets conduct standards, oversees compliance, and handles investor complaints for all registered investment dealers in Canada.

### CIPF Coverage: What It Means for Your Account

Both brokers are members of the Canadian Investor Protection Fund (CIPF), which protects eligible clients if a member firm becomes insolvent. Coverage extends to C$1,000,000 per account category:

<CollapsibleSection title="CIPF Account Category Limits" count={5}>

- **General accounts (non-registered):** C$1,000,000 per investor
- **Registered retirement accounts (RRSP, RRIF, LIRA):** C$1,000,000 combined
- **Registered education savings (RESP):** C$1,000,000 per beneficiary
- **Tax-free savings accounts (TFSA):** C$1,000,000 per investor
- **First Home Savings Accounts (FHSA):** C$1,000,000 per investor

</CollapsibleSection>

CIPF protection does not cover investment losses from market declines, poor investment decisions, or fraud by a third party. It protects only against the insolvency of the broker itself. Both Wealthsimple and Questrade are well-capitalized and have operated without insolvency issues, but understanding CIPF coverage provides an important safety baseline for Canadian investors.

### Platform Security and Account Safeguards

Wealthsimple uses 256-bit SSL encryption, two-factor authentication, and biometric login on mobile. Suspicious login attempts trigger automatic account locks and email verification. Customer funds are held in segregated accounts at Canadian financial institutions and are not used for Wealthsimple's operational purposes.

Questrade employs similar security protocols including two-factor authentication, encrypted data transmission, and dedicated fraud prevention systems. Customer assets are held separately from Questrade's corporate assets, and the firm publishes annual audited financial statements as required by CIRO regulations.

<Tip>
Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on both platforms immediately after opening your account. This single step prevents the majority of unauthorized access attempts. Both Wealthsimple and Questrade support authenticator app-based 2FA, which is more secure than SMS-based codes.
</Tip>
