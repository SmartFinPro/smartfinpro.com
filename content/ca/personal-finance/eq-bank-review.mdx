## Key Features for Canadians

### 1. Savings Plus Account (HISA)

EQ Bank's flagship product is the Savings Plus Account, a high-interest savings account that currently earns approximately 3.00% with no minimum balance, no monthly fees, and completely unrestricted deposits and withdrawals. The account's appeal lies in its simplicity — you deposit funds, earn interest daily (paid monthly), and withdraw at any time without penalties or bureaucratic friction. Unlike traditional banks that require minimum balances or charge dormancy fees on inactive accounts, EQ Bank has stripped away every unnecessary cost layer.

The Savings Plus Account serves as the foundation for EQ Bank's broader product ecosystem. Interest is calculated on the daily closing balance and credited monthly, meaning every dollar deposited immediately begins earning returns. Deposits via Interac e-Transfer typically post within 24 hours, and the account integrates with the mobile app for real-time balance monitoring and transaction alerts. For Canadians who have been accepting the 0.50-1.00% rates offered by RBC, TD, or Scotiabank savings accounts, switching to EQ Bank's ~3.00% represents an immediate and meaningful improvement in savings returns.

### 2. GIC (Guaranteed Investment Certificate) Offerings

EQ Bank's GIC offerings represent some of the most competitive in Canada, with posted rates typically ranging from 3.50% to 4.50% depending on the term length. Options span from 30-day term deposits through 5-year GICs, catering to both short-term cash management and long-term savings goals. These rates apply equally to all customers — there are no promotional tiers, new-customer-only rates, or volume-based pricing. What you see on the [EQ Bank GIC page](https://www.eqbank.ca/) is what every eligible customer receives.

GICs appeal to conservative investors and those seeking guaranteed returns with zero market risk. Unlike stocks, ETFs, or mutual funds, GIC capital is completely protected and [CDIC-insured](https://www.cdic.ca/) up to C$100,000 per account category. EQ Bank enables GIC laddering — a strategy where you purchase GICs of varying maturity dates to ensure regular liquidity as each GIC matures. For risk-averse savers or Canadians approaching retirement, GIC laddering at EQ Bank's rates provides both predictability and meaningful returns in the current interest rate environment.

### 3. Registered Accounts (TFSA, RRSP, FHSA, RESP)

EQ Bank offers a comprehensive suite of registered accounts, all earning the same competitive rates as the standard savings account. The TFSA Savings account lets Canadians grow savings tax-free, while the RRSP Savings account provides tax-deductible contributions for retirement planning. For first-time homebuyers, EQ Bank's FHSA (First Home Savings Account) provides tax-deductible contributions of up to C$8,000 annually with a C$40,000 lifetime limit — enabling buyers to grow their down payment fund while receiving immediate tax relief.

The availability of multiple registered account types under one digital roof is a significant convenience advantage. Rather than managing TFSA contributions at one institution, RRSP at another, and an FHSA at a third, EQ Bank consolidates these into a single app with unified balance monitoring and contribution tracking. Joint Savings Accounts enable couples or partners to manage household savings collaboratively while maintaining separate ownership rights — both account holders retain full access and can make deposits or withdrawals independently.

### 4. EQ Bank Card (Prepaid Mastercard)

The EQ Bank Card functions as both a debit and prepaid solution, offering 0.5% cashback on all purchases and zero foreign exchange fees on international transactions. For Canadians travelling abroad or making purchases in foreign currencies, the FX savings are meaningful — traditional bank debit cards typically charge 2-4% spreads on currency conversions. A Canadian spending C$1,000 USD annually saves approximately C$20-40 on FX fees alone by using the EQ Bank Card instead of a conventional bank debit card.

The card integrates seamlessly with the EQ Bank app, enabling real-time transaction monitoring, balance management, and fraud alerts. Free ATM withdrawals are available across Canada at any Mastercard-compatible ATM, though international ATM withdrawals carry standard fees. The card has no annual fee, no monthly fee, and no transaction fees beyond standard ATM charges.

<Warning>
**Credit Building Limitation:** The EQ Bank Card is a prepaid product and does not report to Canadian credit bureaus (Equifax, TransUnion). It therefore does not build credit history. Canadians prioritising credit score improvement will need a supplementary credit card from another institution.
</Warning>

### 5. US Dollar Account

EQ Bank's USD Account serves Canadians who hold or receive US currency — cross-border workers, businesses with US income, expatriates, or anyone making regular US-denominated purchases. The account earns competitive interest on USD-denominated savings while eliminating the need for unfavourable foreign exchange conversions at traditional banks. Interest accrues in US dollars, and withdrawals occur in USD, minimising conversion friction. The account integrates fully into the EQ Bank app alongside CAD balances with real-time exchange rate displays.

## EQ Bank Features Deep-Dive

### Account Opening and Onboarding Experience

Opening an EQ Bank account is a fully digital process that typically completes within minutes. The application is accessible via desktop or mobile, requiring personal identification, residency verification, and an initial deposit. EQ Bank uses identity verification technology to prevent fraud while maintaining a streamlined experience — a significant improvement over traditional banks that require in-person branch appointments. Once approved, funds can be deposited via Interac e-Transfer, which typically posts within 24 hours. Mobile app download and biometric login (fingerprint or face recognition) complete the onboarding experience.

The speed advantage is meaningful for Canadians accustomed to the multi-day process at legacy banks. Most EQ Bank customers begin earning interest within one business day of applying, compared to the 3-5 business day timeline at institutions requiring branch visits and manual document processing. The seamless integration with Canada's Interac payment infrastructure means deposits from any Canadian financial institution are straightforward and fast.

### FHSA: First Home Savings Account Deep-Dive

The [First Home Savings Account (FHSA)](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/tax/individuals/topics/first-home-savings-account.html) is one of EQ Bank's most strategically important products for younger Canadians. Launched as a federal government initiative, the FHSA allows first-time homebuyers to contribute up to C$8,000 annually (C$40,000 lifetime maximum) with full tax deductibility on contributions — similar to an RRSP — while withdrawals for a qualifying home purchase are tax-free, similar to a TFSA. This dual tax advantage makes the FHSA uniquely powerful among registered accounts.

At EQ Bank's ~3.00% interest rate, a Canadian maximising FHSA contributions over five years would accumulate approximately C$43,000 (C$40,000 in contributions plus ~C$3,000 in tax-sheltered interest). The immediate tax deduction at a 30% marginal rate generates C$2,400 in annual tax savings per C$8,000 contributed, providing a tangible financial benefit even before accounting for interest earned. For Canadians saving for a first home in markets like Toronto, Vancouver, or Ottawa, the FHSA at EQ Bank's competitive rate is one of the most efficient vehicles available.

### GIC Laddering Strategy at EQ Bank

GIC laddering is a strategy where you purchase GICs of varying maturity dates to balance liquidity with yield. At EQ Bank, this might look like splitting a C$50,000 investment across five GICs: C$10,000 each in 1-year, 2-year, 3-year, 4-year, and 5-year terms. As each GIC matures annually, you reinvest it into a new 5-year GIC (typically the highest-yielding term), and over time your entire portfolio earns the 5-year rate while you maintain annual access to one-fifth of your capital.

With EQ Bank's current rate structure (approximately 3.50% on short terms to 4.50% on 5-year terms), GIC laddering generates blended returns of roughly 4.00% annually — substantially better than parking the entire amount in the HISA at 3.00%. The trade-off is reduced liquidity, since GIC capital is locked until maturity. For Canadians with emergency funds already covered in their Savings Plus Account, laddering excess savings into GICs at EQ Bank represents one of the lowest-risk strategies for maximising guaranteed returns.

## How EQ Bank Makes Money

Understanding EQ Bank's revenue model helps you anticipate where the platform's incentives align with yours as a customer — and where potential trade-offs exist.

EQ Bank generates revenue through **five primary channels**:

1. **Net interest margin** — This is the core revenue driver. Equitable Bank lends deposited funds as mortgages, commercial loans, and other credit products at higher rates than it pays to EQ Bank depositors. The spread between lending rates (typically 5-7%) and deposit rates (~3.00%) generates the bulk of revenue. This model incentivises EQ Bank to attract deposits — which directly benefits customers through competitive rates.

2. **GIC margin** — GIC funds are deployed into longer-term lending at predictable returns. The locked-in nature of GIC deposits gives Equitable Bank certainty for asset-liability matching, enabling it to offer higher rates than on-demand savings accounts while still earning a healthy spread.

3. **Card interchange fees** — When you use the EQ Bank Card, Mastercard and Equitable Bank earn a small interchange fee (typically 0.3-0.5%) from the merchant on each transaction. The 0.5% cashback you receive is funded partly from this interchange revenue.

4. **Business banking fees** — The October 2025 launch of EQ Bank Business introduces a new revenue stream, with competitive business savings rates and commercial GIC products attracting deposits from small business owners and sole proprietors.

5. **USD account and FX conversions** — While the EQ Bank Card carries zero FX fees, currency conversions between CAD and USD accounts may generate margin through exchange rate spreads.

This model means EQ Bank benefits most when you deposit more money and leave it for longer. Unlike traditional banks that profit from monthly fees, overdraft charges, and penalty fees, EQ Bank's revenue model is aligned with your savings goals — the more you deposit, the more both you and the bank earn.
