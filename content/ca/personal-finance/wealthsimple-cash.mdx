## Not a Bank Account --- Something Better

Wealthsimple Cash is a **hybrid spending and savings account** that combines the daily functionality of a chequing account with the interest-earning power of a high-interest savings account. It is not a traditional bank account, and that distinction matters.

Here is how it works:

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Account Type** | Hybrid spending + savings |
| **Issued By** | Wealthsimple Payments Inc. |
| **Deposits Held At** | Canadian Western Bank (CDIC member) |
| **Interest Rate** | Up to 4% (Premium), lower on free tier |
| **Monthly Fee** | C$0 |
| **Minimum Balance** | C$0 |
| **Card Type** | Prepaid Mastercard (physical + virtual) |
| **Direct Deposit** | Supported (with early access on Premium) |
| **Peer-to-Peer Transfers** | Instant via Wealthsimple, Interac e-Transfer supported |
| **Bill Payments** | Yes |
| **Mobile Wallets** | Apple Pay, Google Pay |

**Why "not a bank" matters:** Wealthsimple itself is not a bank --- it is a financial technology company regulated by various Canadian authorities. Your cash deposits are held at **Canadian Western Bank**, a Schedule I Canadian chartered bank and CDIC member. This means your money receives the same government-backed deposit insurance as money held directly at any Big Five bank. Wealthsimple handles the technology, the app, and the user experience; Canadian Western Bank provides the banking infrastructure and CDIC eligibility.

<Info>
Wealthsimple is regulated by the Canadian Investment Regulatory Organisation (CIRO) for its investment products and by provincial regulators for payments. The Cash product specifically routes deposits through Canadian Western Bank, a fully licensed and CDIC-insured Canadian bank. Your deposits are protected regardless of what happens to Wealthsimple as a company.
</Info>

### How Interest Is Calculated

Wealthsimple Cash calculates interest **daily** on your full balance and pays it **monthly**. There is no minimum balance required to earn interest, and there is no tiered structure where only the first C$X earns the advertised rate. Every dollar in your account earns the same rate from day one.

**Daily interest calculation example (at 4%):**

- **C$10,000 balance:** Earns approximately C$1.10/day = C$400/year
- **C$50,000 balance:** Earns approximately C$5.48/day = C$2,000/year
- **C$100,000 balance:** Earns approximately C$10.96/day = C$4,000/year

This daily accrual matters because your money starts earning interest immediately when you deposit it --- not at the end of the month, not on a minimum balance, not after a waiting period. Funds that arrive via direct deposit at 8 a.m. start earning interest that same day.

## Your Deposits Are Government-Protected

One of the most common concerns about fintech accounts is safety. **Is my money actually protected?** With Wealthsimple Cash, the answer is yes --- but the mechanism is important to understand.

### How CDIC Coverage Works with Wealthsimple Cash

Your cash deposits are not held at Wealthsimple directly. They are held at **Canadian Western Bank (CWB)**, a Schedule I Canadian chartered bank and a member of the **Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation (CDIC)**.

CDIC is a federal Crown corporation that insures eligible deposits at member institutions. If a member bank fails, CDIC protects your deposits automatically --- you do not need to apply or register.

| Coverage Detail | Amount |
|----------------|--------|
| **Per eligible deposit category** | Up to C$100,000 |
| **Deposits in your name** | C$100,000 |
| **Joint deposits** | C$100,000 |
| **RRSP deposits** | C$100,000 |
| **TFSA deposits** | C$100,000 |
| **Total possible coverage (multiple categories)** | C$400,000+ |

<Warning>
CDIC coverage applies **per member institution, per category**. Since Wealthsimple Cash deposits are held at Canadian Western Bank, your coverage limit is calculated based on your total deposits at CWB --- including any deposits you may hold directly with CWB outside of Wealthsimple. If you have a separate savings account directly at Canadian Western Bank, those balances and your Wealthsimple Cash balances share the same C$100,000 limit per category.
</Warning>

### CDIC vs. CIPF: Understanding the Difference

Wealthsimple offers two types of protection across its products:

| Protection | Covers | Limit | Applies To |
|-----------|--------|-------|-----------|
| **CDIC** | Cash deposits at partner bank | C$100,000 per category | Wealthsimple Cash |
| **CIPF** | Securities if Wealthsimple becomes insolvent | C$1,000,000 per category | Wealthsimple Investing |

**CDIC** protects your cash deposits if Canadian Western Bank fails. **CIPF** protects your investment securities (stocks, ETFs) if Wealthsimple's brokerage arm becomes insolvent. These are separate protections that cover different products within the Wealthsimple ecosystem.

<Info>
CDIC coverage is automatic. You do not need to apply, register, or pay a premium. If Canadian Western Bank were to fail, CDIC would reimburse eligible depositors within days. This is the same protection that covers deposits at TD, RBC, BMO, Scotiabank, and CIBC.
</Info>
