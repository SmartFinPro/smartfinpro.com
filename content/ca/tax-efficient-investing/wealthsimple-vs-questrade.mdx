# Wealthsimple vs Questrade 2026: Which Canadian Platform Is Better?

<AnswerBlock
  question="Wealthsimple vs Questrade: Which is better?"
  answer="Wealthsimple is better for hands-off investors wanting automated portfolios (robo-advisor) with no trading fees on stocks/ETFs. Questrade is better for active traders and DIY investors who want control over individual investments, advanced tools, and lower ongoing fees for large portfolios."

Choosing between Wealthsimple and Questrade is one of the most common decisions facing Canadian investors. Both are leading platforms, but they serve fundamentally different investor types and strategies.

Wealthsimple excels as a robo-advisor with automated, professionally managed portfolios perfect for hands-off investors. Questrade shines as a self-directed platform offering granular control, advanced tools, and rock-bottom fees for active traders.

This comprehensive comparison breaks down fees, features, account types, investment options, and user experience to help you choose the platform that best fits your investing style and financial goals.

**Core Offering:** Robo-advisor with professionally managed, diversified ETF portfolios

- Hands-off, set-and-forget approach

**Best For:**
- Active traders
- DIY investors who want full control
- Those building custom portfolios
- Experienced investors seeking low fees

**Key Feature:** Free ETF purchases, low commission trades, advanced charting and research tools.

## Fee Comparison: The Critical Difference

Fees are where these platforms diverge most significantly.

### Wealthsimple Fee Structure

**Management Fees:**
- **Wealthsimple Invest:** 0.50% annually on balances under $100,000, 0.40% on amounts over $100,000
- **Wealthsimple Generation:** 0.50% (includes estate planning, insurance, financial planning)
- **Wealthsimple Premium:** 0.40% + $25/month (priority support, lower fees start at $1)

**Trading Fees (Wealthsimple Trade):**
- Canadian stocks/ETFs: **$0**
- US stocks/ETFs: **$0** (but 1.5% currency conversion fee each way)
- Options: Not available
- Mutual funds: Not available

**Other Fees:**
- Account transfer out: $0
- Partial transfer: $0
- RRSP withdrawal: $0
- Currency conversion: 1.5% (USD ↔ CAD)

**Example Cost (Wealthsimple Invest):**
- $50,000 portfolio: $250/year (0.50%)
- $150,000 portfolio: $540/year (0.36% blended)
- $500,000 portfolio: $2,100/year (0.42%)

### Questrade Fee Structure

**Management Fees:**
- **Self-directed accounts:** $0 management fee
- **Questrade Portfolio IQ (robo-advisor):** 0.20%–0.25% + ETF fees

**Trading Fees:**
- **ETF purchases:** $0 (market-leading feature)
- **ETF sales:** $4.95–$9.95
- **Canadian stocks:** $4.95–$9.95 per trade
- **US stocks:** $0.01/share (min $4.95, max $9.95)
- **Options:** $9.95 + $1/contract

**Other Fees:**
- Account transfer out: $150 (often reimbursed by receiving institution)
- Partial transfer: $150
- Inactivity fee: $24.95/quarter if no trades and balance &lt;$5,000
- Currency conversion (Norbert's Gambit): ~$10 total vs 1.5% at Wealthsimple

**Example Cost (Self-Directed):**
- $50,000 portfolio: $0/year management (only trading commissions)
- $150,000 portfolio: $0/year management
- $500,000 portfolio: $0/year management

## Tax Efficiency Features

### Wealthsimple Tax Optimization

**Automated Features:**
- **Tax-loss harvesting** (Premium tier): Automatically sells losing positions to realize capital losses, offsetting gains
- **Account type optimization:** Recommends which account types to use for tax efficiency
- **Dividend distribution timing:** Manages to minimize taxable events

**Tax Documents:**
- T3 (investment income)
- T5 (investment income)
- T4RSP (RRSP contributions)
- T4RIF (RRIF withdrawals)
- All available by end of February

### Questrade Tax Optimization

**Manual Control:**
- Full control over when to sell (realize gains/losses)
- Can manually harvest tax losses
- Choose specific lots to sell (FIFO, LIFO, specific identification)

**Tax-Efficient Strategies:**
- **Norbert's Gambit:** Convert CAD ↔ USD at ~$10 cost vs 1.5% at banks/Wealthsimple
- Hold US-listed ETFs in RRSP to avoid 15% foreign withholding tax
- Strategic withdrawal order from multiple account types

**Tax Documents:**
- Same as Wealthsimple (T3, T5, T4RSP, etc.)
- Available by mid-March

**Winner:** Wealthsimple for automated tax-loss harvesting (Premium). Questrade for advanced manual control and Norbert's Gambit.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Which is cheaper: Wealthsimple or Questrade?

**Questrade is cheaper** for most investors:
- **DIY investors:** Questrade charges $0 management fee vs Wealthsimple 0.40%–0.50%
- **Robo-advisor:** Questrade Portfolio IQ (0.20%–0.25%) vs Wealthsimple Invest (0.40%–0.50%)
- **Active stock traders:** Wealthsimple Trade ($0 commissions) vs Questrade ($4.95–$9.95/trade)

For a $100k portfolio, Questrade saves ~$250–$450/year.

### 2. Is Wealthsimple good for beginners?

Yes. Wealthsimple is designed specifically for beginners with:
- Simple onboarding (5-minute sign-up)
- Automated portfolio management
- No investment knowledge required
- Beautiful, intuitive interface

Questrade requires more investment knowledge and active management.

### 3. Can I trade US stocks on both platforms?

Yes, but:
- **Wealthsimple Trade:** $0 commission but 1.5% currency conversion each way (3% round-trip)
- **Questrade:** $0.01/share commission + ~$10 currency conversion via Norbert's Gambit

For US stock trading, Questrade is much cheaper if you use Norbert's Gambit.

### 4. Which platform is better for TFSAs?

**Questrade** for most investors:
- $0 management fee (vs 0.50% at Wealthsimple)
- Free ETF purchases maximize contribution room
- No fees eating into tax-free growth

**Wealthsimple** if you want hands-off automated investing and are willing to pay 0.50% for convenience.

### 5. Does Questrade have a robo-advisor like Wealthsimple?

Yes. Questrade Portfolio IQ is a robo-advisor charging 0.20%–0.25% (vs Wealthsimple 0.40%–0.50%). Both offer:
- Automated portfolio management
- ETF-based diversification
- Automatic rebalancing

Questrade Portfolio IQ is cheaper but less user-friendly.

### 6. Which platform has better customer support?

**Questrade:** Better availability (24/5 phone support during market hours)
**Wealthsimple:** Better chat experience, but limited to business hours for non-Premium

Quality is similar for routine queries. For complex issues, phone support (Questrade) is often faster.

### 7. Can I use both Wealthsimple and Questrade?

Yes. Many investors use:
- **Wealthsimple Invest** for hands-off RRSP
- **Questrade self-directed** for TFSA (full control, lower fees)
- **Wealthsimple Trade** for commission-free Canadian stock trading

There's no restriction on using multiple platforms.

### 8. Which is better for RRSPs?

**Questrade** for advanced optimization:
- Hold US-listed ETFs (VTI, VXUS) to avoid 15% foreign withholding tax
- Norbert's Gambit for cheap currency conversion
- Lower management fees (0% vs 0.50%)

**Wealthsimple** for simplicity:
- Automated tax-efficient asset allocation
- No manual management required

### 9. Does Wealthsimple charge fees for TFSA withdrawals?

No. Both Wealthsimple and Questrade charge $0 for TFSA withdrawals. You can withdraw anytime tax-free (though it reduces your contribution room until the next calendar year).

### 10. Which platform is safer?

Both are equally safe:
- **CIPF protection:** Up to $1 million coverage for brokerage accounts
- **Regulated:** Both are registered with provincial securities commissions
- **Segregated accounts:** Your assets are separate from company assets

Both platforms are secure, trustworthy options.
