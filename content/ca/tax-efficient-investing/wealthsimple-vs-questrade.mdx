# Wealthsimple vs Questrade 2026: Which Canadian Platform Is Better?

<AnswerBlock
  question="Wealthsimple vs Questrade: Which is better?"
  answer="Wealthsimple is better for hands-off investors wanting automated portfolios (robo-advisor) with no trading fees on stocks/ETFs. Questrade is better for active traders and DIY investors who want control over individual investments, advanced tools, and lower ongoing fees for large portfolios."
/>

Choosing between Wealthsimple and Questrade is one of the most common decisions facing Canadian investors. Both are leading platforms, but they serve fundamentally different investor types and strategies.

Wealthsimple excels as a robo-advisor with automated, professionally managed portfolios perfect for hands-off investors. Questrade shines as a self-directed platform offering granular control, advanced tools, and rock-bottom fees for active traders.

This comprehensive comparison breaks down fees, features, account types, investment options, and user experience to help you choose the platform that best fits your investing style and financial goals.

## Platform Overview: Different Philosophies

### Wealthsimple: Automated Simplicity

**Founded:** 2014
**Headquarters:** Toronto, ON
**Assets Under Management:** $30+ billion
**Account Types:** TFSA, RRSP, RRIF, RESP, Personal, Business

**Core Offering:** Robo-advisor with professionally managed, diversified ETF portfolios

**Best For:**
- Beginner investors
- Hands-off, set-and-forget approach
- Those wanting professional portfolio management
- Investors prioritizing simplicity over control

**Key Feature:** Automated rebalancing, tax-loss harvesting, and socially responsible investing (SRI) options built in.

### Questrade: DIY Control

**Founded:** 1999
**Headquarters:** Toronto, ON
**Assets Under Management:** $15+ billion
**Account Types:** TFSA, RRSP, RRIF, RESP, Margin, Options, Corporate

**Core Offering:** Self-directed discount brokerage with advanced trading tools

**Best For:**
- Active traders
- DIY investors who want full control
- Those building custom portfolios
- Experienced investors seeking low fees

**Key Feature:** Free ETF purchases, low commission trades, advanced charting and research tools.

## Fee Comparison: The Critical Difference

Fees are where these platforms diverge most significantly.

### Wealthsimple Fee Structure

**Management Fees:**
- **Wealthsimple Invest:** 0.50% annually on balances under $100,000, 0.40% on amounts over $100,000
- **Wealthsimple Generation:** 0.50% (includes estate planning, insurance, financial planning)
- **Wealthsimple Premium:** 0.40% + $25/month (priority support, lower fees start at $1)

**Trading Fees (Wealthsimple Trade):**
- Canadian stocks/ETFs: **$0**
- US stocks/ETFs: **$0** (but 1.5% currency conversion fee each way)
- Options: Not available
- Mutual funds: Not available

**Other Fees:**
- Account transfer out: $0
- Partial transfer: $0
- RRSP withdrawal: $0
- Currency conversion: 1.5% (USD ↔ CAD)

**Example Cost (Wealthsimple Invest):**
- $50,000 portfolio: $250/year (0.50%)
- $150,000 portfolio: $540/year (0.36% blended)
- $500,000 portfolio: $2,100/year (0.42%)

### Questrade Fee Structure

**Management Fees:**
- **Self-directed accounts:** $0 management fee
- **Questrade Portfolio IQ (robo-advisor):** 0.20%–0.25% + ETF fees

**Trading Fees:**
- **ETF purchases:** $0 (market-leading feature)
- **ETF sales:** $4.95–$9.95
- **Canadian stocks:** $4.95–$9.95 per trade
- **US stocks:** $0.01/share (min $4.95, max $9.95)
- **Options:** $9.95 + $1/contract

**Other Fees:**
- Account transfer out: $150 (often reimbursed by receiving institution)
- Partial transfer: $150
- Inactivity fee: $24.95/quarter if no trades and balance &lt;$5,000
- Currency conversion (Norbert's Gambit): ~$10 total vs 1.5% at Wealthsimple

**Example Cost (Self-Directed):**
- $50,000 portfolio: $0/year management (only trading commissions)
- $150,000 portfolio: $0/year management
- $500,000 portfolio: $0/year management

## Tax Efficiency Features

### Wealthsimple Tax Optimization

**Automated Features:**
- **Tax-loss harvesting** (Premium tier): Automatically sells losing positions to realize capital losses, offsetting gains
- **Account type optimization:** Recommends which account types to use for tax efficiency
- **Dividend distribution timing:** Manages to minimize taxable events

**Tax Documents:**
- T3 (investment income)
- T5 (investment income)
- T4RSP (RRSP contributions)
- T4RIF (RRIF withdrawals)
- All available by end of February

### Questrade Tax Optimization

**Manual Control:**
- Full control over when to sell (realize gains/losses)
- Can manually harvest tax losses
- Choose specific lots to sell (FIFO, LIFO, specific identification)

**Tax-Efficient Strategies:**
- **Norbert's Gambit:** Convert CAD ↔ USD at ~$10 cost vs 1.5% at banks/Wealthsimple
- Hold US-listed ETFs in RRSP to avoid 15% foreign withholding tax
- Strategic withdrawal order from multiple account types

**Tax Documents:**
- Same as Wealthsimple (T3, T5, T4RSP, etc.)
- Available by mid-March

**Winner:** Wealthsimple for automated tax-loss harvesting (Premium). Questrade for advanced manual control and Norbert's Gambit.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<details>
<summary>Which is cheaper: Wealthsimple or Questrade?</summary>

Questrade is cheaper for most investors. DIY investors pay $0 management fee at Questrade versus 0.40%–0.50% at Wealthsimple Invest. Questrade's robo-advisor (Portfolio IQ) charges 0.20%–0.25% versus Wealthsimple's 0.40%–0.50%. The only exception is active stock trading, where Wealthsimple Trade's $0 commissions beat Questrade's $4.95–$9.95 per trade. On a $100,000 portfolio, Questrade saves approximately $250–$450 per year.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Is Wealthsimple good for beginners?</summary>

Yes, Wealthsimple is designed specifically for beginners. It features a 5-minute onboarding process, fully automated portfolio management, and an intuitive interface that requires no prior investment knowledge. Questrade's self-directed platform requires you to research, select, and manage your own investments — better suited to investors who already understand asset allocation and ETF selection.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Which platform supports TFSA and RRSP accounts?</summary>

Both Wealthsimple and Questrade support TFSA, RRSP, FHSA, and non-registered accounts. For TFSAs, Questrade has the edge: $0 management fee means all tax-free growth stays in your account rather than going to fees. For RRSPs, Questrade allows you to hold US-listed ETFs (VTI, VXUS) to avoid the 15% foreign withholding tax — a significant advantage that Wealthsimple does not offer in its robo-advisor product.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Questrade have a robo-advisor like Wealthsimple?</summary>

Yes. Questrade Portfolio IQ is a robo-advisor that charges 0.20%–0.25% annually, roughly half the 0.40%–0.50% that Wealthsimple Invest charges. Both offer automated portfolio management, ETF-based diversification, and automatic rebalancing. Questrade Portfolio IQ is cheaper but less polished — Wealthsimple's interface and user experience remain superior, especially for first-time investors.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I use both Wealthsimple and Questrade at the same time?</summary>

Yes, and many Canadian investors do. A common approach is to use Wealthsimple Invest for a hands-off RRSP, Questrade self-directed for a TFSA with full control and lower fees, and Wealthsimple Trade for commission-free Canadian stock trading. There are no restrictions on holding accounts at multiple CIRO-registered platforms simultaneously.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How do I switch from Wealthsimple to Questrade (or vice versa)?</summary>

Both platforms support account transfers. You initiate the transfer at the receiving platform (not the one you are leaving), and the process typically takes 5–10 business days for registered accounts (TFSA, RRSP). Questrade covers transfer fees up to $150 per account. Note that in-kind transfers preserve your existing holdings, while cash transfers liquidate them first — for registered accounts, an in-kind transfer is usually preferred to avoid triggering ACB calculations.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Which platform is safer for my money?</summary>

Both platforms are equally safe. Wealthsimple and Questrade are both CIRO-registered (Canada's national self-regulatory organization) and members of the Canadian Investor Protection Fund (CIPF), which provides up to $1 million in coverage if a member firm becomes insolvent. Your assets are held in segregated accounts, meaning they are legally separate from the company's own assets and protected even if the platform fails.

</details>
