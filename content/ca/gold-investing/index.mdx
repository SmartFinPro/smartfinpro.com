## Gold Market Overview: Why Canadian Investors Are Paying Attention in 2026

Gold continues commanding attention from Canadian investors as the primary hedge against inflation, currency devaluation, and global uncertainty. The precious metal has climbed from approximately CAD $2,600 per troy ounce in 2019 to the current CAD $3,700-$3,900 range in early 2026, representing over 45% appreciation in Canadian dollar terms. This outperformance versus the S&P/TSX Composite Index over the same period highlights gold's value during periods of economic uncertainty and commodity cycle volatility.

The Bank of Canada's monetary policy path remains a key driver. After aggressive rate hikes through 2023-2024, the overnight rate has been gradually reduced, creating uncertainty about the pace and endpoint of easing. Each rate decision moves CAD/USD exchange rates, directly impacting CAD-denominated gold prices. When the Canadian dollar weakens, gold priced in CAD appreciates even without underlying USD gold price changes, creating a natural hedge for Canadian investors holding predominantly CAD-denominated assets.

Canadian investors operate within a regulatory framework governed by the [Canadian Securities Administrators (CSA)](https://www.securities-administrators.ca/) for ETF products and [Investment Industry Regulatory Organization of Canada (CIRO)](https://www.ciro.ca/) for brokerage transactions. Physical gold purchases from dealers like Silver Gold Bull are not securities transactions and fall outside CIRO jurisdiction, though consumer protection under provincial legislation applies. The [Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency.html) treats gold gains as capital gains with 50% inclusion, making registered account strategies particularly advantageous. ## Gold ETFs in Registered Accounts: CGL, PHYS, and MNT

Exchange-traded funds represent the most cost-efficient and liquid method of gaining gold exposure for the majority of Canadian investors. The key advantage in Canada is the ability to hold gold ETFs within TFSA and RRSP accounts, sheltering gains from taxation entirely.

The iShares Gold Bullion ETF ([CGL](https://www.blackrock.com/ca/investors/en/products/239489/ishares-gold-bullion-etf-fund)) is CAD-hedged, eliminating currency risk for Canadian investors. CGL tracks the gold price in Canadian dollars with a 0.55% MER. For investors who want USD gold exposure, CGL.C (unhedged) provides direct exposure to both gold price movements and CAD/USD fluctuations.

The Sprott Physical Gold Trust ([PHYS](https://sprott.com/investment-strategies/physical-bullion-trusts/gold/)) offers a unique structure: investors can redeem units for physical gold delivery, bridging the gap between ETF convenience and physical ownership. PHYS charges a 0.40% MER and trades on the TSX. The redemption feature provides comfort for investors who want the option of taking physical delivery, though minimum redemption sizes of approximately 400 oz ($1.5M+ CAD) make this impractical for most retail investors.

The Royal Canadian Mint's Gold ETR ([MNT](https://www.mint.ca/en/invest/gold-exchange-traded-receipts)) provides government-backed gold exposure with a 0.35% MER, the lowest among major Canadian gold products. Each MNT receipt represents a claim on gold stored at the Royal Canadian Mint's vaults in Ottawa, backed by the Government of Canada. This sovereign backing eliminates the institutional counterparty risk present in privately-managed ETFs.

For registered accounts, MNT or CGL are optimal choices depending on whether investors prioritize lowest cost (MNT at 0.35%) or currency hedging (CGL at 0.55%). All three products qualify for TFSA, RRSP, RESP, and RDSP accounts through any Canadian discount brokerage.

## RRSP/TFSA Gold Allocation Strategies

Canadian registered accounts provide the most tax-efficient gold investing framework available globally. Understanding the strategic differences between TFSA and RRSP gold allocation helps maximize after-tax returns.

**TFSA Gold Strategy:** Gold appreciation within a TFSA is completely tax-free upon withdrawal, including capital gains that would otherwise face the 50% inclusion rate. For investors expecting gold to appreciate significantly, the TFSA shields the entire gain. A $50,000 TFSA gold position that doubles to $100,000 produces $50,000 in completely tax-free gains. Outside a TFSA, the same gain would incur approximately $6,250-$13,000 in federal and provincial tax depending on marginal rate.

**RRSP Gold Strategy:** Gold held within an RRSP grows tax-deferred, with withdrawals taxed as ordinary income. This structure benefits investors who expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement. A $100,000 RRSP gold allocation growing at 5% annually for 20 years reaches approximately $265,000, with all growth deferred until withdrawal. The key risk: if marginal tax rates at withdrawal equal or exceed contribution rates, the RRSP provides no tax advantage, merely deferral.

## Costs and Tax Implications for Canadian Investors

Gold investing carries distinct tax treatment in Canada that varies significantly by method and account type, making tax planning essential for maximizing after-tax returns.

Physical gold sold outside registered accounts is treated as a capital gain by the CRA. Under the current rules, 50% of the gain is included in taxable income for the first $250,000 of annual capital gains, with 66.7% inclusion for gains above that threshold (effective since June 2024). A $10,000 gain on physical gold held personally incurs approximately $1,250-$2,500 in federal and provincial tax depending on marginal rate and total annual gains.

Gold ETFs held within registered accounts receive the most favorable treatment. TFSA-held gold appreciates completely tax-free. RRSP-held gold grows tax-deferred with withdrawals taxed as ordinary income. For non-registered accounts, gold ETF gains receive the same capital gains treatment as physical gold.

Gold mining stocks held in a TFSA generate completely tax-free capital gains and dividends. In non-registered accounts, eligible Canadian dividends from TSX-listed miners benefit from the dividend tax credit, making them more tax-efficient than physical gold gains at equivalent pre-tax amounts.

<Tip>For most Canadian investors, the optimal tax strategy is: hold gold ETFs (CGL or MNT) inside your TFSA for tax-free growth, and hold gold mining stocks (AEM, ABX) inside your RRSP where dividends compound tax-deferred. Physical gold outside registered accounts should be reserved for amounts exceeding TFSA/RRSP contribution room.</Tip>
