## EQ Bank Business: Zero-Fee Banking for Canadian SMBs

EQ Bank made its most significant product move yet when it launched its Business Account nationwide in October 2025 — bringing its signature zero-fee, high-interest banking model to Canadian small businesses for the first time. As the digital banking trade name of [Equitable Bank](https://www.eqbank.ca/), a federally regulated Schedule I chartered bank and [CDIC member](https://www.cdic.ca/your-coverage/list-of-member-institutions/), EQ Bank occupies a rare position in the Canadian market: a true challenger bank with the regulatory backing and deposit insurance of a traditional institution. With an estimated 600,000+ personal banking customers already on its platform, the business product launch gave Canadian freelancers, sole proprietors, and SMBs a compelling new alternative to fee-heavy traditional bank [business accounts](/ca/business-banking/).

After testing the platform for five months since launch (October 2025 – February 2026), processing over 120 transactions through the business account, we found that EQ Bank Business delivers genuine value for businesses focused on domestic CAD operations. The zero-fee model is exactly what it claims — no monthly charges, no per-transaction costs for e-Transfers, and competitive interest earned on idle deposits. However, businesses with significant international payment needs will find the platform insufficient as a standalone solution. For those requiring multi-currency capabilities, pairing EQ Bank with [Wise Business](/ca/business-banking/wise-business-review) creates the optimal configuration for Canadian SMBs.

## Key Features for Canadian Businesses

### 1. Zero Monthly Fees

The elimination of monthly account fees is EQ Bank Business's headline feature. A sole proprietor running a modest volume of transactions through a traditional bank business account typically pays C$15–35/month in base account fees before any per-transaction charges. Over a year, that is C$180–420 in fees eliminated simply by switching to EQ Bank. Unlike some "free" business accounts that impose minimum balance requirements or hidden per-transaction costs, EQ Bank's fee-free model has been consistently confirmed by users as straightforward and honest. There is no catch — the account is genuinely free for domestic CAD banking operations.

The fee structure is particularly compelling when compared to the Big Five Canadian banks. [RBC](https://www.rbcroyalbank.com/business/) charges C$6–35+/month depending on the plan, with additional per-transaction fees for e-Transfers. [TD](https://www.td.com/ca/en/small-business/) similarly charges C$5.95–19.95/month with C$1.00 per e-Transfer. [Scotiabank](https://www.scotiabank.com/ca/en/small-business.html) and [BMO](https://www.bmo.com/en-ca/main/business/) follow comparable pricing structures. For a business doing even moderate transaction volumes, the cumulative savings from switching to EQ Bank add up quickly.

### 2. Free Unlimited Interac e-Transfers

Free unlimited Interac e-Transfers are included with every EQ Bank Business account — a critical feature for Canadian SMBs paying suppliers, contractors, or receiving client payments domestically. Traditional bank business accounts typically charge C$1.00–1.50 per e-Transfer, meaning businesses doing 50 transfers per month save C$50–75 monthly (C$600–900/year). For high-volume freelancers and agencies receiving dozens of client payments monthly, this single feature can justify the switch entirely.

The e-Transfer functionality works exactly as you would expect — send and receive money instantly to any Canadian bank account using an email address or phone number. Transaction limits are competitive with major banks, and the processing is reliably fast. During our five-month testing period, we found e-Transfers consistently arrived within minutes, matching the performance of [Interac's real-time payment rails](https://www.interac.ca/en/consumers/products/interac-e-transfer/).

### 3. Interest on Business Deposits

EQ Bank's most distinctive feature is the interest paid on business deposits — a benefit essentially unheard of with major Canadian bank business accounts. The Big Five banks pay negligible or zero interest on business chequing balances. EQ Bank's business account rates are competitive with their personal HISA offerings, which as of early 2026 sit in the 3.00–3.25% range tied to the [Bank of Canada overnight rate](https://www.bankofcanada.ca/core-functions/monetary-policy/key-interest-rate/).

For Canadian businesses parking operating cash, payroll reserves, or GST/HST remittances in their business account, this means real passive income. A C$25,000 operating reserve earns approximately C$750/year in interest — money that would earn nothing at RBC, TD, or Scotiabank. For businesses with larger reserves of C$50,000–100,000, the interest income becomes even more significant at C$1,500–3,250/year, approaching the CDIC coverage limit.

### 4. CDIC Deposit Insurance

Unlike fintech competitors operating in Canada such as [Wise](https://wise.com/ca/), [Airwallex](https://www.airwallex.com/ca), and [Mercury](https://mercury.com/), EQ Bank Business deposits are [CDIC insured](https://www.cdic.ca/) up to C$100,000 per depositor per category. For businesses holding significant operating reserves, this regulatory protection is a meaningful distinction. CDIC coverage applies automatically — no registration or fee is required. In the event of institutional failure, the Canadian government guarantee protects eligible deposits, providing a level of safety that payment-focused fintechs simply cannot match.

### 5. Digital-First Infrastructure

EQ Bank Business is managed entirely through the EQ Bank app and web portal. Features include real-time balance visibility, transaction history export, and integration with accounting software for reconciliation. The platform supports both personal and business accounts within a single login, simplifying cash management for sole proprietors who maintain both. During our testing, the app performed reliably with no outages or transaction delays.

## EQ Bank Business Features Deep-Dive

### Multi-Currency Capabilities: The Key Limitation

While EQ Bank Business handles CAD deposits with ease, its multi-currency functionality — particularly for international payments — is more limited than specialists like Wise Business. EQ Bank offers standard bank FX rates for currency conversion, which typically include a markup of 1.5–3.0% over the mid-market rate. For businesses with significant international supplier or client volumes, this makes EQ Bank considerably more expensive than Wise, which offers transparent mid-market rates with markups of 0.45–0.60% per transaction.

The practical implication is that most Canadian businesses with international payment needs will use EQ Bank as their primary domestic account and supplement with Wise Business for cross-border transfers. This dual-account approach is increasingly common among Canadian SMBs — EQ Bank handles the CAD side (e-Transfers, deposit interest, bill payments) while Wise handles the international side (70+ currencies, multi-currency account, mid-market FX). If you are also trading currencies through a [forex broker in Canada](/ca/forex/), keeping your business FX separate on Wise gives you cleaner cost tracking.

### Domestic Payment Infrastructure

EQ Bank's domestic payment capabilities are comprehensive for a digital-only bank. Interac e-Transfers are the primary payment rail, and they work flawlessly for both sending and receiving. The platform also supports bill payments to Canadian billers, which covers utilities, government payments including CRA tax remittances, and supplier invoices that accept bill payment. Wire transfers for larger domestic payments are supported with free incoming wires.

The absence of cheque-writing capability is worth noting for businesses that still deal with cheque-based suppliers or landlords, though this is increasingly rare in the Canadian business landscape. Direct deposit functionality is available for payroll, though the integration with major Canadian payroll platforms like [Wagepoint](https://wagepoint.com/) and [ADP](https://www.adp.ca/) requires manual setup rather than native integration.

### Accounting Software Integration

EQ Bank Business supports transaction history export in standard CSV and PDF formats, which can be imported into [QuickBooks](https://quickbooks.intuit.com/ca/), [Xero](https://www.xero.com/ca/), [FreshBooks](https://www.freshbooks.com/en-ca/), or [Wave](https://www.waveapps.com/) for reconciliation. However, unlike Wise Business which offers native two-way sync with QuickBooks and Xero, EQ Bank's integration is manual — you export transactions and import them into your accounting software. For businesses processing high transaction volumes, this manual reconciliation step adds operational overhead that more integrated platforms eliminate.

<Warning>
**International Payment Limitation:** EQ Bank Business is not designed for regular international payments. If your business sends more than 5 international transfers per month, you will save significantly by using Wise Business (mid-market FX with 0.45–0.60% fees) instead of EQ Bank's standard bank rates (1.5–3.0% markup). Consider pairing both accounts for optimal cost management.
</Warning>

## How EQ Bank Makes Money

Understanding how EQ Bank generates revenue helps explain why the Business Account can be offered at zero cost. Unlike traditional banks that rely heavily on fee income from business accounts, EQ Bank's parent Equitable Bank earns its revenue through fundamentally different channels. This transparency matters because it reveals whether the zero-fee model is sustainable or a temporary promotional strategy.

1. **Net interest margin on lending** — Equitable Bank is one of Canada's largest non-Big Five mortgage lenders. The deposits gathered through EQ Bank (personal and business) fund Equitable Bank's mortgage origination business. Your C$25,000 business deposit helps fund mortgages at 5–6% while EQ Bank pays you 3.0–3.25% — the spread is Equitable Bank's primary revenue driver.

2. **Interchange fees on card transactions** — While EQ Bank Business does not yet offer a business credit card, the personal banking debit card generates interchange revenue on every purchase. A business card is widely expected in future product updates.

3. **Foreign exchange spread** — When business customers convert currencies through EQ Bank at standard bank rates, the markup generates revenue. This is why Wise's mid-market rates are lower — Wise charges an explicit fee while banks embed the cost in the exchange rate.

4. **Premium product upselling** — Equitable Bank offers GICs, mortgage products, and investment products that personal and business customers may adopt over time, generating additional revenue per customer.

5. **Operational efficiency** — By operating without physical branches, EQ Bank's cost structure is dramatically lower than traditional banks. This efficiency allows it to offer zero fees while maintaining healthy margins — the same C$35/month that RBC charges a business customer, EQ Bank saves by not operating a branch network.

The business model is well-aligned with customer interests: EQ Bank benefits from attracting and retaining deposits, which means the zero-fee, high-interest model is structurally sustainable rather than a short-term promotion. As long as Equitable Bank's lending business remains healthy, the business account's economics remain sound.

Switching to EQ Bank means losing access to these features, and the cost of replicating them through separate providers may exceed the fee savings.
