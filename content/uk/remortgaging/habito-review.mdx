## What Is Habito?

Habito is a **digital mortgage broker** that combines technology with human expertise to help you find and apply for mortgages.

Founded in 2016 by Daniel Hegarty (former head of digital at Wonga), Habito pioneered the online mortgage broker model in the UK. Their platform allows you to:

1. Answer questions online about your circumstances
2. Receive personalised mortgage recommendations from 90+ lenders
3. Speak with FCA-qualified mortgage advisers
4. Submit applications digitally
5. Track progress through their online dashboard

## How Habito Works: The Complete Process

### Step 1: Answer Questions Online (10 Minutes)

You start by answering questions on Habito's website about:
- Why you need a mortgage (buying, remortgaging, moving)
- Property value and location
- Deposit or current mortgage details
- Income and employment
- Monthly expenses
- Credit history

**The platform is intuitive** - it feels more like using a modern app than filling out mortgage forms. Questions adapt based on your answers.

### Step 2: Receive Recommendations (Instant)

Within seconds, Habito's algorithm searches 90+ lenders and shows you:
- Best mortgage deals for your circumstances
- Interest rates and monthly payments
- Arrangement fees and total costs
- Comparison of different products (2-year vs. 5-year fixed, etc.)

**All recommendations are personalised** to your specific situation, not generic best-buy tables.

### Step 3: Speak with an Expert Adviser (Optional but Recommended)

Once you see recommendations, you can book a call with an FCA-qualified mortgage adviser. They will:
- Explain the recommendations in plain English
- Answer your questions
- Discuss your specific circumstances
- Provide regulated advice
- Help you choose the right product

**Calls are available:** Monday-Friday 9am-8pm, Saturday 9am-5pm, Sunday 10am-4pm.

**Average wait time:** Same day or next day for phone appointments.

### Step 4: Submit Application (30-60 Minutes)

If you choose to proceed, your adviser will:
- Guide you through the full application
- Upload your documents to the platform
- Submit everything to the chosen lender
- Chase the lender for progress

**Most of the work is done for you.** You just need to provide documents (payslips, bank statements, ID) and answer additional questions.

### Step 5: Track Progress Online

Habito's dashboard shows:
- Application status (submitted, in progress, offer received, completed)
- Outstanding tasks (documents needed, forms to sign)
- Estimated timeline
- Messages from your adviser

**You can log in any time** to check progress without calling or emailing.

### Step 6: Completion

Once the lender approves:
- Habito's conveyancing partners handle legal work (usually free)
- Lender conducts valuation
- Mortgage offer issued
- Completion day arranged
- Funds transferred

**Timeline:** 4-8 weeks from application to completion (typical for all mortgages, not just Habito).

## Fees: How Much Does Habito Cost?

**Habito charges £0 in broker fees.**

There are no:
- Application fees
- Advice fees
- Arrangement fees (from Habito - lenders may charge their own fees)
- Hidden charges

**How do they make money?**

Habito earns commission from lenders when you complete a mortgage. This is standard across the entire mortgage broker industry - traditional brokers also earn lender commission.

**Important:** The commission comes from the lender, not from you. Your interest rate is exactly the same whether you go through Habito or apply direct to the lender.

### What About Lender Fees?

While Habito is free, lenders themselves charge:
- **Arrangement fees:** £0-£2,000 (also called product fees or booking fees)
- **Valuation fees:** Usually free for remortgages; £0-£400 for purchases
- **Legal fees:** Often free (included by lender); otherwise £300-£800

**Habito's recommendations clearly show all lender fees**, so you can compare total costs, not just interest rates.

## Common Questions About Habito

### Is Habito FCA-regulated?

Yes, Habito is fully FCA-authorised. Their FCA registration number is 723454. You can verify this on the FCA Register at register.fca.org.uk.

All Habito advisers are FCA-qualified (CeMAP or equivalent) and provide regulated mortgage advice.

### Does Habito check my credit?

Yes, but initially they do a **soft credit check** which doesn't affect your credit score. This is used to provide recommendations.

If you proceed with an application, the lender will do a **hard credit check** which does appear on your credit report. This is standard for all mortgage applications.

### Can Habito help if I have bad credit?

It depends on the severity. Habito can help with:
- Minor issues (1-2 missed payments over 2 years ago)
- Satisfied defaults from 3+ years ago
- Small CCJs from 4+ years ago

For severe credit issues (recent bankruptcies, multiple recent CCJs, ongoing payment problems), a specialist bad credit broker may be better suited.

### How long does the process take?

- **Initial recommendations:** 10 minutes
- **Adviser call:** Same day or next day
- **Application submission:** 30-60 minutes
- **Mortgage offer:** 1-3 weeks (lender-dependent)
- **Completion:** 4-8 weeks total (4-6 weeks for remortgage, 6-10 weeks for purchase)

These timelines are standard across all brokers and direct applications.

### Can I use Habito if I'm self-employed?

Yes, Habito helps many self-employed customers. You'll need:
- 2-3 years' accounts or SA302 tax calculations
- Business bank statements
- Proof of ongoing contracts (if contractor)

Habito accesses specialist self-employed lenders like Atom Bank, Foundation Home Loans, and Kensington.

### What if I'm not happy with Habito's service?

Habito has a formal complaints procedure:
1. Contact your adviser or customer service
2. If not resolved, escalate to complaints team
3. If still unresolved, escalate to Financial Ombudsman Service

In practice, **4% complaint rate** based on reviews (low for the industry).

### Does Habito offer ongoing support after completion?

Yes, Habito monitors your mortgage and will contact you 6 months before your fixed rate ends to help you remortgage again. This ongoing service is free.
