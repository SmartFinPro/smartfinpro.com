## What Is Habito?

Habito is a digital mortgage broker that combines technology with human expertise to help UK homeowners find and apply for mortgages. Founded in 2016 by Daniel Hegarty, the platform has helped over 50,000 customers secure better mortgage deals by searching across more than 90 lenders simultaneously. Unlike traditional brokers who rely on paper-based processes and office visits, Habito allows you to answer questions online, receive personalised recommendations within minutes, and track your entire application through a real-time dashboard. The platform pairs this technology with FCA-qualified human advisers who are available by phone to explain recommendations, answer questions, and guide you through the full application process.

The mortgage market in the UK can be overwhelmingly complex, with hundreds of products across dozens of lenders, each with different rates, fees, and eligibility criteria. Most homeowners simply accept their current lender's product transfer when their fixed rate ends, often paying hundreds of pounds more per month than necessary. Habito was designed to solve this problem by making whole-of-market comparison accessible, fast, and completely free. Their algorithm searches deals from 90+ lenders and presents results personalised to your specific circumstances, not generic best-buy tables. According to their published data, customers who switch through Habito save an average of £3,180 per year compared to staying on their lender's standard variable rate or accepting a product transfer without shopping around.

## FCA Regulation & Consumer Protection

Understanding the regulatory framework behind any mortgage broker is essential before entrusting them with one of the largest financial decisions of your life. Habito is fully authorised and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority under firm reference number 723454, which means they must comply with strict consumer protection standards including affordability assessments, transparent pricing, and fair treatment obligations. All Habito advisers hold CeMAP qualifications or equivalent, ensuring they meet the FCA's competency requirements for providing regulated mortgage advice. You can verify Habito's regulatory status at any time on the FCA Register at register.fca.org.uk by searching their reference number.

As an FCA-regulated broker, Habito must perform affordability assessments before recommending products, provide clear fee disclosures, and adhere to the FCA's Treating Customers Fairly principles across all interactions. If you are unhappy with Habito's service, you have the right to escalate complaints to the Financial Ombudsman Service free of charge. The Consumer Credit Act 1974 also provides a 14-day cooling-off period on mortgage agreements, giving you a statutory right to withdraw without penalty after signing.

## Fees and Costs

Habito charges absolutely zero in broker fees for standard mortgage advice and applications. There are no application fees, advice fees, arrangement fees from Habito, or hidden charges of any kind. This fee-free model applies whether you are remortgaging, purchasing your first home, or moving to a new property. Habito earns its revenue through commission paid by the lender when you complete a mortgage, which is standard practice across the entire UK mortgage broker industry. Critically, the commission comes from the lender rather than from you, and your interest rate is exactly the same whether you apply through Habito or go directly to the lender.

While Habito itself is free, the lenders you borrow from may charge their own fees. Arrangement fees, also called product fees or booking fees, typically range from £0 to £2,000 depending on the lender and product. Valuation fees are usually free for remortgages but can cost £0-£400 for purchases. Legal fees for conveyancing are often included free by the lender for remortgages, but otherwise range from £300 to £800. Habito's recommendations clearly display all lender fees alongside interest rates, enabling you to compare total costs rather than being misled by headline rates alone. The one exception to Habito's free model is their Habito One product, a fixed-for-life mortgage that does carry an arrangement fee, though this is a niche product rather than their standard offering.

## The Habito Remortgaging Process: Step-by-Step

Remortgaging through Habito follows a clearly structured process that takes most borrowers from initial enquiry to mortgage offer within 2–4 weeks, and to full completion within 4–8 weeks. Here is exactly what happens at each stage.

**Step 1 — Online questionnaire (10–15 minutes).** You visit habito.com and answer questions about your property value, outstanding mortgage balance, remaining term, current lender, employment type, income, and financial commitments. The questionnaire adapts based on your answers — a salaried PAYE applicant completes fewer steps than a self-employed contractor or portfolio landlord. At the end, Habito's algorithm searches across 90+ lenders and presents personalised product recommendations with full cost breakdowns including arrangement fees, monthly payments, and total cost over the product term.

**Step 2 — Adviser call (20–40 minutes).** Once you have reviewed the initial recommendations, you book a call with an FCA-qualified CeMAP-certified mortgage adviser. Adviser appointments are typically available same-day or next-day through Habito's online booking system. The call covers any questions about the recommended products, your specific circumstances, and what documentation will be needed. Your adviser will explain the difference between products in plain English — not financial jargon — and help you select the right combination of rate, term, and product type for your situation.

**Step 3 — Document submission.** Habito's secure digital platform allows you to upload identification documents, recent payslips (usually last 3 months), last 3 months' bank statements, and self-employed accounts or SA302s as applicable. Documents go through Habito's case manager before being forwarded to the chosen lender, reducing back-and-forth between you and the lender directly. For remortgages specifically, no solicitor is typically needed for a standard like-for-like remortgage — the lender handles the legal work at no charge to you.

**Step 4 — Lender submission and mortgage offer.** Your completed application is submitted to the lender digitally. Standard residential remortgage applications receive a mortgage offer from the lender within 1–3 weeks. Habito's online dashboard provides real-time application tracking, showing exactly which stage your application is at and what outstanding requirements remain — eliminating the frustrating experience of chasing brokers for status updates.

**Step 5 — Completion.** For like-for-like remortgages with no additional borrowing, completion typically follows the mortgage offer within 1–2 weeks. Your new lender repays the existing mortgage, and the new product takes effect. Habito's case manager coordinates the completion and confirms the new monthly payment and product start date. No branch visits are required at any stage.

## Habito vs Going Direct to a Lender

The most common alternative to using Habito is accepting a product transfer from your existing lender or applying directly to a new lender online. Both shortcuts cost the typical UK homeowner thousands of pounds in unnecessary interest payments.

**Product transfers — the convenient trap.** When your fixed rate ends, your lender will typically write to you 3–6 months in advance offering a product transfer: a new fixed rate on your existing loan without a formal application. The process is fast — often completed online in minutes — and there is no affordability reassessment if your circumstances have not changed significantly. The problem is that product transfer rates are almost never the most competitive rates in the market. Lenders reserve their best rates for new customers, not existing ones. Habito's data shows that customers who switch through their whole-of-market comparison save an average of £3,180 per year versus staying on their lender's standard variable rate or accepting a product transfer without shopping around.

**Applying direct — partial market view.** You could independently research and apply directly to a new lender. This avoids broker commission costs to the lender, though any commission saving does not flow through to your rate — lender rates are identical whether you apply through a broker or directly. The challenge is that searching 90+ lenders individually, understanding their different eligibility criteria, specialist products, and true total costs (including arrangement fees, legal fee cashback, and free valuations) is a substantial research task. You will almost certainly miss products you qualify for at smaller building societies, specialist lenders, or new entrants to the market.

**Habito's whole-of-market advantage.** A whole-of-market broker like Habito has access to products that are not available directly to the public — including broker-exclusive deals negotiated with lenders. The algorithm applies your specific criteria (LTV, income type, credit history, property type) against every available product simultaneously, producing recommendations that reflect genuine eligibility rather than generic best-buy tables. Since Habito's service is free to the borrower (commission is paid by the lender upon completion), using Habito carries zero financial downside compared to going direct. You cannot get a worse rate through Habito than applying directly — you can only find better options you would have missed.

**Hidden costs of DIY remortgaging.** When remortgaging to a new lender without broker support, borrowers frequently miss or underestimate: arrangement fees (£0 to £2,000 depending on the product), valuation fees (£0 to £400, though most lenders offer free valuations for remortgages), legal fees (£300 to £800, though many lenders include free legal work for standard remortgages), and the cost of choosing a lower-rate product with a higher arrangement fee that is actually more expensive over the product term. Habito's comparison displays total cost over the product term — not just headline rates — giving you an accurate picture before you commit.
