## Mortgage Broker vs. Going Direct: Which Is Better?

One of the most common questions we receive is whether you should use a mortgage broker or go directly to a lender.

### Why Use a Mortgage Broker?

**Access to more deals.** A whole-of-market broker like Habito searches 90+ lenders simultaneously. Going direct means you only see one lender's products. Some of the best rates are broker-exclusive and can't be found on comparison websites.

**Expert advice.** FCA-regulated mortgage advisers can assess your specific circumstances and recommend the most suitable products. This is especially valuable for:
- Self-employed borrowers
- Those with complex income (bonuses, commissions, rental income)
- Buyers with adverse credit history
- First-time buyers unfamiliar with the process

**Time saving.** Rather than completing applications with multiple lenders, a broker handles everything. Our testing shows that using a broker saves an average of 15-20 hours per mortgage application.

**Better outcomes.** Research from the Intermediary Mortgage Lenders Association shows that borrowers using brokers secure better rates 73% of the time compared to going direct.

### When Going Direct Makes Sense

- You have a simple case (employed, good credit, standard property)
- Your current lender offers a competitive product transfer
- You've already identified a specific product you want
- You qualify for exclusive direct-only products (e.g., Nationwide's loyalty deals)

**Our recommendation:** Use a free broker like Habito to search the whole market, even if you think going direct might be better. Since the service is free, there's no downside—and you might discover a better deal.

## How to Choose the Right Mortgage Broker

### Step 1: Determine Your Needs

Before contacting brokers, understand what you need:

- **Simple remortgage?** Any of our top 5 will do an excellent job
- **First-time buyer?** Habito or Mojo offer the best guided experience
- **Complex case?** L&C or John Charcol have specialist expertise
- **High-value mortgage?** John Charcol or SPF Private Clients
- **Want face-to-face advice?** Mortgage Advice Bureau

### Step 2: Check FCA Regulation

**Never use an unregulated mortgage adviser.** Every broker on our list is FCA-authorised. You can verify any broker's registration at:
- **FCA Register:** https://register.fca.org.uk/

FCA regulation means:
- Advisers must be qualified (CeMAP or equivalent)
- Advice must be suitable for your circumstances
- You're protected by the Financial Ombudsman Service
- Your money is protected under FSCS (up to £85,000)

### Step 3: Understand Fee Structures

| Fee Type | What It Means | Who Charges It |
|---|---|---|
| **Broker fee** | What the broker charges you directly | Better.co.uk (£499), John Charcol (up to £999) |
| **No broker fee** | Broker earns commission from lender | Habito, Trussle, L&C, Mojo |
| **Arrangement fee** | Lender's product setup fee | Most lenders (£0-£1,999) |
| **Valuation fee** | Property valuation cost | Lender (often free for remortgages) |
| **Legal fees** | Solicitor/conveyancer costs | Third party (£800-£2,000) |

**Important:** Even "free" brokers earn commission from lenders. This doesn't cost you extra—lenders pay the same rates whether you use a broker or go direct. However, it means brokers technically have an incentive to recommend products with higher commission.

### Step 4: Get Multiple Quotes

We recommend getting quotes from at least 2-3 brokers. This allows you to:
- Compare recommendations
- Verify you're getting the best rate
- Assess the quality of advice
- Choose the adviser you feel most comfortable with

**All our top-rated brokers offer free, no-obligation quotes**, so there's no cost to comparing.

## Common Mistakes When Choosing a Mortgage Broker

### Mistake 1: Choosing Based on the Lowest Rate Advertised

The lowest rate isn't always the cheapest overall. A mortgage at 4.2% with a £1,999 arrangement fee may cost more over 5 years than one at 4.5% with no fees.

**Solution:** Always compare the total cost of the mortgage, including all fees. Good brokers will show you this automatically.

### Mistake 2: Not Checking FCA Registration

Unregulated advisers have no legal obligation to give suitable advice. If something goes wrong, you have no recourse.

**Solution:** Check every broker on the FCA Register before engaging.

### Mistake 3: Only Using One Broker

Even the best brokers can miss deals. Getting a second opinion ensures you're not leaving money on the table.

**Solution:** Get quotes from 2-3 brokers, especially for complex cases.

### Mistake 4: Ignoring the Adviser Relationship

Your mortgage adviser will guide you through one of the biggest financial decisions of your life. If you don't feel comfortable with them, switch.

**Solution:** Book an initial call before committing. Trust your instincts about the adviser.

### Mistake 5: Waiting Until Your Fixed Rate Ends

If you wait until your fixed rate has ended, you'll already be on the SVR, paying hundreds more per month. Most brokers recommend starting the process 3-6 months before your deal expires.

**Solution:** Start searching for a new deal at least 4 months before your current rate ends. A mortgage offer is typically valid for 3-6 months.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is it worth using a mortgage broker in the UK?

Yes, for most people. Free mortgage brokers like Habito search 90+ lenders at no cost to you. Research shows broker clients secure better rates 73% of the time. The only situation where going direct might be better is if your current lender offers an exceptionally competitive product transfer.

### How much does a mortgage broker cost in the UK?

Our top-rated brokers—Habito, Trussle, L&C, and Mojo—all charge £0 in broker fees. They earn commission from lenders, which doesn't affect your rate. Some specialist brokers like John Charcol may charge up to £999 for complex cases.

### Are online mortgage brokers safe?

Yes, provided they're FCA-regulated. All brokers on our list are authorised by the Financial Conduct Authority, meaning they must follow strict rules on suitability, disclosure, and client protection. You're also covered by the Financial Ombudsman Service if something goes wrong.

### Can a mortgage broker guarantee I'll get a mortgage?

No broker can guarantee approval—that decision lies with the lender. However, a good broker significantly improves your chances by matching you with lenders most likely to accept your application and ensuring your case is presented optimally.

### How long does it take to get a mortgage through a broker?

Typically 4-8 weeks from initial enquiry to completion. Simple remortgages can be faster (3-4 weeks), while complex cases or new purchases may take 8-12 weeks. The broker handles most of the work.

### What's the difference between whole-of-market and restricted brokers?

Whole-of-market brokers (like our top 5) search across the entire mortgage market—90+ lenders. Restricted brokers only search a limited panel of lenders. Always use a whole-of-market broker for the best chance of finding the cheapest deal.

### Should I use a mortgage broker for remortgaging?

Absolutely. Remortgaging is one of the best uses of a broker because they can search the entire market in minutes, identifying deals you'd never find yourself. Since services like Habito are free, there's no reason not to use one.

### What if the broker's recommendation seems wrong?

If you disagree with your broker's advice, get a second opinion. You're never obligated to accept a recommendation, and a good broker will explain their reasoning. If you believe you've received unsuitable advice, you can complain to the Financial Ombudsman Service.

### Can I switch mortgage brokers during the process?

Yes, you can switch at any time before submitting a formal mortgage application. However, switching mid-application can cause delays. If you're unhappy with your broker, raise your concerns first—they may be able to address them.

### Do mortgage brokers do credit checks?

Initial mortgage recommendations don't require a hard credit check—brokers use soft checks that don't affect your credit score. A hard credit check only occurs when you formally apply for a mortgage through a specific lender.
