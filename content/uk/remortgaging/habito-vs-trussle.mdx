## Category 1: Fees and Costs

### Habito: £0

Habito charges absolutely nothing in broker fees. Their business model earns revenue from lender commissions—the same commissions that exist whether you use a broker or go direct.

There are no hidden charges, no premium tiers, and no obligation to proceed with any recommendation.

### Trussle: £0

Trussle's standard service is also completely free. Like Habito, they earn lender commissions.

Trussle previously offered a "Trussle Plus" premium service at £299/month, but this was discontinued in 2025. The full service is now free for all customers.

### Verdict: Tie

Both brokers offer genuinely free, whole-of-market mortgage advice. Neither charges hidden fees or requires payment at any stage. This is a dead heat.

## Category 2: Technology and Platform

### Habito's Platform

Habito's technology is its defining advantage. Their platform was built from the ground up by a team of engineers, and it shows.

**The questionnaire experience** takes approximately 10 minutes. Questions are adaptive—they change based on your answers, so you never face irrelevant questions. The interface is clean, modern, and genuinely enjoyable to use.

**Recommendation speed** is instant. Once you complete the questionnaire, Habito's algorithm searches all 90+ lenders and displays personalised results in seconds. Each recommendation shows:
- Monthly payment amount
- Interest rate
- Total cost over the term
- Arrangement fees
- Estimated savings vs. your current deal

**The dashboard** allows you to track your application in real time, upload documents, message your adviser, and see estimated timelines. It works equally well on desktop and mobile.

**Mobile app** — Habito offers a dedicated mobile app for iOS and Android, making it easy to manage your mortgage application on the go.

### Trussle's Platform

Trussle's platform is good, but noticeably a step behind Habito in terms of polish and speed.

**The questionnaire** takes 15-20 minutes—somewhat longer than Habito. It covers similar ground but feels slightly more traditional in its approach.

**Recommendation speed** is fast but not instant. Trussle typically takes a few minutes to generate personalised results, compared to Habito's near-instant delivery.

**The dashboard** is functional and allows document uploading and progress tracking, but lacks the sleekness of Habito's interface.

**No mobile app** — Trussle doesn't offer a dedicated mobile app. Their website is mobile-responsive, but the experience isn't as smooth as a native app.

**Where Trussle shines: Trussle Switch.** This is Trussle's standout technology feature. After your mortgage completes, Trussle continues monitoring the market and alerts you when a better deal becomes available. This is genuinely useful—many homeowners forget to remortgage and end up paying hundreds more per month on their lender's SVR.

### Verdict: Habito Wins

Habito's technology platform is more polished, faster, and offers a mobile app. However, Trussle's automatic remortgage monitoring is a unique and valuable feature that Habito doesn't match. If ongoing monitoring is your priority, Trussle has the edge.

For everything else—initial application, recommendations, dashboard, mobile experience—Habito is superior.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Habito really better than Trussle?

For most UK homeowners, yes. Habito has a better technology platform, higher customer ratings, longer adviser availability hours, and faster processing times. The one area where Trussle is superior is automatic remortgage monitoring through their Trussle Switch feature.

### Are both Habito and Trussle genuinely free?

Yes, both charge £0 in broker fees. They earn lender commissions, which don't affect the rate you receive. There are no hidden charges with either service.

### Do Habito and Trussle access the same lenders?

Both are whole-of-market brokers with access to 90+ lenders, and the overlap is substantial. There may be minor differences in lender panels, but both cover all major UK lenders and most specialist ones.

### Can I trust online mortgage brokers with my financial information?

Both Habito and Trussle are FCA-regulated, meaning they must comply with strict data protection and financial conduct rules. Your information is protected by encryption and regulatory safeguards. Both are as trustworthy as traditional high-street brokers.

### How do Habito and Trussle make money if they're free?

Both earn commissions from mortgage lenders when a customer successfully takes out a mortgage. These commissions are standard in the industry—lenders pay them regardless of whether you use a broker or go direct.

### What if I start with one broker and want to switch?

You can switch at any time before submitting a formal mortgage application. Simply tell your current broker you wish to pause, and start afresh with the other. There's no penalty for switching.

### Do either Habito or Trussle offer insurance?

Both offer home insurance, life insurance, and income protection through partner providers. However, these are optional add-ons—there's no pressure to take insurance from your mortgage broker.

### Which broker is better for buy-to-let?

Neither has a particular advantage for buy-to-let mortgages. Both handle standard buy-to-let cases well. For complex portfolio landlord cases, consider a specialist broker like John Charcol.

### How do Habito and Trussle compare to London & Country?

London & Country (L&C) is an older, more established broker (founded 1987) that's better for complex cases. Habito and Trussle have superior technology but less specialist expertise. For standard mortgages, Habito or Trussle are better. For complex income structures or adverse credit, L&C may be preferable.

### Can first-time buyers use Habito or Trussle?

Absolutely. Both brokers welcome first-time buyers and offer guided processes designed for those new to mortgages. Habito's platform is particularly good for first-timers due to its intuitive interface and detailed explanations.
