## Fees and Costs

### Habito: £0

Habito charges absolutely nothing in broker fees. Their business model earns revenue from lender commissions — the same commissions that exist whether you use a broker or go direct to the lender. There are no hidden charges, no premium tiers, and no obligation to proceed with any recommendation. The service is genuinely free from initial enquiry through to mortgage completion, and you will never be asked to pay for any part of the advice process.

### Trussle: £0

Trussle's standard service is also completely free, with the same commission-based business model as Habito. Trussle previously offered a premium service called Trussle Plus at £299 per month, which provided dedicated adviser access and priority processing, but this was discontinued in 2025 and the full service is now available to all customers at no cost.

### Verdict: Tie

Both brokers offer genuinely free, whole-of-market mortgage advice with no hidden fees or premium upsells. Neither charges at any stage of the process. This category is a complete dead heat, and cost should not be a factor in your decision between the two.

## Technology and Platform

### Habito's Platform

Habito's technology is its defining advantage and the area where it pulls furthest ahead of Trussle. Their platform was built from the ground up by a dedicated engineering team, and the investment shows in every aspect of the user experience. The initial questionnaire takes approximately 10 minutes and uses adaptive questioning — the interface changes based on your answers so you never face irrelevant questions.

Recommendation speed is essentially instant. Once you complete the questionnaire, Habito's algorithm searches all 90+ lenders and displays personalised results in seconds, showing monthly payment amounts, interest rates, total cost over the term, arrangement fees, and estimated savings versus your current deal. The dashboard allows you to track your application in real time, upload documents, message your adviser, and see estimated timelines. Habito also offers a dedicated mobile app for iOS and Android, making it straightforward to manage your mortgage application from anywhere.

### Trussle's Platform

Trussle's platform is good but noticeably a step behind Habito in terms of polish and speed. The questionnaire takes 15 to 20 minutes, somewhat longer than Habito, and covers similar ground but with a slightly more traditional approach. Recommendation speed is fast but not instant — Trussle typically takes a few minutes to generate personalised results compared to Habito's near-instant delivery. The dashboard is functional with document uploading and progress tracking capabilities, but it lacks the sleekness of Habito's interface and does not have a dedicated mobile app.

Where Trussle truly shines is its Trussle Switch feature. After your mortgage completes, Trussle continues monitoring the market and alerts you when a better deal becomes available. This is genuinely useful because many homeowners forget to remortgage when their fixed rate ends and wind up paying hundreds more per month on their lender's standard variable rate.

<Tip>
**Technology tip:** If ongoing market monitoring matters more to you than the initial application experience, Trussle Switch is a genuinely unique feature. The optimal approach is to use Habito for your primary application and sign up with Trussle separately for their monitoring alerts.
</Tip>

### Verdict: Habito Wins

Habito's technology platform is more polished, faster, and offers a mobile app. Trussle's automatic remortgage monitoring is a unique and valuable feature, but for the core mortgage application process — initial questionnaire, recommendations, dashboard, and mobile experience — Habito is clearly superior.
