## Are AI Finance Tools Available and Regulated in the UK?

### Current FCA Regulatory Status

The Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) oversees AI tools used in UK financial services through existing regulatory frameworks rather than AI-specific legislation. In January 2026, the FCA launched a long-term review into AI and retail financial services, led by Executive Director Sheldon Mills, examining how AI could reshape consumers' lives and market structures through to 2030 and beyond.

The key frameworks governing AI tool use in UK finance currently include Consumer Duty (PRIN 2A), which requires firms using AI to distribute products or generate client communications to ensure positive customer outcomes; Senior Managers & Certification Regime (SM&CR), which requires senior managers to maintain oversight and accountability over AI deployment; and Operational Resilience rules, which require incident response procedures for AI-enabled services supporting critical business functions.

### UK GDPR and Data Protection

The UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018 apply whenever AI systems process personal financial data. The ICO updated its AI guidance in January 2026, requiring finance firms to complete Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) before deploying AI in client-facing roles. UK data residency matters: Microsoft Copilot for Finance operates within a confirmed UK Data Boundary, and Sage maintains UK-based data centres with ISO 27001 certification.

### Subscription Setup

Most cloud-based AI finance tools offer instant online sign-up with 30-day free trials. Business accounts typically require Companies House registration or sole trader UTR number verification, completed online in under 15 minutes.
