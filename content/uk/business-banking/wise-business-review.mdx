## What is Wise Business?

Wise, formerly TransferWise, is an FCA-authorised electronic money institution providing international money transfer and multi-currency account services to businesses across the United Kingdom. Founded in 2011 by two Estonian entrepreneurs who were frustrated with hidden banking fees, Wise has grown into a publicly listed company on the London Stock Exchange processing over GBP 12 billion monthly for 16 million customers worldwide. The business proposition is fundamentally simple: give customers the real exchange rate and charge a transparent fee, rather than hiding profit inside an inflated spread.

The platform is purpose-built for international commerce. Unlike traditional UK banks that bolt international payments onto domestic infrastructure, Wise built its technology from the ground up to route payments through local banking rails in each destination country. This architectural approach means that a GBP-to-EUR transfer does not actually cross borders as a single international wire. Instead, Wise debits your GBP balance domestically and credits the recipient from a EUR balance held locally, eliminating the correspondent banking chain that drives up cost and slows settlement at traditional banks.

## UK Regulation & Fund Protection

### FCA Authorisation

Wise is authorised by the Financial Conduct Authority as an Electronic Money Institution, which imposes strict requirements on how customer funds are handled. Unlike unregulated payment platforms, Wise must comply with the FCA's safeguarding rules, which require that 100 percent of customer funds are held in segregated accounts at major banks, entirely separate from Wise's own operational capital.

| Regulatory Status | Details |
|-------------------|---------|
| **FCA Registration** | FRN 900507 |
| **Authorisation Type** | Electronic Money Institution |
| **Safeguarding** | 100% of customer funds in segregated accounts |
| **Partner Banks** | Barclays, J.P. Morgan, Wells Fargo |
| **FSCS Protection** | No (e-money, not bank deposits) |
| **LSE Listed** | Publicly traded (WISE.L) since July 2021 |


<Warning>
**Important:** Wise is NOT a bank. Your funds are safeguarded under FCA e-money regulations but are not covered by the Financial Services Compensation Scheme (FSCS) £85,000 guarantee. For businesses holding large GBP balances that require FSCS deposit protection, consider using Starling Business or Tide alongside Wise for your domestic treasury.
</Warning>

The practical implication of safeguarding versus FSCS protection is nuanced. Under FCA safeguarding rules, if Wise were to become insolvent, your funds would be returned to you from the segregated accounts because they are legally ring-fenced from Wise's creditors. This is a strong protection mechanism, but it differs from FSCS coverage in that there is no government-backed guarantee underwriting the process. For most businesses using Wise as a payment rail rather than a savings account, this distinction is less critical because funds typically pass through Wise rather than sitting idle for extended periods.


<Warning>
**Hidden fee alert on large transfers:** While Wise's percentage-based pricing is transparent, be aware that fees on very large transfers (£100,000+) can accumulate significantly. A 0.43% fee on a £250,000 GBP-to-EUR transfer equals £1,075. For transfer volumes at this level, contact Wise's business team directly to negotiate volume-based pricing, which can reduce fees to 0.3% or lower on committed monthly volumes.
</Warning>
