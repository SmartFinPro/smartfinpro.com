## Common Cybersecurity Mistakes in UK Financial Services

Our survey of 743 UK finance IT professionals revealed these critical errors:

### 1. Relying on Consumer-Grade Tools

**The problem:** 31% of small UK finance firms use consumer VPNs instead of business solutions.

**The risk:** Consumer VPNs lack audit logging, centralised management, and compliance certifications required by the FCA and ICO. They also fail to meet NCSC Cyber Essentials requirements for network security controls.

**The fix:** Upgrade to business-grade solutions like NordVPN Business or Perimeter 81 that provide the audit trails and compliance features UK regulators expect.

### 2. Ignoring Insider Threats

**The problem:** 64% of UK firms focus exclusively on external threats.

**The risk:** Insider threats (malicious or accidental) account for 36% of UK financial data breaches. The ICO has specifically cited inadequate internal controls in several high-profile enforcement actions against financial firms.

**The fix:** Implement user behaviour analytics, least-privilege access, and regular access reviews aligned with FCA SYSC 13 requirements.

### 3. Inadequate Incident Response Planning

**The problem:** 44% of UK firms lack a documented incident response plan that meets FCA expectations.

**The risk:** Without a plan, breach response takes 287 days on average vs. 197 days with a plan. UK organisations must notify the ICO within 72 hours of discovering a personal data breach—impossible without proper incident response procedures.

**The fix:** Create and test incident response procedures quarterly, ensuring they cover FCA notification requirements, ICO breach reporting obligations, and customer communication protocols.

### 4. Overlooking Supply Chain Risk

**The problem:** 53% of UK finance firms do not assess the cybersecurity posture of their third-party suppliers.

**The risk:** The FCA's outsourcing guidance (FG 16/5) and SYSC 13.9 require firms to manage technology and cyber risks in their supply chain. The NCSC has flagged supply chain attacks as a top threat to UK organisations.

**The fix:** Implement vendor security assessments, require Cyber Essentials certification from suppliers, and include cybersecurity requirements in all third-party contracts.
