## Key Features for British Investors

### Three Portfolio Types — Different Risk and Cost Profiles

Nutmeg offers three distinct portfolio types serving different British investor needs. Each is available across all account types (ISA, LISA, JISA, pension, general) with risk levels from 1 (conservative) to 10 (aggressive).

**Fully Managed (0.75%):** Nutmeg's investment team actively manages portfolio allocation across asset classes and geography, adjusting based on macroeconomic conditions. Risk levels 1-10 are available. This is the core offering for investors who want professional discretionary management without making individual fund decisions.

**Fixed Allocation (0.45%):** Lower-cost portfolios with a fixed asset allocation that is only rebalanced when it drifts beyond tolerance bands. No active tactical management. The best value option for British investors who want broad index-based exposure without paying for active allocation shifts.

**Smart Alpha by J.P. Morgan (0.75%):** Available since 2022, Smart Alpha applies J.P. Morgan Asset Management's proprietary factor-based ETF selection within Nutmeg's portfolio framework. Performance history is limited given the 2022 launch date.

<Tip>
For cost-conscious investors, Fixed Allocation at 0.45% provides similar diversification to Fully Managed at a significantly lower cost. The active management premium of 0.30% is difficult to justify purely on historical returns. Start with Fixed Allocation and upgrade to Fully Managed only if you specifically want tactical allocation shifts.
</Tip>

1. **Management fees (primary)** — The 0.25-0.75% annual charge is the core revenue source. Nutmeg earns more when your portfolio grows, aligning incentives with positive investment outcomes.

2. **Underlying fund revenue sharing** — Fund providers may share a portion of their management charges with Nutmeg. Standard industry practice but a potential conflict in fund selection.

3. **JPMorgan synergies** — Smart Alpha uses JPMorgan's proprietary ETFs, generating revenue within the JPMorgan group.

Nutmeg charges no trading commissions, entry fees, exit fees, or performance fees.
