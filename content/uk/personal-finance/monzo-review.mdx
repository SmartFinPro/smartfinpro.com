## Monzo: The UK's Largest Digital-Only Bank

Monzo Bank Limited is the UK's largest digital-only bank with over 10 million customers as of 2026. Founded in London in 2015 and authorised as a full UK bank by the [PRA](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/prudential-regulation) in 2017, Monzo has evolved from a prepaid card into a comprehensive personal banking platform offering current accounts, savings pots, personal loans, and premium tiers. The platform expanded significantly since 2024, adding a high-interest savings account via partner bank, personal loans, and the Max tier with travel insurance and cashback.

For British consumers evaluating [personal finance options in the UK](/uk/personal-finance), Monzo's combination of free core banking, industry-leading spending analytics, and genuine [FSCS](https://www.fscs.org.uk/) protection makes it a serious contender alongside [Starling](https://www.starlingbank.com/) and [Chase UK](https://www.chase.co.uk/). After testing the platform for three months, we found that Monzo delivers unmatched budgeting tools and app quality, but savings rates remain a secondary consideration.

## Key Features for British Consumers

### Spending Analytics and Pots

Monzo's core differentiator is automatic spending categorisation. Every transaction is tagged by category (eating out, transport, shopping, bills, groceries, entertainment) in real time. Pots allow you to ring-fence money for specific goals such as holidays, emergency funds, or bills. Salary sorter automatically distributes pay into different Pots on payday, and round-ups sweep spare change from every transaction into a savings Pot.

### International Spending

Monzo uses the [Mastercard](https://www.mastercard.co.uk/) exchange rate with no foreign transaction fee on any tier — one of the best UK debit cards for holiday spending.

| Tier | Abroad ATM Free Allowance | FX Fee |
|------|--------------------------|--------|
| Free | £200/month | 0% on card, 3% above ATM limit |
| Plus | £400/month | 0% on card, 3% above ATM limit |
| Extra | Unlimited | 0% |
| Max | Unlimited | 0% |

### Personal Loans and Overdrafts

Monzo offers personal loans from £200 to £25,000 at competitive APRs, displayed transparently in-app before acceptance. Overdrafts are available at 19-39% APR with costs shown clearly before use — a transparency advantage versus traditional banks.

### Monzo for Teens and Business

Monzo 16 is a current account for UK 16-17 year olds with full features including Pots, spending analytics, and contactless card with parental oversight. Monzo also offers a separate [business account](https://monzo.com/business/) with Xero integration — see the [best business banking UK](/uk/business-banking) guide for coverage.

## FCA Compliance & Security

### Regulatory Status

Monzo Bank Limited is authorised by the [PRA](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/prudential-regulation) and regulated by the [FCA](https://www.fca.org.uk/) and PRA under firm reference number [730437](https://register.fca.org.uk/s/firm?id=001b000003NGHF7AAP) — the same status as traditional high street banks.

| Regulatory Detail | Status |
|-------------------|--------|
| **FCA/PRA Registration** | [FRN 730437](https://register.fca.org.uk/s/firm?id=001b000003NGHF7AAP) |
| **Licence Type** | Full UK banking licence (PRA-authorised) |
| **FSCS Protection** | [Up to £85,000](https://www.fscs.org.uk/) per depositor |
| **Data Processing** | UK GDPR compliant, UK-incorporated |
| **Enforcement Actions** | None active as of March 2026 |

### Compliance History

Monzo has maintained a clean regulatory record with no FCA or PRA enforcement actions. In 2022, Monzo self-reported potential anti-money laundering concerns to the [FCA](https://www.fca.org.uk/), leading to enhanced automated transaction monitoring. The strengthened monitoring is the reason behind the account freeze rate that users report — a trade-off between compliance and convenience.

### FSCS Protection

Unlike e-money institutions, Monzo has held client funds directly as a PRA-authorised bank since 2017. Deposits are protected by the [FSCS](https://www.fscs.org.uk/) up to £85,000 per depositor. Savings via Monzo's partner bank may provide additional separate FSCS coverage up to £85,000 — effectively doubling protection for savers using both products.

<Info>
The distinction between a full banking licence (Monzo, Starling) and an e-money licence is critical. Full banking licence holders protect deposits via FSCS automatically. E-money institutions safeguard funds differently, with potentially weaker protection in firm failure.
</Info>
