### How Your Money Is Protected

1. **FSCS coverage:** Up to **£85,000** per person if Trading 212 were to fail
2. **Segregated accounts:** Client assets are held separately from Trading 212's own company funds, in accordance with FCA Client Money (CASS) rules
3. **FCA regulation:** Trading 212 must comply with conduct of business rules, capital adequacy requirements, and treating customers fairly obligations
4. **Financial Ombudsman Service:** Free complaint escalation for unresolved disputes
5. **Nominee structure:** Your shares are held in a nominee account managed by Trading 212's custodian

### Securities Lending: What You Should Know

Trading 212 may lend your shares to other market participants (such as hedge funds) as part of a securities lending programme. Key points:

- **You can opt out** in your account settings at any time
- If shares are lent, you retain economic ownership and receive dividends
- Trading 212 receives a fee for lending, which helps fund the free platform
- There is a small counterparty risk if the borrower defaults (mitigated by collateral requirements)
- FSCS protection still applies to your holdings

<Info>
**Should you opt out of share lending?** For most ISA investors holding mainstream shares and ETFs, the risk from securities lending is minimal and well-collateralised. However, if you prefer maximum protection and are uncomfortable with the concept, you can disable it in your account settings without any penalty.
</Info>
