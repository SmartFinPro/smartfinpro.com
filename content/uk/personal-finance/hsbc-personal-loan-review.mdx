## Eligibility Requirements

### Basic Requirements

To apply for an HSBC personal loan, you must meet the following criteria:

- **Age:** 18 years or older
- **Residency:** UK resident
- **Income:** Regular income sufficient to afford repayments
- **Bank account:** UK bank account (HSBC current account for preferential rates and same-day funding)
- **Identification:** Valid photo ID
- **Credit history:** No active bankruptcies, IVAs, or CCJs

### Credit Score Guidance

HSBC uses data from all three UK credit reference agencies:

| Credit Agency | Score Range | Minimum for Approval | Score for Best Rate (6.9%) |
|---------------|------------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| Experian | 0-999 | ~750+ | 961+ |
| Equifax | 0-700 | ~400+ | 531+ |
| TransUnion | 0-710 | ~580+ | 628+ |

### Premier & Advance Eligibility

For preferential personal loan rates, you need to qualify for HSBC Premier or Advance banking:

**HSBC Premier requirements (any one of):**
- Annual income of £75,000 or more paid into an HSBC account
- Savings and/or investments of £50,000 or more with HSBC
- UK or international mortgage of £500,000 or more with HSBC

**HSBC Advance requirements:**
- Annual income of £10,000 or more paid into an HSBC current account
- Active HSBC current account with regular use

<Tip>
**Upgrade before applying:** If you hold a standard HSBC current account and meet the Premier or Advance criteria, upgrade your account before applying for a personal loan. The preferential rate can save you hundreds or thousands over the loan term, particularly on larger loans.
</Tip>

## Large Loan Considerations

Borrowing £25,000 or more is a significant financial commitment. Here are the key factors to consider when taking out a large personal loan with HSBC.

### Affordability Assessment

The FCA requires HSBC to verify your ability to comfortably repay any loan. For larger amounts, the affordability assessment is more rigorous:

- **Detailed income verification:** Recent payslips, P60, or tax returns
- **Expenditure analysis:** A comprehensive review of your monthly outgoings
- **Stress testing:** Can you still afford payments if interest rates rise or your income decreases?
- **Existing commitments:** All current loans, credit cards, and financial obligations

### When a Large Unsecured Loan Makes Sense

A large unsecured loan is appropriate for major home renovations, substantial debt consolidation, weddings and events, vehicle purchases, or professional development — any situation where you need significant funding without putting your property at risk.

For any unsecured borrowing above £35,000, HSBC is effectively the only major lender option — making their competitive 6.9% representative APR and Premier discounts particularly valuable.

<Warning>
**Consider independent advice for large loans.** If you are borrowing £25,000 or more, we recommend speaking to an independent financial adviser (IFA) before committing. An IFA can assess whether an unsecured personal loan, secured loan, remortgage, or other product is the most cost-effective solution for your specific circumstances. You can find an IFA through Unbiased.co.uk.

### Overpayment Strategy for Large Loans

With no early repayment charges, HSBC allows you to overpay at any time. On a £40,000 loan at 6.9% APR over 8 years, overpaying by £100/month saves £2,160 in interest, £200/month saves £3,840, and £500/month saves £6,480. For large loans, a disciplined overpayment strategy is one of the most effective ways to reduce your total borrowing cost.
