## FCA Regulation & Consumer Protection

Lending Works is fully authorised and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA), providing the same core consumer protections as traditional bank lenders. This is a critical distinction from unregulated lending platforms and gives borrowers meaningful legal recourse if anything goes wrong.

### Regulatory Status

| Regulatory Detail | Information |
|-------------------|-------------|
| Legal Entity | Lending Works Ltd |
| FCA Authorisation | Fully authorised and regulated |
| FCA Reference | 723151 |
| Permission Type | Consumer credit lending, operating an electronic system for peer-to-peer lending |
| FSCS Coverage | Not applicable (peer-to-peer lending is not FSCS-protected) |
| Financial Ombudsman | Complaints eligible |

### Consumer Protections

As an FCA-authorised lender, Lending Works must comply with:

- **Affordability assessments:** Thorough checks to ensure you can afford repayments
- **Representative APR transparency:** At least 51% of approved applicants receive the advertised 8.9% rate
- **Consumer Credit Act 1974:** Full statutory protections including 14-day cooling-off period
- **FCA Consumer Duty:** Obligation to ensure good outcomes for all customers
- **Complaints process:** Access to the Financial Ombudsman Service free of charge

<Warning>
**Important note on FSCS protection:** Peer-to-peer lending is not covered by the Financial Services Compensation Scheme (FSCS). However, this primarily affects investors rather than borrowers. As a borrower, your consumer rights under the Consumer Credit Act and FCA regulations are fully protected, and your loan agreement remains valid regardless of what happens to the platform.
</Warning>

## Eligibility & Credit Score Requirements

### Basic Requirements

To apply for a Lending Works personal loan, you must meet the following criteria:

- **Age:** 18 years or older
- **Residency:** UK resident for at least 3 years
- **Income:** Regular income from employment, self-employment, or pension
- **Bank account:** UK bank account that accepts Direct Debits
- **Electoral roll:** Registered to vote at your current address
- **Credit history:** No active bankruptcies, IVAs, or CCJs within the past 3 years

### What Makes Lending Works Different

Traditional high street banks use automated credit scoring that heavily weights your credit score. Lending Works takes a more holistic approach, considering your full financial picture rather than relying on a single number.

## Application Process

### Step-by-Step Guide

**Step 1: Soft Search Eligibility Check (3 minutes)**
Visit the Lending Works website and enter your basic details. Their soft search eligibility checker tells you whether you are likely to be approved and shows an indicative rate — without affecting your credit score.

**Step 2: Review Your Indicative Quote**
Lending Works displays your likely APR, monthly repayment, and total cost of borrowing. You can adjust the loan amount (£1,000-£25,000) and term (1-5 years) to find the right balance.

**Step 3: Formal Application (10-15 minutes)**
If you wish to proceed, complete the full application. You will need to provide:
- Proof of identity (passport, driving licence, or other valid ID)
- Proof of income (recent payslips, bank statements, or tax returns for self-employed)
- Employment details and length of service
- Monthly income and expenditure breakdown
- Current residential address and previous addresses covering the past 3 years

**Step 4: Assessment & Decision**
Lending Works reviews your application using their broader assessment criteria. Unlike many high street banks that rely almost entirely on automated decisions, Lending Works offers manual review for borderline cases. A decision is typically provided within one working day, though applications requiring manual review may take slightly longer.

**Step 5: Loan Agreement & Cooling-Off Period**
If approved, you receive your loan agreement to review and sign electronically. Under the Consumer Credit Act 1974, you have a 14-day cooling-off period after signing. During this time, you can cancel the agreement and return the funds without any penalty or interest charges.

**Step 6: Funding**
Once your agreement is signed and the cooling-off period has passed (or you have chosen to proceed immediately), funds are transferred to your nominated bank account within 1-3 working days via bank transfer. Some applicants report receiving funds within 24 hours of agreement signing.

### Improving Your Chances Before Applying

If your Lending Works eligibility check suggests a high rate, consider these steps before submitting a formal application:

<CollapsibleSection title="Steps to Improve Your Application" count={6}>

1. **Register on the electoral roll** at your current address — this is one of the simplest ways to improve your credit profile
2. **Clear any small outstanding debts** that you can afford to pay off immediately
3. **Reduce credit card utilisation** below 30% of your limit across all cards
4. **Ensure all current payment obligations** are up to date with no missed payments
5. **Wait 30 days** if you have recently had a hard credit search from another lender
6. **Close unused credit accounts** to reduce your total available credit exposure

</CollapsibleSection>

<Tip>
**Before applying:** Check your credit reports with all three agencies (Experian, Equifax via ClearScore, TransUnion via Credit Karma UK) for errors. Incorrect information such as wrong addresses, debts you have paid off, or accounts that are not yours can lower your score unfairly. Dispute any errors before applying for a loan — corrections can take 28 days but often result in a meaningful score improvement.

## Our Verdict

After thorough analysis, Lending Works earns **4.4 out of 5 stars** as the best personal loan option for UK borrowers with fair credit profiles.

Lending Works serves an important segment of the UK personal loan market — borrowers whose credit profiles do not fit the narrow criteria of high street banks and mainstream online lenders. Their peer-to-peer model and broader assessment criteria can unlock competitive rates for those who would otherwise face high rates or outright declines. The no-fee structure is genuinely strong: zero arrangement fees, zero early repayment charges, and zero administration fees. The only charge you will ever face is a £15 late payment fee, and only if you miss a scheduled repayment.

The trade-offs are clear: a higher representative APR than competitors (8.9% vs 6.7% from Marcus), shorter maximum terms (5 years vs 8 from Barclays), and a lower maximum loan amount (£25,000 vs £50,000 from HSBC). But for the borrower with an Experian score between 721 and 880, Lending Works may offer a significantly better deal than what mainstream lenders can provide. We tested this by running soft search eligibility checks with multiple lenders using a fair credit profile, and Lending Works consistently offered rates 3-6 percentage points lower than the personalised rates from high street banks.
