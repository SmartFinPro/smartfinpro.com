## ISA Allowance & Tax Benefits

The AJ Bell ISA operates within the standard UK ISA framework, giving every UK resident aged 18 or over the ability to invest up to £20,000 per tax year with all returns — dividends, interest, and capital gains — completely free from UK income tax and capital gains tax. This allowance is set by HMRC and shared across all ISA types you hold, meaning contributions to a Cash ISA, Lifetime ISA, or Innovative Finance ISA all count toward the same £20,000 annual limit.

AJ Bell offers three ISA products: the Stocks and Shares ISA (the core product reviewed here), a Lifetime ISA for first-time buyers and retirement savers aged 18 to 39, and a Junior ISA for children under 18 with a separate £9,000 annual allowance. The LISA adds a 25% government bonus on contributions up to £4,000 per year, making it a powerful tool for younger investors planning a first home purchase (up to £450,000 property value) or supplementing their retirement savings. Cash ISAs and Innovative Finance ISAs are not available through AJ Bell, so investors wanting those products will need to look elsewhere.

For investors with a time horizon of five years or more, the historical evidence strongly favours a Stocks and Shares ISA over a Cash ISA. Based on average annualised returns, a £10,000 investment in a diversified equity portfolio would have grown to approximately £14,200 over five years, compared to £11,300 in a typical Cash ISA. Over twenty years, the gap widens dramatically to roughly £40,400 versus £16,400. Past performance is not a guarantee of future results, but the long-term compounding advantage of equity investing within a tax-free wrapper is one of the most powerful wealth-building strategies available to UK savers.

<Tip>
**Use it or lose it.** You cannot carry forward unused ISA allowance from previous tax years. If you do not use your £20,000 allowance before 5 April, the unused portion is gone permanently. Even investing £100 per month through AJ Bell's regular investment service is better than waiting for a lump sum that may never materialise.
</Tip>

Your investments held with AJ Bell are protected through multiple layers of security. Client assets are held in AJ Bell's nominee company, which is legally separate from the company's own corporate assets. This means that if AJ Bell were to fail as a business, your investments would not form part of the company's estate and should be returned to you. <Warning>

## ISA Transfer & Tax Year Planning

AJ Bell accepts ISA transfers from all UK providers with both cash and in-specie options, and there are no transfer-in fees. A cash transfer involves selling your existing investments, transferring the proceeds to AJ Bell, and reinvesting — this typically takes 15 to 30 working days and means you will be out of the market during the transfer period. An in-specie transfer moves your existing holdings directly without selling, which avoids market exposure gaps but takes longer at four to eight weeks. Online transfer tracking is available so you can monitor progress throughout the process.

Tax year end planning is an important consideration for ISA investors. The £20,000 ISA allowance resets on 6 April each year, and any unused allowance is lost permanently. Setting up a regular monthly investment ensures consistent contributions throughout the year, which also benefits from pound-cost averaging during volatile markets. For investors holding assets in a general (non-ISA) account, a bed-and-ISA strategy — selling investments outside the ISA and rebuying inside — can shelter all future growth from tax, though the sale may trigger capital gains tax if gains exceed the current £3,000 annual CGT allowance (2025/26 tax year).

<Info>
**Bed-and-ISA explained.** If you hold investments in a general (non-ISA) account, you can sell them and immediately rebuy the same investments inside your AJ Bell ISA. This shelters all future growth from tax. Be aware of the capital gains tax implications on the sale — the current CGT annual allowance is £3,000 (2025/26).
</Info>
