## ISA Allowance & Tax Benefits

Every UK resident aged 18 or over can invest up to £20,000 per tax year (6 April to 5 April) inside an ISA, and all returns within the HL ISA are completely tax-free. This includes dividends, interest income, and capital gains. For a higher-rate taxpayer at 40%, the annual tax saving on a £50,000 ISA portfolio generating £3,000 in returns could exceed £1,000 per year. Over a 20-year investment horizon, the compounding effect of those tax savings becomes genuinely transformational for long-term wealth building.

HL offers three of the four ISA types available in the UK. The core Stocks and Shares ISA accepts up to the full £20,000 allowance and provides access to the complete investment range. The Lifetime ISA allows those aged 18-39 to contribute up to £4,000 per year with a 25% government bonus worth up to £1,000 annually, usable towards a first home purchase or retirement from age 60. HL also offers a Cash ISA through its Active Savings service, which provides access to competitive rates from a panel of banking partners. The only type not available is the Innovative Finance ISA, which few mainstream platforms offer.

## Fees & Cost Analysis

The most important consideration when choosing an ISA platform is the total cost you will pay over the lifetime of your investment. HL charges a platform fee of 0.45% per year on funds, which is higher than budget alternatives such as Vanguard at 0.15% or AJ Bell at 0.25%. For a £50,000 fund portfolio, that translates to £225 per year at HL versus £75 at Vanguard — a difference of £150 annually. Over 20 years with compounding, this fee gap can result in a portfolio value difference of several thousand pounds.

However, the fee picture changes significantly for investors who hold shares and ETFs. HL caps the platform fee on shares, ETFs, and investment trusts at just £45 per year, regardless of portfolio size. A £100,000 share portfolio costs the same annual platform fee as a £10,000 one. For investors building diversified portfolios that include individual equities alongside funds, HL can actually be more cost-effective than platforms charging a percentage-based fee on all holdings.

## FCA Regulation & FSCS Protection

Hargreaves Lansdown Asset Management Limited is authorised and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority under reference number 115248. As a FTSE 250 company listed on the London Stock Exchange (ticker: HL.), the firm is subject to comprehensive regulatory oversight including annual financial reporting, client asset audits, and FCA conduct requirements. This level of regulatory scrutiny provides a degree of transparency and accountability that smaller or privately held platforms cannot match.

Your investments are protected by the Financial Services Compensation Scheme up to £85,000 per person if HL were to fail as a firm. Client assets are held in HL's nominee company, which is legally separate from HL's own corporate assets. This means that even in a worst-case insolvency scenario, your investments should be identifiable and returnable. Cash held by HL is ring-fenced in accordance with FCA Client Assets Sourcebook (CASS) rules, and client asset arrangements are independently audited annually. You also have the right to escalate complaints to the Financial Ombudsman Service free of charge.

## ISA Transfer & Switching Guide

HL makes ISA transfers straightforward and offers both cash and in-specie (stock) transfers from other providers. A cash transfer involves selling your existing investments, transferring the cash to HL, and reinvesting — typically taking 15-30 working days. An in-specie transfer moves your holdings directly without selling, provided HL can hold the same investments. This avoids being out of the market during the transfer period and eliminates bid-offer spread costs, though in-specie transfers may take 4-8 weeks to complete.

Many UK investors accumulate ISAs across several providers over multiple tax years. HL's platform makes consolidation straightforward, allowing you to combine old Cash ISAs and Stocks and Shares ISAs from different providers into a single portfolio dashboard. There are no transfer-in fees, and HL provides an online transfer tracker so you can monitor progress. The dedicated transfer support team handles the administrative burden of contacting your existing provider and managing the transfer paperwork. Both current year and previous year ISAs can be transferred, and you can transfer partial holdings from some providers.

<Tip>
**In-specie transfers save money.** If you hold funds that are also available on HL, such as Fundsmith Equity or Vanguard index funds, always request an in-specie transfer. This eliminates the risk of being out of the market during the transfer and avoids any bid-offer spread costs that would apply if you sold and rebought.
</Tip>
