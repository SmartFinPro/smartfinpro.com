## ISA Allowance & Tax Benefits

### The £20,000 Tax-Free Allowance

Every UK resident aged 18 or over can invest up to **£20,000 per tax year** (6 April to 5 April) into ISAs. This allowance is set by HMRC and applies across all ISA providers and types combined. You can allocate the full £20,000 to a Fidelity Stocks and Shares ISA, or split it between different ISA types according to your financial goals.

| ISA Type | Annual Limit | Fidelity Offers? |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Stocks & Shares ISA** | Up to £20,000 | Yes |
| **Cash ISA** | Up to £20,000 | Yes |
| **Lifetime ISA** | Up to £4,000 (within £20,000) | No |
| **Innovative Finance ISA** | Up to £20,000 | No |

### The Power of Tax-Free Compounding

The true value of an ISA reveals itself over time through **compounding within a tax wrapper**. When your investments grow and generate dividends, those returns are reinvested without any tax deduction — unlike a general investment account where capital gains tax (up to 24%) and dividend tax (up to 39.35%) erode your returns year after year. For a higher-rate taxpayer investing £20,000 annually at 7% average growth over 20 years, the difference between an ISA and a taxed general investment account can exceed £130,000 in cumulative tax savings.

<Warning>
**Use it or lose it.** Your £20,000 ISA allowance cannot be carried forward to the next tax year. Any unused allowance on 5 April is lost permanently. Even investing a small amount before the deadline opens an ISA for that tax year, which you can then top up at any point before the following 5 April.
</Warning>

## FCA Regulation & FSCS Protection

Fidelity International is authorised and regulated by the **Financial Conduct Authority (FCA)**, reference number 222525. This regulatory status provides comprehensive protection for UK investors across multiple layers. Your investments are held in nominee accounts entirely separate from Fidelity's own company assets, meaning that if Fidelity were to fail as a firm, your investments would be identifiable and returnable through the normal client asset process.

The **Financial Services Compensation Scheme (FSCS)** provides an additional safety net, protecting you up to **£85,000 per person** in the event that Fidelity fails and your assets cannot be returned through the standard process. It is important to understand that FSCS covers firm failure, not investment losses — if your portfolio drops in value due to market movements, that is investment risk rather than a matter of regulatory protection.

<CollapsibleSection title="Full FCA Protection Details" count={4}>

- **Client asset segregation:** Your investments are held in nominee accounts separate from Fidelity's own company assets, ensuring they are ring-fenced in the event of firm insolvency
- **FSCS protection:** The Financial Services Compensation Scheme protects you up to £85,000 per person if Fidelity fails and your assets cannot be returned
- **FCA conduct rules:** Fidelity must treat customers fairly, provide clear information about fees and risks, and handle complaints within regulated timeframes
- **Financial Ombudsman Service:** You can escalate unresolved complaints to the FOS free of charge, with binding decisions up to £415,000

</CollapsibleSection>

<Warning>
**Capital at risk.** The value of investments within a Stocks and Shares ISA can go down as well as up. You may get back less than you invest. Tax treatment depends on your individual circumstances and may be subject to change in future. FSCS protection covers firm failure only, not investment performance losses.
</Warning>
