## ISA Allowance & Tax Benefits

### The £20,000 Tax-Free Allowance

Every UK resident aged 18 or over can invest up to **£20,000 per tax year** (6 April to 5 April) into ISAs. This allowance is shared across all ISA types:

| ISA Type | Annual Limit | Fidelity Offers? |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Stocks & Shares ISA** | Up to £20,000 | Yes |
| **Cash ISA** | Up to £20,000 | Yes |
| **Lifetime ISA** | Up to £4,000 (within £20,000) | No |
| **Innovative Finance ISA** | Up to £20,000 | No |

### The Power of Tax-Free Compounding

The true value of an ISA reveals itself over time through **compounding within a tax wrapper**. When your investments grow and generate dividends, those returns are reinvested without any tax deduction — unlike a general investment account where capital gains tax (up to 24%) and dividend tax (up to 39.35%) erode your returns.

**Example: £20,000 invested annually for 20 years at 7% growth**
- **Inside ISA:** £877,390 (entirely tax-free on withdrawal)
- **Outside ISA (higher-rate taxpayer):** Approximately £745,780 after capital gains and dividend tax
- **Tax saving: £131,610** over 20 years

<Warning>
**Use it or lose it.** Your £20,000 ISA allowance cannot be carried forward to the next tax year. Any unused allowance on 5 April is lost permanently. Even if you invest just £100 before the deadline, you have opened an ISA for that tax year which can be topped up.
</Warning>

## Understanding Tax-Wrapped Investing

### How the ISA Tax Shield Works

An ISA is a **tax wrapper** — a legal structure that shields your investments from UK tax. Understanding exactly what this means is crucial for maximising your wealth:

**Taxes you do NOT pay inside an ISA:**
- **Capital Gains Tax (CGT):** Normally 10% (basic rate) or 24% (higher rate) on investment profits above the £3,000 annual allowance
- **Dividend Tax:** Normally 8.75% (basic), 33.75% (higher), or 39.35% (additional) on dividends above the £500 annual allowance
- **Income Tax on interest:** No tax on interest from bonds or cash held within the ISA

**The compounding advantage:** Every pound saved in tax is a pound that remains invested and compounding. Over 20-30 years, this tax shelter significantly amplifies your total returns — particularly for higher-rate taxpayers.

<Warning>
**Capital at risk.** The value of investments within a Stocks and Shares ISA can go down as well as up. You may get back less than you invest. Tax treatment depends on your individual circumstances and may be subject to change in future.
</Warning>

## FCA Regulation & FSCS Protection

Fidelity International is authorised and regulated by the **Financial Conduct Authority (FCA)**, reference number 222525. This provides comprehensive protection:

1. **Client asset segregation:** Your investments are held in nominee accounts separate from Fidelity's own company assets. If Fidelity were to fail, your investments would be identifiable and returnable.

2. **FSCS protection:** The Financial Services Compensation Scheme protects you up to **£85,000 per person** if Fidelity fails and your assets cannot be returned through the normal client asset process.

3. **FCA conduct rules:** Fidelity must treat customers fairly, provide clear information, and handle complaints within regulated timeframes.

4. **Financial Ombudsman Service:** You can escalate unresolved complaints to the FOS free of charge.
