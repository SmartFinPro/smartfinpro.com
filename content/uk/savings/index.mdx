## Marcus by Goldman Sachs — Best Easy-Access Account

Marcus UK consistently offers one of the top five easy-access savings rates in the UK market, with zero fees, no minimum balance, and the backing of Goldman Sachs International Bank's [PRA](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/prudential-regulation) banking licence. Eligible deposits are [FSCS](https://www.fscs.org.uk/)-protected up to £85,000 per depositor. The simplicity of Marcus is its primary advantage over competitors: one rate applied from £1, no tiered structure, no introductory bonus period to monitor, and no conditions beyond opening the account and transferring funds.

For British savers who want to optimise cash savings without ongoing management, Marcus requires a single action — open the account online and move your money. Marcus also offers a competitive Cash ISA at the same rate as its easy-access account, which allows savers to shelter up to £20,000 per tax year from income tax entirely. This combination of a strong headline rate and tax-free wrapper makes Marcus a compelling choice for both basic-rate and higher-rate taxpayers who want maximum simplicity from a well-capitalised institution.

The main limitation is the web-only platform with no dedicated mobile app, which may not suit savers who prefer app-based management or automated savings features. Marcus does not offer fixed-term bonds or investment products, so savers looking for a broader financial platform will need to supplement it with additional providers. For those who want a simple, set-and-forget approach backed by one of the world's largest financial institutions, Marcus remains the benchmark easy-access account in the UK market.

## Best Fixed-Term Bonds — Raisin UK Marketplace

For savers who can commit funds for a defined period ranging from 3 months to 5 years, fixed-term bonds offer higher guaranteed rates than easy-access accounts. The trade-off is clear: early withdrawal is not permitted, or incurs a significant penalty that can exceed the interest earned. Savers must therefore be confident they will not need the funds before the maturity date, making fixed-term bonds best suited for money that is genuinely surplus to short-term requirements.

**[Raisin UK](https://www.raisin.co.uk/)** is the most efficient platform for UK fixed-term savings. It operates as a savings marketplace that aggregates fixed-term bond offers from multiple [FCA](https://www.fca.org.uk/)-authorised, FSCS-protected UK banks, allowing savers to compare and access a wide range of fixed rates without opening individual accounts with each bank separately. The platform itself is regulated, and each bond is held with a separate FSCS-protected institution — always verify the underlying bank for each bond you purchase.

**Indicative rates (March 2026):**

| Term | Typical AER Range |
|------|-------------------|
| 3 months | 4.0-4.5% |
| 6 months | 4.3-4.8% |
| 1 year | 4.5-5.0% |
| 2 years | 4.3-4.8% |
| 5 years | 4.0-4.5% |

Direct alternatives to Raisin include Charter Savings Bank, Aldermore, and [Shawbrook Bank](https://www.shawbrook.co.uk/), which offer competitive fixed-term rates outside the Raisin marketplace. For consumers who prefer dealing directly with a single institution, these banks provide FSCS-protected fixed bonds at rates that are often competitive with Raisin's aggregated offers.

<Warning>
**Early access restrictions:** Fixed-term bonds do not permit early withdrawal, or charge a penalty that can exceed the interest earned. Only commit funds you are certain you will not need before the maturity date. If there is any chance you may need the money within the term, keep it in an easy-access account instead.
</Warning>

## NS&I Premium Bonds — Best for Large Balances and Tax-Free Returns

[NS&I](https://www.nsandi.com/) Premium Bonds are backed by HM Treasury with **unlimited protection** — there is no £85,000 cap. This makes them the only UK savings product that eliminates counterparty risk entirely for balances above the FSCS limit, which is particularly important for savers with substantial cash holdings who cannot afford to risk any loss of principal. The maximum holding is £50,000 per person, and the minimum purchase is £25.

Instead of interest, Premium Bond holders enter a monthly prize draw. The estimated prize fund rate is approximately 4.40% AER equivalent (variable, set by NS&I), though individual returns vary significantly — some holders win considerably more, most win less, and a minority win nothing in any given month. Over long periods with a full £50,000 holding, returns statistically approach the prize fund rate. All prizes are **completely tax-free**, regardless of your tax band, which makes Premium Bonds particularly attractive for additional-rate taxpayers at 45% who pay income tax on all savings interest outside an ISA.

Premium Bonds are best suited for three groups: UK savers with balances above £85,000 who need protection beyond the FSCS limit; additional-rate taxpayers for whom the tax-free prize structure is particularly valuable compared to taxable savings accounts; and savers who prioritise absolute capital safety over guaranteed returns. For balances under £85,000 where FSCS protection already applies, an easy-access account at a competitive AER will typically deliver higher guaranteed returns than the probabilistic prize structure of Premium Bonds.

For British consumers who can commit to a fixed monthly deposit, regular savings accounts offer the highest headline AERs in the UK market — often 5-7% AER — but with strict monthly deposit limits that typically range from £25 to £500 per month. These accounts are designed to encourage consistent saving behaviour rather than to house large lump sums, and they generally require the saver to hold a current account with the same bank.

**Top regular savers (March 2026):**

- **Nationwide Flex Regular Saver:** Up to 6-6.5% AER on deposits up to £200/month (Nationwide current account required)
- **First Direct Regular Saver:** Up to 7% AER on deposits up to £300/month (First Direct current account required)
- **HSBC Regular Saver:** 5-6% AER on deposits up to £250/month (HSBC current account required)

The key limitation is the monthly deposit cap, which constrains the total interest earned regardless of the headline rate. At £200 per month at 6.5% AER, the maximum additional interest over 12 months is approximately £88 — a high headline rate but a modest absolute benefit for most savers. Regular savers work best as a complement to, not a replacement for, a primary easy-access or ISA account where the bulk of savings can earn competitive returns on larger balances.

## How to Choose the Right UK Savings Account

<AnswerBlock question="What is the right savings strategy for your situation?">
Match your savings to your access needs and tax position. Money you may need within a month belongs in easy-access (Marcus, Chip). Money you will not need for 3-12 months earns more in a fixed-term bond via Raisin UK. Higher-rate taxpayers should prioritise filling the £20,000 Cash ISA allowance before using taxable accounts. For balances above £85,000, split across banking groups and use NS&I Premium Bonds for the portion exceeding the FSCS limit. The right structure depends on your tax band, time horizon, and whether you need guaranteed returns or can accept probabilistic prizes.
</AnswerBlock>

Choosing the right savings account requires answering four questions in sequence, and the answers will differ for every saver depending on their balance size, tax position, and spending patterns. The structure below walks through each decision point in the order that matters most for maximising your annual return. British consumers who follow this framework can typically increase their savings income by two to four times compared to leaving funds in a legacy high-street account.

**Step 1 — Access needs:** Money you might need within a month belongs in an easy-access account such as Marcus or Chip. Money you will not need for 3-12 months earns more in a fixed-term bond. Savings earmarked for a specific date (such as a property deposit in 2 years) suit a 2-year fixed bond.

**Step 2 — Tax position:** Basic-rate taxpayers with under £1,000 in annual savings interest can use non-ISA accounts comfortably. Higher-rate taxpayers approaching the £500 Personal Savings Allowance should prioritise a Cash ISA. Additional-rate taxpayers benefit most from NS&I Premium Bonds (tax-free) plus the full £20,000 ISA allowance.

**Step 3 — FSCS limits:** If your savings are under £85,000 with one provider, standard [FSCS](https://www.fscs.org.uk/) protection applies. If your savings exceed £85,000, split across multiple banking groups and use NS&I Premium Bonds for the excess — the government backing has no cap.

**Step 4 — Automation:** Consistent savers are well-served by Marcus or any competitive easy-access account. Inconsistent savers should consider Chip's AutoSave feature, which uses Open Banking to save affordable amounts automatically and can increase saving rates materially.

### Scenario 2: Higher-Rate Taxpayer Maximising Tax Efficiency (£60,000)

A couple earning £95,000 combined with £60,000 in savings faces a different challenge. As higher-rate taxpayers with only a £500 Personal Savings Allowance each, tax efficiency becomes the dominant consideration. The optimal structure: fill both Cash ISA allowances (£20,000 each, £40,000 total) at a top-rate provider; hold the remaining £20,000 in an easy-access account. Inside ISAs at approximately 5% AER, £40,000 earns **£2,000 completely tax-free**. The remaining £20,000 earns approximately £1,000 pre-tax, of which £500 falls within one partner's PSA (tax-free) and £500 is taxed at 40% — netting approximately £800 after tax. Total annual return: **£2,800**, versus approximately £300 in a legacy account paying 0.5%.

### Scenario 3: Retiree with Large Savings Exceeding FSCS Limits (£150,000)

A retired couple with £150,000 in combined savings must prioritise protection alongside returns. Optimal structure: £50,000 in NS&I Premium Bonds (HM Treasury-backed, tax-free prizes at approximately 4.40% equivalent); £85,000 split across two separate banking groups (£42,500 each) in easy-access at approximately 5% AER; remaining £15,000 in a Cash ISA. This structure ensures **full protection** on every pound while earning approximately **£6,700 per year** — compared to approximately £1,500 at a legacy rate. The NS&I allocation provides unlimited government-backed security beyond the [£85,000 FSCS cap](https://www.fscs.org.uk/check/check-your-money-is-protected/).

## UK Savings Protection — FSCS, PSA, and ISA Rules

Understanding the UK's savings protection framework is essential before placing funds with any institution, because the rules operate per banking group rather than per account. [FSCS](https://www.fscs.org.uk/) protection covers £85,000 per depositor per banking group — meaning multiple accounts at the same bank share a single £85,000 limit. Joint accounts are protected up to £170,000 (£85,000 per person). Every institution recommended in this guide is [FCA](https://www.fca.org.uk/)-authorised with FSCS coverage or backed directly by HM Treasury in the case of [NS&I](https://www.nsandi.com/).

The **Personal Savings Allowance** provides tax-free interest of £1,000 for basic-rate taxpayers, £500 for higher-rate taxpayers, and £0 for additional-rate taxpayers. Interest earned above these thresholds is taxable income reported via your Self Assessment or collected through your tax code. This makes Cash ISAs particularly important for higher earners — the £20,000 annual ISA allowance shelters interest permanently from income tax, and unused allowance cannot be carried forward from previous tax years.

For consumers exploring [UK forex platforms](/uk/forex/) or [UK trading accounts](/uk/trading/), savings protection works differently from investment protection — FSCS covers deposits up to £85,000, but investment claims are capped at £85,000 separately under a different compensation scheme. Understanding which regime applies to each product is critical for structuring your finances correctly.
