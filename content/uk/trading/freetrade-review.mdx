## Freetrade: Commission-Free ISA Investing for UK Retail Investors

Freetrade is a London-based investment platform that brought commission-free stock trading to the UK retail market in 2018, several years before incumbents like [Hargreaves Lansdown](/uk/trading/hargreaves-lansdown-review) and AJ Bell adjusted their pricing models. With over 1.4 million UK customers and more than £2 billion in assets under administration, Freetrade has established itself as the leading commission-free investment app for British retail investors seeking a clean, mobile-first experience for stocks, ETFs, and ISA investing. The platform operates under [FCA](https://www.fca.org.uk/) authorisation (FRN [714399](https://register.fca.org.uk/s/firm?id=0014G00002fCKdSQAW)) and focuses exclusively on long-term investing with no CFDs, spread betting, or options products.

The defining development in Freetrade's recent history occurred in 2024 when [IG Group](https://www.iggroup.com/) Holdings plc (FTSE 250, LSE: IGG) acquired the platform. This acquisition resolved the business continuity concerns that had been a legitimate worry during Freetrade's independent fintech years. IG Group brings approximately £3 billion in market capitalisation, decades of UK regulatory experience, and institutional-grade infrastructure to Freetrade's operations. For UK investors evaluating Freetrade today, the IG Group ownership provides a material layer of stability that smaller independent platforms cannot match. The full range of [UK trading platforms](/uk/trading) is worth comparing before committing to any single provider, particularly the direct competitor analysis between Freetrade and Trading 212 that we cover in our fee comparison section below.

## UK Pricing & Plans 2026

Freetrade operates a subscription model with no per-trade commissions on any plan. All three tiers provide commission-free trading on 6,200+ UK and US stocks, ETFs, investment trusts, and REITs. The critical differentiator between tiers is account wrapper access: Basic offers only a General Investment Account, Standard adds an ISA, and Plus unlocks a SIPP pension alongside the ISA. For the vast majority of UK investors, the ISA wrapper is essential for tax-efficient investing, making the Standard plan the practical minimum.

| Plan | Monthly Fee | Annual Cost | ISA | SIPP | Key Features |
|------|------------|-------------|-----|------|-------------|
| **Basic** | £0 | £0 | No | No | General investment account only, 6,200+ assets |
| **Standard** | £4.99 | £59.88 | Yes | No | ISA wrapper, 6,200+ stocks and ETFs |
| **Plus** | £9.99 | £119.88 | Yes | Yes | SIPP pension, advanced orders, priority support |

### Hidden Costs to Watch

The headline pricing is transparent, but several costs deserve attention before you commit. The 0.99% FX conversion fee on non-GBP assets is embedded in the conversion rate rather than displayed as a separate line item, which can mislead investors who assume all trading is genuinely free. Stamp duty of 0.5% applies to all UK share purchases (this is a government tax, not a Freetrade charge, and applies on every UK platform). The Basic plan's lack of ISA access creates a hidden tax cost that many new investors underestimate.

### Break-Even Analysis: Standard ISA vs No ISA

The Standard plan costs £59.88/year. For UK investors using the £20,000 ISA allowance, the tax savings rapidly outweigh the subscription cost. Capital gains above the £3,000 annual CGT allowance (2025/26) are taxed at 10-20%, and dividends above £500 are taxed at 8.75-39.35%. An investor achieving modest 7% annual returns on a £10,000 portfolio generates approximately £700 in gains — the ISA saves £70-£140 in tax annually, comfortably exceeding the £59.88 subscription cost.

## Key Features for UK Investors

### 1. Commission-Free Trading on 6,200+ Assets

Freetrade offers zero-commission trading on UK and US stocks, ETFs, investment trusts, and REITs. For British investors building a diversified long-term portfolio combining FTSE 100 shares, S&P 500 trackers, and global index ETFs, this eliminates the per-trade cost that traditionally made small regular investing prohibitively expensive on platforms like Hargreaves Lansdown. A UK investor making 12 purchases per year on HL (£11.95/trade for fewer than 10 trades/month) would pay £143.40 in commissions; the same activity on Freetrade Standard costs £59.88 total for the annual subscription — a saving of £83.52 in the first year alone, with the advantage compounding as portfolio values grow relative to percentage-based platform fees.

### 2. Stocks and Shares ISA

Available on Standard (£4.99/month) and Plus (£9.99/month) tiers, the ISA shelters capital gains and dividend income from UK tax within the £20,000 annual allowance. This is the core account type for most UK retail investors on Freetrade. The ISA enables tax-free growth on index ETFs from providers like [Vanguard](https://www.vanguard.co.uk/), iShares, and HSBC, as well as individual UK and US shares. For investors contributing regularly via direct debit, the ISA combined with recurring investments creates an efficient pound-cost averaging strategy that compounds tax-free over decades.

### 3. Self-Invested Personal Pension (SIPP)

The Plus tier (£9.99/month) adds a SIPP, allowing UK investors to contribute to a pension with income tax relief at their marginal rate. Contributions up to £60,000/year receive 20% basic-rate relief automatically; higher and additional-rate taxpayers claim the additional relief via [HMRC self-assessment](https://www.gov.uk/self-assessment-tax-returns). The Freetrade SIPP invests in the same 6,200+ assets as the ISA, and notably, Trading 212 does not currently offer a SIPP — making Freetrade the only commission-free UK platform with both ISA and pension in one app.

### 4. Fractional Shares from £2

Invest in major US stocks from £2, including Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, and Tesla, without purchasing a full share. With some US stocks trading above £300 per share, fractional investing enables UK investors to build diversified positions across multiple companies without needing thousands of pounds in capital upfront. This feature is particularly valuable for investors using recurring investments to build positions gradually over time.

### 5. Recurring Investments from £1

Automatic monthly investing from £1/month into specific stocks or ETFs removes the behavioural friction of manual investing. UK investors can set up standing orders into Vanguard FTSE All World ETF, individual UK stocks, or any asset on the platform, and Freetrade executes the purchase automatically each month. This is the most effective tool for pound-cost averaging in a long-term ISA, and it eliminates the temptation to time the market.

As an [FCA](https://www.fca.org.uk/)-authorised investment firm, eligible client assets at Freetrade are protected by the [Financial Services Compensation Scheme (FSCS)](https://www.fscs.org.uk/) up to £85,000 per eligible claimant in the event of Freetrade's failure. This is the standard UK investment firm protection — it covers firm insolvency, not investment losses. Client investments are held in a nominee account structure, segregated from Freetrade's corporate assets under FCA CASS rules. In the event of Freetrade ceasing operations, your investments remain your property and would be transferred to another provider.

## How Freetrade Makes Money

Understanding Freetrade's revenue model helps UK investors anticipate where costs may evolve and where the platform's incentives align with yours. Freetrade's commission-free model means the company must generate revenue through channels other than per-trade fees, and understanding these channels is important for any investor evaluating the platform long-term.

Freetrade generates revenue through **five primary channels**:

1. **Subscription fees** — The Standard (£4.99/month) and Plus (£9.99/month) tiers generate predictable recurring revenue from millions of subscribers. This is the primary revenue stream and is well-aligned with customer value — Freetrade earns more by attracting and retaining long-term investors, not by encouraging excessive trading.
2. **FX conversion fees** — The 0.99% charge on non-GBP asset purchases generates meaningful revenue from UK investors buying US stocks. This is the most criticised revenue source and the area where Freetrade's pricing is least competitive.
3. **Net interest income** — Freetrade earns interest on client cash balances held before investment and on securities lending. As a subsidiary of IG Group, this revenue stream benefits from institutional treasury management capabilities.
4. **Order flow and execution** — While Freetrade does not use the US-style payment for order flow model, the platform earns revenue through best execution arrangements with market makers and liquidity providers.
5. **Premium features** — Plus tier features like the SIPP, advanced order types, and priority support generate incremental subscription revenue from power users who need pension access or faster customer service.
