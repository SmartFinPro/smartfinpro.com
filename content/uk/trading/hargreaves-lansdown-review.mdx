## FCA Regulation & FSCS Protection

Hargreaves Lansdown is fully authorised and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority, providing UK investors with comprehensive regulatory protections that underpin every account and every transaction on the platform.

As a FTSE 100-listed company, Hargreaves Lansdown is subject to rigorous ongoing scrutiny from both the FCA and the broader financial markets. Unlike privately held challengers, HL publishes quarterly trading updates, annual reports, and detailed regulatory disclosures that provide full transparency over its financial health. The company reported revenues of over £750 million for its most recent financial year, with a client base that has grown continuously for over four decades.

### FSCS Protection Explained

The Financial Services Compensation Scheme (FSCS) protects UK investors in the event of a regulated firm becoming insolvent. For investments held through Hargreaves Lansdown, FSCS protection covers up to £85,000 per eligible person. This protection is relevant specifically in the unlikely scenario that HL itself becomes insolvent — it does not protect against normal investment losses caused by market movements.

In practice, HL holds all client assets in a nominee account that is completely segregated from HL's own company assets. This means that even in the event of HL's failure, your investments would not be used to pay creditors and would be returned to you or transferred to another provider. The FSCS £85,000 limit therefore acts as an additional safety net beyond the segregation already in place.

<Tip>
The FSCS £85,000 protection limit applies per person, per firm — not per account. If you hold an ISA, SIPP, and Fund & Share Account with HL, the combined protection across all three accounts is £85,000. Investors with very large portfolios sometimes spread assets across two FCA-authorised providers to maximise their FSCS coverage.
</Tip>

## Fees & Costs

Understanding Hargreaves Lansdown's fee structure is essential before committing to the platform. HL operates a percentage-based model for funds (with tiered reductions for larger portfolios) and a flat per-trade charge for shares, which has important implications depending on your investment style and portfolio size.

### Share Dealing Fees

| Monthly Trades | Fee per Trade |
|---------------|---------------|
| 0–9 trades | £11.95 |
| 10–19 trades | £8.95 |
| 20+ trades | £5.95 |

At £11.95 per trade for occasional investors, HL's share dealing charge is substantially higher than newer digital platforms. Investors who trade shares frequently — more than ten times per month — will find the cost becomes more manageable at £8.95, and active traders executing 20 or more trades monthly pay just £5.95 per deal. However, for most long-term investors who buy and hold funds rather than trading individual shares, the share dealing fee is rarely the primary cost consideration.

## Account Types — ISA, SIPP & More

One of HL's greatest strengths is the breadth and quality of tax-efficient investment wrappers available on a single platform. Most UK investors will find every account type they need — from their primary ISA through to pension drawdown — without needing to use a second provider.

### Stocks & Shares ISA

The Stocks & Shares ISA is the cornerstone product for most HL clients. UK adults can contribute up to £20,000 per tax year (running from 6 April to 5 April), and all investment gains and income generated within the ISA wrapper are completely exempt from Capital Gains Tax and Income Tax — permanently. There is no time limit on how long you can hold an ISA, and proceeds can be withdrawn at any time without tax consequence.

HL's ISA provides access to the full range of over 3,000 funds, 2,000+ UK shares, 500+ US and international shares, 400+ ETFs, and 400+ investment trusts — all within a single tax-efficient account. There is no additional account fee beyond the standard platform fee, and ISA transfers from other providers are accepted free of charge without using your current year's allowance.

<CollapsibleSection title="ISA Key Facts" count={7}>

- **Annual allowance:** £20,000 per tax year (2025/26)
- **Tax treatment:** No Capital Gains Tax, no Income Tax on dividends or interest
- **Withdrawals:** Unrestricted — withdraw funds at any time without penalty
- **Transfer:** Transfer existing ISAs from other providers free of charge, in specie or in cash
- **Flexible ISA:** HL's ISA is a Flexible ISA — money withdrawn and replaced in the same tax year does not count against your annual allowance
- **Inheritance:** ISAs can be passed to a spouse or civil partner via an Additional Permitted Subscription on death
- **Junior ISA:** Separate £9,000 annual allowance for children under 18

</CollapsibleSection>

### Self-Invested Personal Pension (SIPP)

HL's SIPP is widely regarded as the best pension platform available to UK retail investors, combining the widest investment choice with excellent retirement planning tools and flexible drawdown options. Contributions to a SIPP attract basic-rate tax relief automatically at 20%, claimed from HMRC and added to your pension — meaning a £800 net contribution effectively becomes a £1,000 pension contribution. Higher and additional rate taxpayers can claim further relief through their Self Assessment tax return.

The SIPP platform fee is capped at £200 per year across all investments held within the pension, making it highly competitive for larger pension portfolios. A £500,000 pension pot would pay just £200 per year in platform fees with HL — an effective rate of 0.04%. The SIPP supports income drawdown, allowing investors to flexibly withdraw pension income from age 55 (rising to 57 from 2028) without purchasing an annuity.

<CollapsibleSection title="SIPP Tax Relief & Allowances" count={6}>

- **Basic rate relief:** 20% added automatically — £800 net becomes £1,000 in pension
- **Higher rate relief:** 40% taxpayers claim additional 20% via Self Assessment
- **Additional rate relief:** 45% taxpayers claim additional 25% via Self Assessment
- **Annual allowance:** Up to £60,000 per year (or 100% of earnings if lower) for 2025/26
- **Money Purchase Annual Allowance:** Reduces to £10,000 once you begin drawing flexible income
- **Platform fee cap:** £200 per year across all SIPP investments, regardless of portfolio size

</CollapsibleSection>

The LISA is best suited to first-time buyers or younger investors supplementing pension savings.

## Who Should Use Hargreaves Lansdown?

The question of whether Hargreaves Lansdown is the right platform for you is fundamentally a question of what you value in an investment provider. For investors whose primary concern is minimising costs, HL is not the optimal choice — platforms like Trading 212, Vanguard UK, or Interactive Investor (for larger portfolios) will consistently deliver lower total annual fees. For investors who place significant value on research quality, expert guidance, comprehensive account types, and the reassurance of dealing with the UK's most established platform, HL justifies the premium.

Long-term fund investors — particularly those building a diversified portfolio across the Wealth Shortlist — represent HL's core and best-served customer. The research benefit from the Wealth Shortlist, the breadth of the fund range, and the availability of knowledgeable support staff collectively deliver value that is difficult to quantify precisely but is consistently cited by HL's 1.8 million clients as the primary reason for staying on the platform despite cheaper alternatives.

For investors whose primary objective is cost minimisation, it is not. Both positions are defensible — which is why HL retains 1.8 million clients despite consistently higher prices.
