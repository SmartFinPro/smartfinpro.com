## CopyTrader & Social Trading

### How CopyTrader Works

CopyTrader is eToro's flagship feature and the primary reason millions of investors choose the platform over traditional brokers. It allows you to browse top-performing traders, analyse their full track records across multiple time periods, and copy their every move automatically. When the copied trader opens a position, the same trade is mirrored proportionally in your account. When they close a position, yours closes too.

The minimum allocation to copy a single trader is $200, and you can copy up to 100 traders simultaneously. Each copied position includes a customisable Copy Stop Loss that defaults to 40% of the allocated amount — meaning if your copy portfolio drops by 40%, the copy relationship automatically closes to limit further losses. You can adjust this threshold, pause copying, or close individual positions at any time without affecting the rest of your copy portfolio.

| Minimum copy amount | $200 |
| Maximum traders to copy | 100 |
| Copy Stop Loss | Customisable (default 40%) |

### Popular Investor Programme

eToro rewards its best traders through the Popular Investor Programme, which creates a transparent incentive structure. Successful traders earn up to 1.5% annual management fees based on their assets under copy, encouraging them to maintain consistent performance and share their strategies openly. The programme has four tiers based on the amount of capital being copied, starting from Cadet at $500 and reaching Elite Pro at $5 million or more.

### Smart Portfolios

For investors who prefer a hands-off, diversified approach, Smart Portfolios bundle assets around a theme or strategy. The minimum investment is $500 per Smart Portfolio, and eToro rebalances these portfolios periodically to maintain the target allocation.

### Real Stocks vs CFDs

When you buy stocks on eToro without leverage, you are purchasing the real underlying asset. You own the share, receive dividends, hold voting rights, and pay zero commission. This is a critical distinction because leveraged stock positions are executed as CFDs, which carry spreads, overnight financing charges, and no ownership rights. Always verify your leverage setting before placing a trade — the default for stocks is 1x (real ownership), but accidentally selecting 2x or 5x converts the trade into a CFD.
