### Why FCA Regulation Matters

All platforms in our top 5 are authorised and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority:

| Protection | Coverage |
|------------|----------|
| **FSCS** | Up to £85,000 per firm |
| **Client money rules** | Funds held separately |
| **Best execution** | Must get you the best price |
| **Clear pricing** | No hidden fees allowed |

### FSCS Protection Explained

The Financial Services Compensation Scheme protects your investments:

- **Coverage:** Up to £85,000 per person, per firm
- **Applies to:** Platform failure, fraud, negligence
- **Does NOT cover:** Investment losses from market movements

<Warning>
**Important:** FSCS protection is per firm, not per account. If you have multiple accounts with the same provider, total protection is still £85,000.
</Warning>

### Verifying FCA Registration

Always verify your broker is FCA registered:
1. Visit the FCA Register: register.fca.org.uk
2. Search by firm name or number
3. Check "Current Status: Authorised"
4. Verify permitted activities include investment dealing

## UK Trading Platform Regulation 2026

All platforms recommended in this guide hold FCA authorisation. Key UK protections:

- **FSCS**: Eligible client investments at FCA-authorised investment firms are FSCS-protected up to £85,000 per claimant in the event of firm failure (not investment losses)
- **CASS rules**: FCA Client Assets Sourcebook requires client money and investments held separately from firm assets
- **ISA tax wrapper**: Capital gains and dividend income within a Stocks and Shares ISA are permanently tax-free; £20,000 annual allowance applies
- **CGT considerations**: Outside an ISA, capital gains above £3,000/year (2025/26 allowance) are taxable; dividend income above £500/year is taxable
- **Spread betting/CFDs**: 75–82% of retail CFD accounts lose money — FCA-mandated disclosures apply to all providers

Trading 212 is the best free option for UK beginners — no monthly fee, no commissions, a simple ISA wrapper, fractional shares from £1, and an intuitive interface. Freetrade Standard (£4.99/month) adds IG Group institutional backing and a SIPP option. Both are FCA-regulated and FSCS-protected.

### Q2: Is a Stocks and Shares ISA worth it in the UK?
Yes, for virtually all UK investors with money they won't need for 5+ years. A Stocks and Shares ISA shelters capital gains and dividend income from UK tax permanently — worth £0 to £4,000+/year depending on your portfolio size, tax band, and investment returns. The £20,000 annual allowance resets every April — unused allowances cannot be carried forward.

### Q3: What is the cheapest UK investment platform?
Vanguard Investor (0.15% platform fee, max £375/year) is the cheapest for fund investors. Trading 212 is the cheapest for commission-free stock and ETF ISA investors (£0 total platform fee). ### Q4: What percentage of UK retail traders lose money?
FCA-mandated disclosures from UK CFD brokers show that 65–82% of retail CFD accounts lose money — the variation reflects different trader populations and market conditions. This applies specifically to CFD and spread betting trading, not to long-term ISA investing in diversified funds and shares. UK investors should be clear about whether they are investing (buying assets with intent to hold long-term) or trading (short-term speculation with leverage).

### Q5: Can I have multiple investment ISAs in the UK?
You can only contribute to one Stocks and Shares ISA with one provider per tax year (though you can hold ISAs from previous years with different providers). You can hold a Cash ISA and a Stocks and Shares ISA in the same tax year, but your combined contributions across both cannot exceed £20,000 in a single tax year.

### Q6: Is Hargreaves Lansdown or Vanguard better for a UK ISA?
Vanguard is significantly cheaper for pure index fund investing: approximately £135/year versus £225/year on a £50,000 fund portfolio. However, Vanguard only offers Vanguard's own funds — no individual shares, investment trusts, or other fund providers. Hargreaves Lansdown offers 13,000+ investments, research tools, and customer service. Choose Vanguard for pure low-cost passive investing; choose HL for full-service investing with individual stock selection capability.
