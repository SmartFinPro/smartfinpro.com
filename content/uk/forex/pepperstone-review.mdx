## How Pepperstone Makes Money

Understanding a broker's revenue model helps assess whether their incentives align with yours as a trader.

**Pepperstone earns revenue through three primary channels:**

1. **Commissions** — Razor account charges $3.50/lot/side ($7 round-trip) on every trade. This is the transparent, disclosed cost and represents the primary revenue source from active traders.

2. **Spread markup** — Standard account incorporates Pepperstone's margin into wider spreads (~1.0 pip on EUR/USD versus 0.0 pips raw). No separate commission is charged.

3. **Overnight financing (swaps)** — Positions held past the daily rollover incur swap charges based on interbank interest rate differentials. These are variable and disclosed in real-time within each platform.

**Pepperstone operates an agency/STP model** — client orders are routed to external liquidity providers rather than being warehoused internally. This means Pepperstone does not profit when clients lose money, unlike some B-book (market-maker) brokers. This alignment of interests is an important distinction for UK traders evaluating broker trustworthiness.
