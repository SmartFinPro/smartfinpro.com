## UK Pricing 2026

IG primarily earns revenue through spreads — the difference between buy and sell prices — rather than flat commissions. The exception is share CFDs and physical shares, where a commission model applies. This spread-based model means UK traders pay no separate trading fees on forex, indices, or commodities.

### Forex & CFD Spreads

| Instrument | Typical Spread | Commission |
|---|---|---|
| EUR/USD | 0.6 pips | None |
| GBP/USD | 0.9 pips | None |
| UK 100 (FTSE 100) | 1 point | None |
| Germany 40 (DAX) | 1 point | None |
| Brent Crude Oil | 0.3 points | None |
| Gold | 0.3 points | None |
| UK Shares CFD | — | 0.1% (min. £10) |
| US Shares CFD | — | 2¢/share (min. $15) |
| Physical Shares | — | £8 (or £3 for frequent traders) |

**Spread betting** uses identical spread pricing to CFDs. Profits from spread betting are exempt from UK capital gains tax and stamp duty — a meaningful advantage versus share trading for UK tax residents. Losses on spread bets cannot be offset against other capital gains.

### Hidden Costs to Watch

IG's headline pricing is transparent, but there are costs that newer UK traders may not anticipate. Overnight funding charges apply to positions held past the daily settlement time, which can accumulate significantly on leveraged positions held for weeks or months. Guaranteed stop-loss orders (GSLOs) carry a premium charge above the standard spread. The inactivity fee is the most commonly cited hidden cost among UK users.

| Fee | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Overnight funding** | Variable | Applies to leveraged positions held overnight |
| **GSLO premium** | Variable | Charged only if guaranteed stop-loss triggers |
| **Inactivity fee** | £12/month | After 24 months with no trading activity |
| **Currency conversion** | 0.5% | On non-GBP denominated share trades |
| **ISA annual fee** | £0 | No custody charge on IG ISA |
| **Smart Portfolio fee** | 0.19% avg | BlackRock ETF TER; no IG dealing commission |

**Client fund protection** is the most important distinction between FCA-regulated brokers and offshore alternatives. IG holds client money in segregated accounts at major UK banks, completely separate from company operating funds, in compliance with FCA Client Assets Sourcebook (CASS) rules. In the event of IG's failure, eligible clients are protected up to £85,000 through the [Financial Services Compensation Scheme](https://www.fscs.org.uk/). This protection does not cover trading losses — it covers the scenario where the broker itself becomes insolvent.

**Risk warning compliance:** FCA rules require UK CFD providers to prominently display the percentage of retail accounts that lose money. IG's current disclosure: 74% of retail investor accounts lose money when trading CFDs with IG. This is a legal requirement under [FCA PS19/18](https://www.fca.org.uk/publications/policy-statements/ps19-18-restricting-contract-difference-products-sold-retail-clients), not a marketing caveat.

## How IG Makes Money

Understanding IG's revenue model helps UK traders anticipate where costs may shift and where the platform's incentives align with theirs. IG generates revenue through **four primary channels**:

1. **Spread revenue** — the primary source. IG earns the difference between buy and sell prices on forex, indices, commodities, and other spread-based instruments. This is the cost UK traders pay on every trade, embedded in the quoted spread.

2. **Commission revenue** — earned on share CFDs (0.1% min. £10), physical shares (£8 or £3 for frequent traders), and institutional DMA trading via L2 Dealer.

4. **Platform fees and subscriptions** — Smart Portfolio management fees (passed through as ETF TERs), ProRealTime charting subscription (free for active traders, £30/month otherwise), and other platform charges.
