These protections set the UK apart from offshore jurisdictions where leverage can reach 500:1 but client recourse in the event of broker insolvency is effectively zero. ## How to Choose a UK Forex Broker in 2026

### Step 1: Verify FCA Authorisation

Always check any UK forex broker on the [FCA Register](https://register.fca.org.uk/) using the broker's firm reference number. Authorisation as an investment firm means full FSCS eligibility and mandatory CASS client money protections. Being "registered" with the FCA is different from being "authorised" — only authorised firms carry these protections. The [FCA Warning List](https://www.fca.org.uk/scamsmart/warning-list) also flags unauthorised firms targeting UK consumers.

### Step 2: Calculate All-In Trading Costs

Compare total trading cost, not just the headline spread. For raw or ECN accounts, add the round-trip commission to the spread. A 0.0-pip spread plus £7 round-trip commission equals approximately 0.64 pips equivalent at EUR/USD 1.10 — similar to a zero-commission 0.6-pip spread but different depending on volume.

### Step 3: Match Platform to Your Strategy

MT4 and MT5 are best for algorithmic trading and the MetaTrader ecosystem. cTrader is preferred by many professional manual traders for its depth-of-market display and execution quality. Proprietary platforms like IG and CMC Next Generation are typically better for beginners and discretionary traders who value research and charting integration. TradingView integration is increasingly available at Pepperstone and IG for traders who prefer browser-based charting.

### Step 4: Consider UK Tax Treatment

Spread betting is tax-free for UK residents; CFD trading is subject to capital gains tax at 18% or 24% depending on your income band. For UK higher-rate taxpayers making consistent profits, the tax difference is material. If you trade UK instruments primarily and hold overnight positions, spread betting is typically more tax-efficient. See [HMRC guidance on spread betting](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim22015) for current rules.
