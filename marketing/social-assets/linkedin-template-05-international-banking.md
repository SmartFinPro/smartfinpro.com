## Post Version A: The "Expensive Lesson" Format

```
I lost $4,200 to currency conversion in my first year of business.

Here's how I fixed it.

---

Context: US-based agency with clients in UK, EU, and Australia.

Old setup:
• Chase business account
• Invoiced in USD
• Clients paid in their currency
• Chase converted it

The hidden cost:

Chase's FX markup: 2.5-3%
→ $140,000 in international revenue
→ $3,500-4,200 lost to conversion

I didn't even know this was happening.

---

The fix (took 30 minutes):

1. Opened Wise Business account
2. Created local receiving accounts in GBP, EUR, AUD
3. Invoiced clients in THEIR currency
4. Converted only when I needed USD

New FX cost: ~0.5%
Annual savings: $2,800+

---

The bigger insight:

Most "business banking" advice assumes:
• You're in one country
• Your clients are in one country
• You pay suppliers in one currency

That's not how modern business works.

International-first banking:

✅ Multi-currency accounts
✅ Local receiving details (IBAN, sort code, BSB)
✅ Transparent FX rates
✅ No hidden "conversion fees"

---

The decision framework I use now:

Domestic-only business → Traditional fintech (Mercury, Relay)
International clients → Add Wise or multi-currency option
International team → Wise mandatory for payroll savings
```

## Post Version B: The "Framework" Format

```
Paying international contractors?

You're probably losing 3-5% on every payment.

Here's the math nobody shows you:

---

Traditional bank wire transfer:

Wire fee: $25-45
FX markup: 2-3%
Intermediary bank fee: $15-25
Receiving bank fee: $10-20

On a $5,000 contractor payment:
→ Total cost: $175-250
→ That's 3.5-5% gone

Multiply by 12 months.
Multiply by multiple contractors.

I've seen agencies lose $15K+ annually on this.

---

The alternative stack:

Option 1: Wise Business
• Fee: ~1% total
• $5,000 payment costs ~$50
• Contractor receives exact amount

Option 2: Mercury (for US-based)
• Free domestic transfers
• $5 international wires
• Okay FX rates (not best, not worst)

Option 3: Payoneer (contractor-initiated)

Monthly international payments > $10K

Monthly international payments < $10K

Paying contractors in their local currency
```

## Post Version C: The "Myth Busting" Format

```
"Just use PayPal for international payments"

This advice cost my client $8,000 last year.

Let me explain.

---

PayPal's actual costs (business account):

Transaction fee: 2.9% + $0.30
Currency conversion: 3-4% markup
Withdrawal fee: varies by method

Real example:

£10,000 payment from UK client
PayPal receives: £10,000
After conversion + fees: ~$12,100
Should have been: ~$12,600

$500 lost on ONE payment.

---

The "PayPal is easy" trap:

Yes, everyone has PayPal.
Yes, setup is instant.
Yes, it "works."

But "works" and "optimal" aren't the same.

---

Better alternatives by use case:

Receiving payments from international clients:
→ Wise (local account details in 10+ currencies)
→ Mercury (international wire receiving)

Paying international contractors:
→ Wise (best FX rates)
→ Deel or Remote (if you need compliance help)

Holding multiple currencies:
→ Wise (10+ currencies, interest on balances)
→ Revolut Business (similar, different fee structure)

---

The decision tree:

1. Do you receive international payments? → Need local receiving accounts
2. Do you pay international contractors? → Need good FX rates
3. Do you operate in multiple currencies? → Need multi-currency holding

Most businesses need at least one dedicated international tool.

PayPal as a backup? Sure.
PayPal as your primary? Expensive mistake.
```
