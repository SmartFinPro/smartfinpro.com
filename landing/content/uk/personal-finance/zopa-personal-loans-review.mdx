## FCA Regulation & Consumer Protection

Zopa is fully authorised and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA), providing comprehensive consumer protections for UK borrowers.

### Regulatory Status

| Regulatory Detail | Information |
|-------------------|-------------|
| FCA Reference | 800542 |
| Firm Status | Authorised and regulated |
| Permission Type | Consumer credit lending |
| Bank Licence | Full UK banking licence (2020) |
| FSCS Membership | Yes (for deposit products) |
| Financial Ombudsman | Complaints eligible |

### What FCA Regulation Means for You

As an FCA-authorised lender, Zopa must comply with:

- **Affordability assessments:** Zopa must verify you can comfortably afford repayments before approving your loan
- **Transparent pricing:** The representative APR must be offered to at least 51% of successful applicants
- **Fair treatment:** FCA Treating Customers Fairly (TCF) principles apply to all interactions
- **Complaints handling:** You have the right to escalate complaints to the Financial Ombudsman Service free of charge
- **Cooling-off period:** 14 days to cancel your loan agreement without penalty under the Consumer Credit Act 1974

<Info>
**Consumer Credit Act protection:** All Zopa personal loans are regulated credit agreements under the Consumer Credit Act 1974. This gives you statutory rights including a 14-day cooling-off period and the right to repay early at any time.
</Info>

## Eligibility Requirements

### Basic Requirements

To apply for a Zopa personal loan, you must meet the following criteria:

- **Age:** 20 years or older
- **Residency:** UK resident for at least 3 years
- **Income:** Regular income (minimum not published, but affordability assessed)
- **Bank account:** UK bank account that accepts Direct Debits
- **Electoral roll:** Registered on the electoral roll at your current address
- **Credit history:** No bankruptcies, IVAs, or CCJs in the past 6 years

### Credit Score Guidance

Zopa uses data from all three UK credit reference agencies to assess applications:

### Factors That Improve Your Chances

## Debt Consolidation with Zopa

Zopa personal loans are a popular choice for UK borrowers looking to consolidate existing debts into a single, lower-cost monthly payment.

### When Consolidation Makes Sense

Debt consolidation with Zopa is financially beneficial if:

1. **Your existing debt is at a higher rate** — Credit cards at 19.9%-24.9% APR can be consolidated at Zopa's 7.9% representative APR
2. **You have multiple monthly payments** — Simplify to a single Direct Debit
3. **You commit to not re-borrowing** — Avoid building up new debt on paid-off credit cards

### Consolidation Savings Examples

| Existing Debt | Current APR | Zopa APR | Monthly Saving | Total Interest Saved (5 years) |
|---------------|------------|----------|---------------|-------------------------------|
| £5,000 credit card | 19.9% | 7.9% | £27 | £1,620 |
| £10,000 credit card | 22.9% | 7.9% | £65 | £3,900 |
| £15,000 mixed debts | 18.9% | 7.9% | £73 | £4,380 |
| £25,000 mixed debts | 21.9% | 7.9% | £148 | £8,880 |

<Warning>
**Critical warning:** Debt consolidation only works if you stop using the credit facilities you have paid off. If you consolidate £10,000 in credit card debt but then spend on those cards again, you will end up with more total debt than you started with.
</Warning>

### How to Consolidate with Zopa

1. List all your existing debts, balances, and interest rates
2. Use Zopa's soft search to check your eligible rate
3. Apply for a loan covering your total outstanding balances
4. Use the funds to pay off each existing debt in full
5. Set up your single monthly Zopa Direct Debit
6. Consider closing paid-off credit card accounts to avoid temptation
