National Australia Bank (NAB) is one of Australia's big four banks, established in 1982 through the merger of the National Bank of Australasia and the Commercial Banking Company of Sydney. The NAB Tailored Home Loan is the bank's primary variable rate mortgage product, offering a customisable structure with access to offset accounts, rate lock, and government first home buyer schemes.

## First Home Buyer Support

NAB's first home buyer proposition is the strongest among the big four banks. Here is a complete overview of the support available.

### First Home Guarantee (FHG)

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Scheme name** | First Home Guarantee (formerly FHLDS) |
| **Administered by** | Housing Australia (formerly NHFIC) |
| **Minimum deposit** | 5% (no LMI required) |
| **Places per year** | 35,000 nationally |
| **Income cap (single)** | A$125,000 per annum |
| **Income cap (couple)** | A$200,000 combined per annum |
| **Property price cap** | Varies by location (e.g., A$900,000 in Sydney/Melbourne) |
| **Eligible properties** | Existing dwellings, new builds, off-the-plan, house and land |
| **NAB participation** | Yes — approved participating lender |

### LMI Savings Under the Guarantee

| Property Value | Deposit (5%) | LMI Avoided | Annual Saving |
|---------------|-------------|-------------|--------------|
| **A$500,000** | A$25,000 | ~A$12,000 | N/A (one-off) |
| **A$600,000** | A$30,000 | ~A$16,800 | N/A (one-off) |
| **A$700,000** | A$35,000 | ~A$22,400 | N/A (one-off) |
| **A$800,000** | A$40,000 | ~A$28,000 | N/A (one-off) |
| **A$900,000** | A$45,000 | ~A$33,600 | N/A (one-off) |

*LMI estimates based on 95% LVR. Actual LMI premiums vary by insurer, loan amount, and borrower profile.*

### Additional First Home Buyer Support

NAB also provides access to:

- **Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee** — 5% deposit for regional purchases (10,000 places per year)
- **Family Home Guarantee** — 2% deposit for single parents with dependants
- **First Home Super Saver Scheme** — guidance on accessing voluntary super contributions for deposit
- **State-based stamp duty concessions** — assistance navigating first home buyer stamp duty exemptions

<Info>
**Eligibility check:** First Home Guarantee places are limited and allocated on a first-come, first-served basis each financial year. NAB's home loan bankers can check your eligibility and reserve a place during your pre-approval application. Apply early in the financial year (July) to maximise your chances of securing a place.
</Info>

## NAB Price Match Explained

NAB's Price Match policy allows existing and prospective borrowers to request that NAB match a lower rate offered by a competitor. Here is how it works in practice.

### How to Request a Price Match

1. **Obtain a competitor offer** — secure a formal conditional approval or letter of offer from another lender at a lower rate
2. **Contact NAB** — call 13 22 65 or visit a branch with your competitor offer documentation
3. **Submit evidence** — provide the competitor's written rate offer including the product name, rate, and comparison rate
4. **Wait for assessment** — NAB's pricing team reviews each request individually (typically 2-5 business days)
5. **Receive outcome** — NAB will confirm whether they can match, partially match, or cannot match the rate

### Price Match Considerations

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Guaranteed?** | No — assessed case by case |
| **Success rate** | High for strong borrower profiles with genuine competing offers |
| **Timing** | Best used at application or during annual rate review |
| **Evidence needed** | Formal written offer from a licensed Australian lender |
| **Ongoing?** | Matched rate typically applies for the life of the loan |
| **Which products?** | Variable rate loans under Choice Package |

<Tip>
**Annual rate review strategy:** Even if you did not use Price Match at the start, you can request a rate review annually. Obtain a competing offer from another lender (many provide conditional rates via soft search) and present it to NAB. This simple exercise can save you 0.10-0.25% without the hassle of refinancing.
</Tip>

## Our Verdict

After 6 months of testing, NAB earns **4.4 out of 5 stars** as the best big four choice for first home buyers in Australia.

**Bottom line:** NAB has carved out a clear niche as the most first-home-buyer-friendly among the big four banks. The First Home Guarantee partnership is the headline feature — allowing eligible buyers to purchase with just 5% deposit without the substantial cost of LMI. For a first home buyer purchasing a A$700,000 property, avoiding approximately A$22,400 in LMI is a significant financial benefit that outweighs NAB's less competitive variable rate.

The NAB Price Match policy adds another layer of value, giving borrowers a tool to negotiate a better rate without refinancing. In our testing, NAB matched a competing rate within 3 business days — a straightforward process that saved 0.05% on our test loan.

However, the higher comparison rate (6.47% vs 6.31% for ANZ), inconsistent approval times, and a below-average app experience hold NAB back from a higher rating. Borrowers who do not need first home buyer support or price matching will find better value with ANZ (for offset) or Athena (for the lowest rate).
