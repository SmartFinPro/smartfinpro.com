## What is SoFi?

SoFi (Social Finance) is an online lender and financial services company that has grown to become one of the most popular personal loan providers in the United States. Founded in 2011, SoFi became a nationally chartered bank in 2022, allowing it to offer competitive rates and expanded services.

For personal loans, SoFi stands out due to:
- **Zero fees** across the board
- **Competitive rates** starting at 8.99% APR
- **Unique member benefits** including unemployment protection
- **Fast funding** often the same day

## Application Process

### Step 1: Prequalification (2 minutes)

1. Visit SoFi's website
2. Enter basic information (name, income, loan amount)
3. Receive rate estimate
4. **No impact on credit score** (soft pull only)

### Step 2: Full Application (5-10 minutes)

If you like your rate estimate:
1. Complete full application
2. Provide income documentation
3. Authorize credit check (hard pull)
4. Receive final decision

### Step 3: Verification (1-2 days)

SoFi may request:
- Recent pay stubs (2 months)
- Bank statements
- Tax returns (if self-employed)
- ID verification

### Step 4: Funding (Same day to 3 days)

After approval:
- Sign loan agreement electronically
- Receive funds via direct deposit
- Same-day funding available in many cases

<Info>
SoFi's entire process can be completed online or via their mobile app. No branch visits required.
</Info>

## Who Qualifies for SoFi?

### Minimum Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Credit score | ~680+ (unofficial minimum) |
| Employment | Employed or have job offer |
| Income | Sufficient to cover payments |
| Citizenship | U.S. citizen or permanent resident |
| Age | 18+ (19+ in AL/NE) |

### What SoFi Looks For

Beyond minimum requirements, SoFi evaluates:
- **Debt-to-income ratio:** Prefer under 40%
- **Payment history:** On-time payments matter most
- **Employment stability:** Longer tenure helps
- **Monthly cash flow:** Income minus expenses

### Who May NOT Qualify

- Credit scores below 680
- High debt-to-income ratios (45%+)
- Recent bankruptcies (typically wait 7 years)
- No verifiable income
- Residents of certain states (check SoFi's availability)
