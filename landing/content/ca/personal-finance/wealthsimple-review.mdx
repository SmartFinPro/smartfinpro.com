### TFSA (Tax-Free Savings Account)

The TFSA is the single most powerful wealth-building tool available to Canadian residents, and Wealthsimple's implementation is the best in the country:

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **2026 Contribution Limit** | C$7,000 |
| **Cumulative Room (since 2009)** | Up to C$102,000 (if 18+ since 2009) |
| **Commission** | C$0 on all trades |
| **Available Assets** | Stocks, ETFs, crypto (select), fractional shares |
| **CRA Integration** | Automatic contribution tracking |
| **Withdrawals** | Tax-free, contribution room restored next calendar year |

**Why TFSA-first matters:** Every dollar of growth inside your TFSA is completely tax-free --- no capital gains tax, no dividend tax, no tax on withdrawals. A C$7,000 annual TFSA contribution growing at 7% for 30 years becomes **C$661,226** --- all tax-free. The same growth in a non-registered account would lose approximately C$130,000 to capital gains taxes.

<Info>
**TFSA Strategy Tip:** Max out your TFSA before your RRSP if you earn under C$100,000/year. The tax-free compounding and withdrawal flexibility outweigh the RRSP tax deduction for most Canadians in lower-to-middle tax brackets. Wealthsimple's automatic tracking ensures you never accidentally over-contribute.
</Info>

### RRSP (Registered Retirement Savings Plan)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **2026 Contribution Limit** | 18% of prior-year earned income, max C$32,490 |
| **Tax Deduction** | Contributions reduce taxable income dollar-for-dollar |
| **RRSP Deadline** | March 2, 2026 (for 2025 tax year) |
| **Spousal RRSP** | Supported on Wealthsimple |
| **Home Buyers' Plan** | Withdraw up to C$60,000 for first home (tax-free) |
| **Lifelong Learning Plan** | Withdraw up to C$20,000 for education |

**RRSP deadline urgency:** Contributions made before March 2, 2026 reduce your 2025 taxable income. For a Canadian in the 33% combined marginal bracket contributing C$20,000, that is an immediate **C$6,600 tax refund** --- which should go straight back into your TFSA or RRSP.

**Wealthsimple RRSP advantages over bank brokerages:**
- No commission on rebalancing trades (banks charge C$9.95 each time you rebalance)
- Automatic dividend reinvestment at C$0 cost (DRIP)
- Spousal RRSP support with proper attribution tracking
- Home Buyers' Plan withdrawals processed digitally through the app
- T4RSP tax slips generated automatically each February

<Warning>
**RRSP vs. TFSA decision:** If your marginal tax rate is above 30%, prioritise RRSP contributions for the immediate tax deduction. If your rate is below 30%, prioritise your TFSA first --- the tax-free growth and flexible withdrawals are more valuable. Wealthsimple supports both, so you can split contributions based on your situation.
</Warning>

### Tax-Loss Harvesting (Premium & Generation)

Tax-loss harvesting is one of Wealthsimple's most underrated features. The system automatically:

1. **Identifies positions** trading at a loss in your non-registered account
2. **Sells the losing position** to crystallise the capital loss
3. **Immediately purchases** a similar (but not identical) ETF to maintain market exposure
4. **Applies the loss** against capital gains at tax time

**Real impact:** On a C$500,000 non-registered portfolio, tax-loss harvesting can save C$2,000-5,000 per year in taxes --- more than offsetting the 0.4% management fee. This feature alone makes Premium worthwhile for taxable accounts.

<Info>
Tax-loss harvesting only applies to non-registered (taxable) accounts. Inside a TFSA or RRSP, there is no taxable event, so this feature is not relevant for registered accounts.
</Info>

### Managed vs. Self-Directed: Which Should You Choose?

This is the most important decision on the platform. Here is a straightforward framework:

**Choose Managed Investing if:**
- You do not want to pick individual stocks or ETFs
- You prefer a fully automated, set-and-forget approach
- You value automatic rebalancing and dividend reinvestment
- You want SRI or Halal portfolio options without doing your own research
- You have a non-registered account and want tax-loss harvesting

**Choose Self-Directed if:**
- You are comfortable selecting your own ETFs (even just 1-3 broad market funds)
- You want to save the 0.5% management fee (which compounds significantly over decades)
- You enjoy monitoring and adjusting your portfolio
- You want access to individual stocks, not just ETFs

**The math:** On a C$200,000 portfolio, the 0.5% Core management fee costs C$1,000/year. Over 25 years at 7% growth, that fee difference means approximately **C$68,000 less** in your account compared to self-directed. If you are willing to buy and hold 3-4 ETFs yourself (which takes about 15 minutes per quarter), self-directed is the better value.
