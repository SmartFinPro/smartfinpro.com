## Why Robo-Advisors Are Revolutionizing Canadian Investing

Robo-advisors have democratized professional investment management for Canadians. What once required a $500,000 minimum and 1-2% annual fees is now accessible with as little as $1 and costs as low as 0.40% annually.

These automated investment platforms use algorithms to build and manage diversified portfolios based on your goals, timeline, and risk tolerance. They handle rebalancing, tax-loss harvesting, and dividend reinvestment automatically—tasks that would take hours manually and often go neglected by DIY investors.

For most Canadians, especially those with under $500,000 in investable assets, robo-advisors offer the optimal balance of cost, convenience, and performance. This guide compares Canada's leading platforms to help you choose the right one.

## Robo-Advisor vs Self-Directed: Which Is Right for You?

### Choose Robo-Advisor If:
- You're new to investing (less than 2 years experience)
- You have under $500,000 invested
- You don't want to research ETFs, rebalance, or harvest losses manually
- Your time is worth more than the 0.25-0.50% fee
- You want emotional discipline (automated investing prevents bad timing decisions)

### Choose Self-Directed If:
- You're an experienced investor (5+ years)
- You have $500,000+ (0.50% fee = $2,500/year—worth DIY effort)
- You enjoy researching and managing your portfolio
- You can commit to quarterly rebalancing and annual tax planning
- You want to use advanced strategies (options, sector rotation, individual stocks)

### Hybrid Approach:
Many investors use both:
- **Robo-advisor for TFSA/RRSP:** Set-and-forget core holdings
- **Self-directed for taxable account:** Active tax-loss harvesting and custom strategies

This gives you automation for most assets while maintaining control where it matters most (taxable accounts with tax management).

## Tax-Loss Harvesting: Is It Worth Paying More?

Tax-loss harvesting can save 0.5-1.0% annually in taxable accounts by:
1. Selling positions with losses
2. Buying similar (but not identical) securities to maintain exposure
3. Using losses to offset capital gains

**Example:**
$100,000 taxable account realizes $5,000 in losses via TLH. This offsets $5,000 in capital gains elsewhere. At 26.5% capital gains tax rate, you save $1,325 in taxes—1.33% of your account value.

**Platforms offering TLH:**
- **Wealthsimple Premium:** $100,000 minimum (0.40% fee)
- **CI Direct Investing:** $25,000 minimum (0.60% fee under $100K)

**Is it worth it?**

**At $100,000:**
- Wealthsimple Premium fee: $400
- TLH savings: ~$500-1,000
- **Net benefit: $100-600**

**Verdict:** Yes, TLH is worth it for taxable accounts $100,000+ if the platform offers it. Below that threshold, the fee may exceed the benefit.

**DIY TLH:**
You can manually harvest losses with Questrade self-directed, but it requires:
- Monitoring positions for loss opportunities
- Understanding superficial loss rules (30-day restriction)
- Executing timely trades
- Record-keeping for tax filing

Most investors prefer to pay 0.40% for automated TLH rather than manage it manually.

## Common Robo-Advisor Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Choosing Based on Fees Alone
A 0.25% fee difference is $125 annually on a $50,000 account. If one platform saves you $500 through tax-loss harvesting or prevents a $2,000 emotional selling mistake, the higher fee is worth it.

### 2. Changing Risk Tolerance During Market Downturns
Selling a growth portfolio in a crash and moving to conservative locks in losses. Robo-advisors prevent this by maintaining your chosen allocation automatically.

### 3. Not Maxing Out Lower-Fee Registered Accounts First
If you have both TFSA and taxable accounts, maximize TFSA first—it's tax-free, making the management fee less impactful.

### 4. Ignoring Tax-Loss Harvesting Eligibility
If you have a $150,000 taxable account, you're eligible for Wealthsimple Premium's TLH. The savings (~$750-1,500/year) far exceed the premium fee increase (~$150).

### 5. Not Setting Up Automatic Contributions
Manual contributions lead to inconsistency. Automate bi-weekly contributions to ensure disciplined investing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Are robo-advisors safe?**

A: Yes. All major Canadian robo-advisors are regulated by CIRO and provincial securities commissions. Your investments are protected by CIPF insurance up to $1 million if the firm fails. Funds are held in segregated accounts and can't be used for company operations. The risk is market volatility, not platform safety.

**Q: Can I lose money with a robo-advisor?**

A: Yes, robo-advisors invest in stocks and bonds, which fluctuate with market conditions. You can lose money in market downturns. However, robo-advisors use diversified portfolios to minimize risk and are designed for long-term growth (5+ years). Short-term losses are normal; historically, markets recover and grow over time.

**Q: How much money do I need to start with a robo-advisor?**

A: Wealthsimple has no minimum ($0). Questrade Portfolio IQ, BMO SmartFolio, and CI Direct Investing require $1,000. You can start small and add regularly through automatic contributions.

**Q: Do robo-advisors work for retirement savings?**

A: Absolutely. Robo-advisors are ideal for RRSP and TFSA retirement accounts. They handle rebalancing, dividend reinvestment, and maintain your target allocation automatically—perfect for long-term, hands-off retirement investing.

**Q: What's the difference between a robo-advisor and an ETF?**

A: An ETF is an investment product (like a basket of stocks). A robo-advisor is a service that builds a portfolio of ETFs for you, rebalances it, and manages it automatically. You could buy the same ETFs yourself (DIY), but robo-advisors handle the management for a fee.

**Q: Can I withdraw money from a robo-advisor anytime?**

A: Yes, from non-registered accounts (taxable). Withdrawals from TFSA, RRSP, FHSA have rules: TFSA withdrawals are tax-free and contribution room is restored next year; RRSP withdrawals are taxable (unless using Home Buyers' Plan or Lifelong Learning Plan); FHSA withdrawals must be for a qualifying first home purchase to remain tax-free.

**Q: Do robo-advisors charge trading fees?**

A: No. The management fee (0.25-0.70%) covers all trading, rebalancing, and account administration. There are no additional trading commissions or hidden fees.

**Q: Which robo-advisor has the best performance?**

A: Performance is very similar across platforms (within 0.5% annually) because they all use low-cost index ETFs with similar holdings. The difference is primarily fees, features, and service quality, not returns.

**Q: Can I transfer my existing investments to a robo-advisor?**

A: Yes. You can transfer TFSA, RRSP, RRIF, or taxable accounts from another institution. The robo-advisor will sell your existing holdings (if not compatible with their portfolios) and reinvest in their ETFs. This may trigger capital gains in taxable accounts, so consult a tax professional before transferring.

**Q: Is Wealthsimple better than Questrade?**
