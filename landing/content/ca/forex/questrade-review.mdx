## Canadian Tax Treatment

### Questrade Tax Documentation

As a Canadian-registered dealer, Questrade provides comprehensive tax documents:

- **Consolidated T5008 slips** — covering forex, equity, and ETF transactions
- **RRSP contribution receipts** — for registered account holders
- **TFSA transaction summaries** — for CRA reporting
- **Detailed trade confirmations** — available for download year-round

### Capital Gains vs Business Income for Questrade Forex Traders

| Factor | Capital Gains | Business Income |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Inclusion rate** | 50% of net gains taxable | 100% of net gains taxable |
| **Typical profile** | Occasional forex trades alongside equity investments | Dedicated, frequent forex trading |
| **RRSP/TFSA forex ETFs** | Tax-sheltered (no immediate tax) | N/A (registered accounts are capital gains by default) |
| **Loss deduction** | Against capital gains only | Against all income sources |

### Integrated Tax Advantage

Because Questrade consolidates all your trading activity (forex, equities, ETFs, registered accounts) under one dealer, your year-end tax preparation is simplified:

- **One T5008 slip** covers all non-registered trading activity
- **Capital losses** from equities can offset capital gains from forex (if both are classified as capital)
- **Registered account activity** (RRSP, TFSA) is reported separately with proper tax sheltering

<Warning>
**Do not assume uniform tax treatment.** Even within a single Questrade account, the CRA may classify frequent forex trading as business income while treating your occasional equity trades as capital gains. Consult a CPA familiar with multi-asset trading for proper classification.
</Warning>
